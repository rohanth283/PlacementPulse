My SAP Labs Interview Experience  
Total Rounds: 5 
Date: 18th August 2025 
Hi everyone! I wanted to share my SAP Labs interview journey because when I was 
preparing, I used to read these kinds of experiences and they helped me a lot. So, here’s my 
detailed story — from the online test to the HR round. Hopefully, it gives you some idea of 
what to expect and how to prepare. 😊 
 
🖥️ Round 1: Online Assessment (60 mins) 
The very first hurdle was the online coding test, which was conducted in a virtual mode at 
CUIC. We had to solve two coding questions in one hour. 
🔹 Question 1: Bank Transfers Simulation 
We were given: 
 
A string R and an array V. 
 
If R[i] = 'A', transfer V[i] amount from Bank A to Bank B. 
 
If R[i] = 'B', transfer V[i] amount from Bank B to Bank A. 
The task was to find the final balance of both banks after all transactions. 
Constraints were huge (N up to 100,000), so efficiency was key. 
🔹 Question 2: Balancing the Board 
We had two strings (row1, row2) representing a board: 
 
Cells contained 'R', 'W', or '?'. 
 
'?' could be replaced with 'R' or 'W'. 
We had to make the board “balanced” with the minimum replacements. If impossible, 
return "not possible", else return the count of replacements. 
This round tested both logic and optimization skills. After completing it, I felt quite 
confident and fortunately, I cleared it. 
 
 
 
 
⚙️ Round 2: Technical Interview – 1 
From hundreds of students, 90 were shortlisted. 
This round started casually with: 
 
Self-introduction 
 
A few questions about my internship experience 
After this warm-up, we quickly moved to problem-solving. 
💻 SQL Questions I faced: 
1. From the Employees table, find the top-most manager of a given employee. 
2. From Orders and Restaurants tables, find the Top 5 restaurants with the 
maximum orders in the last 7 days. 
This round was a mix of SQL + clarity of thought. I cleared it and was super excited to move 
forward. 
 
⚡ Round 3: Technical Interview – 2 
Now things got serious. Out of 90, only 30 candidates moved ahead, and this round was the 
most grilling one. 
I was interviewed by two experienced professionals who really tested me. It started with 
another introduction (yes, again 😅), and then they literally went line by line through my 
resume. 
Here’s what happened: 
 
I had to explain my projects in detail — not just what I built, but also how I built 
them and why I chose certain approaches. 
 
Then came the conceptual questions: 
o Difference between a process and a task. 
o Internal working of compiler vs interpreter. 
 
One coding task: Implement a basic sorting algorithm on the spot. 
 
Some deep Operating Systems concepts: mutex, critical section, producer-consumer 
problem — and I had to explain them with real-life examples (for example, producer-
consumer like “chef cooking food and waiter serving customers”). 
This round went on for almost 1 hour 30 minutes. Honestly, it was tough, but I stayed calm 
and answered with examples whenever possible. Thankfully, I got through! 🙌 
 
🧑💼 Round 4: Managerial Round 
Now only 15 candidates were left. This round was more about my personality and 
behavior rather than coding. 
The panel was quite friendly (and luckily, one of them had already interviewed me in a 
previous round, so I felt comfortable). 
They asked: 
 
Why do you want to join SAP? 
 
Where do you see yourself in 5 years? 
 
What are your strengths and weaknesses? 
 
How are you working on improving your weaknesses? 
This was more like a conversation than an interview. It went on for about 40 minutes and I 
came out smiling. 
 
👥 Round 5: HR Round 
Finally, 13 candidates were shortlisted for this stage. 
This was the most relaxed and informal round. It started with some storytelling questions 
like: 
 
How did I choose MIT? 
 
My college journey so far. 
After that, the HR explained the compensation details, the internship structure, and the job 
location. I also got a chance to ask my own questions about the role and internship. 
It ended smoothly, and after about 15 minutes of waiting… the results were out! 
🎉 Out of 13, 10 were selected. I was thrilled to be the 4th person on the list. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
💡 Suggestions 
 
Resume is everything – Every line will be questioned. Be honest and well-
prepared. Don’t put something unless you are ready to defend it. 
 
Simple questions matter – They’re not checking if you know “rocket science”. They 
want to see if you understand basics and can explain them clearly. 
 
Use real-life examples – Whenever possible, connect your answers to real-world 
scenarios. It shows you really understand the concept. 
 
Communication is key – Speak confidently but don’t bluff. If you don’t know, it’s 
okay to admit and move on.
