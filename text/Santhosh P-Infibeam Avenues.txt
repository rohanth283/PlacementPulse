digital economy.

Interview (Offline and Virtual mixed at CUIC): 
All three rounds are technical. [No HR or managerial rounds] 
• Round 1 - Offline: 
The interview began with a self-introduction, followed by two Data Structures and 
Algorithms (DSA) problems and a puzzle. 
➢ Problem 1: A modified linked-list problem where each node had both a child 
and a next pointer, resembling a tree structure. The task was to flatten this 
structure into a linear linked list by traversing through child nodes first, then 
next nodes. I initially solved it using a stack-based iterative DFS approach. 
Upon being asked to optimize space complexity, I proposed a recursive DFS 
solution. The interviewer later requested pseudocode, which I couldn’t provide 
at that moment. 
➢ Problem 2: Given a binary tree, the objective was to find the sum of the 
maximum possible Binary Search Tree (BST) within it. Each leaf node was 
considered a BST, and the goal was to compute the highest sum across all 
possible BSTs. Only the logic was required, and the interviewer provided 
guidance during the discussion. 
➢ Puzzle: Sand Timer Puzzle with solution | PrepInsta. I was unable to solve it 
during the interview.
