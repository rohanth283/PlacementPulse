About the Company:  
Tekion, founded in 2016 by ex-Tesla CIO Jay Vijayan, is a rapidly growing SaaS company

Read this First: 
Tekion mainly focuses on your problem-solving skills and willingness to learn new things. 
If you don’t know something, they don’t expect you to be fully updated; instead, they 
want to see whether you are trainable or not. Their evaluation is centered around DSA 
problems, core CS fundamentals, and the creative projects you have mentioned in your 
resume. They are not much concerned about which new technologies, frameworks, or 
languages you have used in your projects — though having them is, of course, a plus. 
The company mainly focuses on candidates from the CS branch, though it also hires 
from IT. Being a CSE student definitely gives you an advantage. (14 people were selected 
in total)

Online Test: 
1.The problem requires finding integer coefficients a and b for each query z from a 
queries array, such that ax + by = z, where x and y are elements from a given arr of 
integers. Additionally, the sum a + b must be less than a given integer m. 
 If such a and b exist and satisfy the condition, the value a + b is added to a result array; 
otherwise, -1 is added. This problem can be approached by iterating through all possible 
pairs of x and y from arr and attempting to solve for a and b for each z in queries. (You 
may find better test cases in AI do check) 
Arr = [2, 3] m = 5 queries = [7, 8, 5]:-  Result = [3, 3, 2]. 
I managed to solve around 8 test cases for this problem. 🥲 
2. You are given two arrays of equal length: process[] and state[].The process 
[] array contains alphanumeric process identifiers such as "A1", "B102", etc., while 
the state [] array contains only "Login" or "Logout", indicating the corresponding 
action for each process at the same index. A process’s activation counts increases by 1 
whenever it logs in, and decreases by 1 whenever it logs out, but the count can never go 
below 0. After processing all the events in order, you are given an integer m, and your 
task is to determine which processes remain active in the system with an activation 
count strictly less than m. 
process = ["B1", "A1", "B1", "B1", "A1"] state   = ["Login", "Login", "Login", "Login", 
"Logout"] m = 2 
Result :- ["A1"] 
I solved this question fully, with all test cases passing. 
3. We had 15 MCQs (Java OOPs, DBMS, OS) and the two coding questions above, all on

Interview Preparation: - 
Honestly, I was not fully prepared for the interview. I thought I wouldn’t even get 
shortlisted since I couldn’t solve the first coding problem completely. But to my surprise, 
the results were announced around 9:30 PM, and I got through. I am strong in DSA and 
SQL, so I didn’t even practice a single problem that day. Instead, I started brushing up 
DBMS and OS from GeeksforGeeks, going through only the topics I already knew. I also 
just revised the overview of my projects rather than going in-depth. So, my last-minute 
preparation was mainly on core subjects like OOPs, DBMS, and OS. 
A kind piece of advice: don’t do this! Try to prepare your core concepts well in advance, 
ideally during your semester leave, so you won’t have to rush at the last moment. My 
interview process started the next day around 11:50 AM, right after the online test. 
Technical round-1: - (1hr) 
In the first technical round, the interviewer mainly focused on DSA problems. He quickly 
went through my resume, asked about the languages and tech stacks I had used in my 
projects, and then gave me three DSA problems. 
Problem 1: 
Sort the array in descending order of frequency of elements. If two numbers have the 
same frequency, sort them in ascending order by value. 
arr = [9, 9, 1, 1, 1, 2, 3, 3] result= [1, 1, 1, 3, 3, 9, 9, 2] 
Problem 2: 
 You are given a matrix (graph) represented with edges. For a given src (source node) 
and target (destination node), return true if there exists a path from src to target, 
otherwise return false.  
n = 5 (nodes 0 to 4), edges = [ [0,1], [1,2], [2,3], [3,4]],  src = 0 target = 4 
Ans :- true 
 
Problem 3: 
Two sum problem in which we must return each pair which satisfies the sum=k. 
I have solved all these problems with the help from the interviewer, and he was satisfied 
with all my solutions 🥲 
Technical round-2: -(1hr 15 mins) 
This round was completely focused on CS fundamentals. The interviewer went topic by 
topic and checked how strong I was in the basics. 
OOPs: 
 What is polymorphism? 
 What is static and dynamic binding? 
 Explain abstract classes. 
 Difference between coupling and cohesion. 
DBMS: 
 What are transactions? 
 Explain ACID properties. 
 What is serializability in DBMS? 
 Indexing concepts, difference between B-tree and B+ tree. 
 What is query optimization and its techniques? 
 Sharding, partitioning, and replication. 
 Locks in DBMS transactions. 
Operating System (OS): 
 What is a context switch and why is it important? 
 Explain threads and multithreading. 
 (Some more OS-based questions I can’t recollect). 
Computer Networks (CN): 
 Difference between IPv4 and IPv6. 
 Basics of routing. 
SQL: 
 Write queries using aggregate functions with joins. 
 Implement outer join without using outer join syntax (by using left, right, inner,

DSA: 
 Explain Kadane’s algorithm.

Projects: 
 I explained the motivation behind my projects, why I chose them, and some interesting

🥲 I did answer around four questions incorrectly, but I solved the DSA problem much

HR Round: - (25 mins) 
This round was mainly to evaluate how well I fit into Tekion’s culture and whether I 
would be a good team player. The interviewer started by asking general questions like 
“What is Tekion?” and “Why do you want to join us?” and then moved on to some 
personal and experiential topics. 
She asked about the struggles I had faced so far and how I overcame them. I made sure 
to highlight both my technical skills and soft skills during the discussion. She also went 
through my resume and asked me to explain my projects — for which I gave a brief 
overview. 
At one point, I spoke in detail about my role in a club position I had mentioned in my 
resume. She was interested in knowing about the club and the contributions I had 
made, which gave me a chance to share some of my leadership and teamwork 
experiences. 
Overall, the experience was very positive and smooth. Although I felt nervous initially, 
my anxiety faded after the first few minutes of conversation because the interviewer 
was extremely friendly and supportive. 
 
Finally, the overall interview experience was really good. For students with a CGPA of 
around 7, don’t lose hope — focus on studying the CS fundamentals. I am the first 
student with a 7-pointer CGPA and a history of arrears in CS to get placed within the 
very first month of the placement drive. So, keep preparing and wait for the right 
opportunity, your time will come!
