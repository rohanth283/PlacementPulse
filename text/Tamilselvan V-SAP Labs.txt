My SAP LABS Interview Experience: 
About SAP LABS: 
SAP (Systems, Applications, and Products in Data Processing) is a global leader 
in enterprise software, best known for its ERP solutions that help businesses 
integrate and manage core processes like finance, supply chain, HR, and 
customer service. Headquartered in Germany, SAP operates in over 180 
countries and enables organizations to run efficiently, sustainably, and 
intelligently 
Role:Developer Associate 
 
 
1st Round – Online Test 
The first round was an online coding assessment consisting of two questions: 
1. An array-based problem 
2. A tree-based problem 
Both were of easy to medium difficulty level. 
 
2nd Round – Technical Interview (Round 1) 
From the test, about 90 candidates were shortlisted for the interviews. 
My slot was late in the day, and I was called around 4:20 PM. The panel had 
two members. 
Flow of the Interview 
• Self-Introduction 
• Project Discussion 
o Explained my project. 
o Questions on which data structures I used and why. 
o Asked to normalize an ER diagram from my DBMS project. 
• DSA Questions: 
1. 
Merge point in a linked list 
2. 
Most frequent letter in a word 
• Puzzle: 8-ball puzzle 
• Club Activities: Questions about my involvement 
• SQL Queries: 
1. 
Using subquery 
2. 
Using joins 
• My Questions to Panel: 
o What are the skills an intern can learn at SAP? 
o How does a regular day look like at SAP? 
  Duration: ~50 minutes 
Takeaways: 
• Communicate with clarity and confidence. 
• Keep answers simple; don’t exaggerate (interviewers ask follow-ups 
based on your words). 
• Be thorough with your projects, as most questions revolve around them. 
 
3rd & 4th Rounds – Technical + Behavioral Combined 
Since my previous round ended late, my second technical and behavioral 
rounds were combined into one long session (~90 minutes). 
Panel 
• Senior Architect 
• Manager 
Flow of the Interview 
• Self-Introduction 
• Project Discussion 
o Explained workflow and security aspects. 
o Asked to draw and normalize an ER diagram for another project 
(not the one I had prepared). 
• Technical Questions: 
o API-related concepts 
o One SQL query 
o DoS attack 
o SQL Injection 
o OOPs concepts (4 pillars) 
o Java (multithreading, garbage collection) 
o Indexing 
o Deadlock 
• Behavioral Questions (by Manager): 
o “You say you’re a quick learner and adaptable—can you share a 
situation to prove this?” 
o “Tell me about your journey so far.” 
o Teamwork-related questions 
o Work-life balance questions 
Takeaways: 
• Technical: Strong grasp of core concepts (DBMS – especially 
normalization, CN, OOPs, SQL). 
• Behavioral: Be honest and always back answers with real-life examples. 
 
5th Round – HR Interview 
The final round was the HR round, which lasted about 20 minutes. 
Flow of the Interview 
• Self-introduction 
• Questions about extra-curricular activities 
• “Why SAP?” 
• “What is SAP?” 
• Discussion on package 
• “What will you do if we don’t select you?” 
• Since I mentioned reading books, he asked: “What was the last book you 
read?” 
The HR ended with a friendly note: “Let’s cross our fingers and hope for the 
best.” 
Takeaways: 
• Be genuine and positive in your responses. 
 
Final Result 
Out of 90 candidates, only 10 were selected—and I’m grateful to be one of 
them.   
 
Overall Key Learnings 
1. Confidence + Communication are as important as technical skills. 
2. Don’t overcomplicate or exaggerate—simple and clear answers work 
best. 
3. Be well-prepared with your projects and core subjects (DBMS, OOPs, 
SQL, CN, DSA). 
4. For behavioral rounds, answer honestly with examples from your 
experiences.
