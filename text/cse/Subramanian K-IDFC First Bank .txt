Sliding Window ( Banking Use Case): 
Question: The RBI continuously monitors bank accounts. If three consecutive transactions cross a 
total of ₹800, the account should be blocked for one day. Implement a solution. 
o I explained my sliding window approach: 
▪ Maintain a window of size 3 over the transaction array. 
▪ For each window, check if the sum exceeds ₹800. 
▪ If yes → mark account as blocked. 
o I wrote the code, explained it line by line, and discussed time complexity. 
Sample Input: [200, 350, 400, 100] 
Output: Blocked (because 200+350+400 = 950 > 800).

General Tips: 
• 
Stay confident and calm – don’t put unnecessary pressure on yourself. 
• 
Communicate naturally – speak as if you are talking to a friend; this helps reduce nervousness. 
• 
Maintain composure – don’t be overly afraid; show a balanced level of confidence and positive 
attitude. 
• 
Build rapport – connect with the interviewers by greeting them warmly (e.g., a firm handshake in 
offline rounds). 
• 
Answer smartly – if you don’t know a question, try to relate it to relevant topics you are familiar 
with instead of staying silent. 
• 
First impression matters – give a clear, confident, and engaging self-introduction 
• 
Real life examples-provide real life examples whenever possible
