About the rounds: 
Round 1: Online Test  
The online assessment was conducted on the HirePro Platform and lasted for about 1 hour 
and 30 minutes. It consisted of 3 sections, each with a time limit. 
1. The first section consisted of questions from English Language and Verbal Ability, 
including Sentence Arrangement, Fill in the Blanks, etc. 
2. The second section consisted of Technical Aptitude questions from core CS fundamentals 
such as DSA (Sorting, Graphs, Heaps), DBMS (SQL queries), OOPs, Networking, and 
output-based code questions. It also included questions related to Prompt Engineering 
and Generative AI (basic level). The difficulty ranged from easy to medium. 
3. The third section consisted of two coding questions, both of easy-level difficulty. 
4. A total of 75 students were shortlisted for the next round. 
 
Problem 1: String Rearrangement 
Description: 
You are given a string of uppercase alphabets. Each character in the string needs to be 
rearranged according to the following pattern: 
 
Characters at even positions should be shifted backward by 1 in the alphabet (Z->A). 
 
Characters at odd positions should be shifted forward by 2 in the alphabet (Z → B). 
The task is to apply this transformation to all characters and print the new string. 
Example: 
Input    :     SPLURGE 
Output:     RRKWQID 
 
Problem 2: Reward Score for Customer IDs 
Description: 
 
Given two ID’s(numbers) you have to find the sum of the digits individually for the two 
numbers and then return their product.  
Example: 
Input    :     123 456 
 
Output:     90 
Explanation: 
 
Sum of digits of 123 = 1 + 2 + 3 = 6 
 
Sum of digits of 456 = 4 + 5 + 6 = 15 
 
Final score = 6 × 15 = 90 
 
Round 2:  Technical Interview 
 
The technical interview was conducted at CUIC at 9:00 AM. The session began with a brief 
introduction about the company. 
Unlike most interviews, the interviewer did not ask me to introduce myself. Instead, he directly 
referred to my resume and asked me to explain my favourite project. After my explanation, he 
asked a few follow-up questions related to the project. 
He then asked, “What do you know about Fidelity?” Since I had done prior research about the 
company, I was able to answer confidently. Preparing for such questions can positively impact 
your score. 
Next, he asked me to explain a sorting algorithm of my choice. I chose Merge Sort and 
demonstrated the step-by-step process on paper. 
Following that, he asked which data structures I like. I mentioned Vector and Linked List, and 
explained their pros, cons, and real-world applications. He then moved on to Non-linear Data 
Structures, where I explained Graphs and Trees along with their practical use cases—such as 
file systems (Trees) and maps/navigation systems (Graphs). 
He also asked whether I preferred working in a team, to which I responded positively. 
Towards the end, he gave me the opportunity to ask questions. I asked: 
 
“Could you share your experience at Fidelity?” 
 
“What do you like the most about working here?” 
 
“What kind of projects will interns get to work on at Fidelity?” 
The interviewer was very friendly and patiently listened to my answers. The discussion was highly 
interactive, and I felt comfortable throughout. 
The round lasted for about 30 minutes. One important takeaway is: never hesitate to ask the 
interviewer relevant questions, as it reflects your interest and curiosity. 
Round 3:  HR Interview 
 
The HR interview began with a self-introduction, followed by a few personal questions 
about my family and native place. 
The interviewer then asked me about Fidelity, to which I responded confidently based on my prior 
research. After that, he inquired about my projects. 
One of the key questions was: “Why should we hire you? We can use AI to develop applications.” 
I explained that while AI can indeed assist in building basic applications, a developer’s role is 
crucial for ensuring productivity, handling complex requirements, and maintaining applications in 
case of issues. AI can enhance efficiency, but it cannot replace the need for skilled developers. 
He then asked me about my strengths. Another interesting question was: “Since AI itself can do 
some development tasks, what will you do in the future if your job gets replaced by AI?” 
I answered that I would adapt by shifting my focus towards building applications that leverage and 
automate tasks using AI. 
Towards the end, he asked if I had any questions. I asked: 
 
“How is the work-life balance at Fidelity?” 
 
“What is the working mode for interns—hybrid or in-person?” 
The HR interview lasted for 15–20 minutes. Afterward, around 25 to 30 students awaited the 
results. 
Finally, 12 students were selected for the internship, and I was delighted to be one among 
them. 
 
Key points to note: 
1. Clearly and calmly articulate your thought process during the interview. 
2. Strengthen CS Fundamentals – Be well-prepared in OOPs and DBMS, and also cover 
topics from Operating Systems and Computer Networks. 
3. Know Your Projects Thoroughly – Be ready to explain your projects end-to-end, as most 
technical questions are often based on them. 
4. Focus on One Programming Language – Master one language and practice problem-
solving consistently on platforms like LeetCode, CodeChef, or HackerRank. 
5. Research the Company – Understand the company’s background and prepare meaningful 
questions to ask the interviewer. 
6. Keep the Discussion Interactive – Avoid one-line answers. Engage in a two-way 
conversation.
