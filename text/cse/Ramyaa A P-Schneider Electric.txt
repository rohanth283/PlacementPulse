 
 
SCHNEIDER ELECTRIC 
ABOUT THE COMPANY 
Schneider Electric is a global leader in digital automation and energy management solutions. 
It focuses on electrification, industrial automation, and digitilization, helping industries, 
buildings, and homes operate more efficiently. The company is well known for its 
commitment to sustainability and has received multiple awards in this area. Schneider 
Electric operates worldwide and hires across departments for multiple roles. 
I was shortlisted for the Electrical & Automation (E&A) role. 
SELECTION PROCESS 
1. Online Assessment 
o The assessment was a mix of technical MCQs and a gaming/behavioral test. 
o Technical MCQs:  
Domain-based questions. For Computer Science students, most of the questions were 
from Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA), Operating Systems and Computer 
Networks. Difficulty was of medium level. 
o Gaming/Behavioral Section:  
This stage lasted about 1.5–2 hours and was designed to evaluate both cognitive 
skills and behavioral traits through interactive tasks. It had two components; 
1. Puzzle-Like Games (Time-Bound) 
• Required fast thinking and quick responses. 
• Tested problem-solving, reasoning, decision-making, adaptability, and 
persistence. 
2. Situational Behavioral Questions (~100 questions) 
• Scenario-based personality assessment. 
• Measured teamwork, leadership, stress handling, consistency, and 
adaptability. 
 
 
o Around 120 students were shortlisted across all departments. For the E&A role, 
specifically, 70 students were shortlisted. 
Following this, candidates were called for interviews either in-person or virtual. 
2. Technical Interview 
My technical interview was conducted virtually with a panel of two interviewers. 
o The session began with general questions like 'What do you know about Schneider 
Electric?' and a quick check on my willingness to relocate since they hired only for 
Mysore location. 
o I gave a brief self-introduction, after which they moved to my projects. 
o Since I had developed an Electricity Bill Management System, which aligned closely 
with Schneider’s domain, they were highly interested. 
o For about 20 minutes, I was questioned on various aspects of the project: 
  • Workflow and core functionalities. 
  • The database tables used in the project. 
  • Real-world applications of the system. 
  • Possible extensions or improvements. 
o After the project, they tested my basics from the core subjects — DBMS, DSA and 
Computer Networks. 
o All questions were fundamental level, focusing more on understanding than 
difficulty. 
o The interview tone remained conversational, and I felt they valued clarity and 
relevance over complex answers. 
o At the end of my technical interview, the second panelist asked me some light and 
funny questions to test my spontaneity, such as: 
- 'What was your favorite subject in school?' 
- 'Why did you choose engineering?' (to which I honestly replied that I did not prefer 
medical and hence chose engineering). 
I felt they appreciated my honest and open answers. 
 
 
3. HR Interview 
      The next day, I had a dedicated HR round (~20 minutes): 
o Began with a self-introduction. 
o The HR explained details about the role, responsibilities, and work location. 
o I was given space to clarify queries, making it more of a two-way discussion than a 
Q&A. 
At the end of the selection process, a total of 19 candidates were shortlisted and 4 were 
from the CSE. The offer included a 6-month internship followed by FTE. 
 
KEY TAKEAWAYS 
- Be Honest: I believe my honesty throughout the interview made a strong impression. If I 
didn’t know an answer, I said so, rather than pretending. 
- Know Your Projects: Be ready to explain your projects in detail — they can drive most of 
the interview discussion. 
- Stay Calm in Casual Questions: Even when interviewers get informal, be yourself. It 
shows your personality beyond academics. 
- Understand the Company: Read about their focus areas (in Schneider’s case: 
sustainability, digital automation, energy management). 
 
I strongly believe that authenticity and clarity can take you a long way in interviews. 
Efforts get paid always. Stay CONSISTENT.
