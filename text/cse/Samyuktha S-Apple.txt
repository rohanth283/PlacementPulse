Apple SDE Intern - Interview Experience 
 
About Apple 
Apple Inc., is an American multinational technology company, founded in 1976 by Steve Jobs, 
Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne, and headquartered in Cupertino, California. Apple is a 
global leader in consumer electronics, software, and services. The company is best known for 
products like the iPhone, iPad, Mac computers, Apple Watch, and AirPods. Beyond hardware, 
Apple develops software platforms such as iOS, macOS, watchOS, and iPadOS, and offers 
services including iCloud, Apple Music, and the App Store. The company is renowned for its 
design philosophy, innovation, and ecosystem integration. 
 
Eligibility Criteria 
UG: CS, IT and AI&DS 
CGPA: 7 & above 
No current arrears 
 
Role: Software Development Engineering Intern 
 
Internship Process 
1. Online Test - (Eliminatory) 
2. Resume Shortlisting 
3. Technical Interview - Webex, Coderpad (Eliminatory) 
4. Techno-Managerial Interview - Webex, Coderpad 
 
Online Test 
MCQs from DSA, DBMS, OS, OOP and Networks 
2 coding questions on Data Structures and Algorithms 
 
26 students were shortlisted for the SDE intern interview rounds. 
 
Technical Interview - 45 min 
This round focused on data structures and algorithms, database concepts, and project discussion. 
 
The interview began with the interviewer introducing herself, followed by my introduction. She 
then asked about the project mentioned in my resume — including how the team was formed, the 
project’s objective, the challenges faced, and how they were resolved.  
 
After that, she moved on to DSA questions, such as explaining a Binary Search Tree (BST) and 
finding the Lowest Common Ancestor (LCA) in a BST. First, she asked me to explain my 
approach along with the edge cases, then to implement the solution in CoderPad, and finally to 
discuss the time and space complexities. Next, she asked a few questions from SQL. The 
interview concluded with me asking her a few questions. 
 
After the first round, I received mail for the second round. 
 
Techno-Managerial Interview - 45 min 
This round focused on in-depth technical knowledge, projects, and behavioral assessment. The 
interviewer asked me to introduce myself and then asked questions from every corner of my 
resume. 
 
Questions from JavaScript and React were asked (as mentioned on my resume). Then he asked 
me which language I was most comfortable with. I said Java, so he asked questions such as: what 
is static in Java, why is it used, and if a method is declared static, is an object needed to call it? 
 
Then came questions from my  project: 
●​ What is ML? 
●​ Explain the ML project. 
●​ Explain ML algorithms used in the project, why were these algorithms chosen? 
●​ What is Streamlit? Why Streamlit? 
●​ My contributions to the project. 
●​ Then he asked me to explain my other projects in my resume and the tech stacks used. 
 
Other technical questions include: (as I had mentioned them in my resume) 
●​ What is Firebase?  
●​ When is NoSQL used and when is RDBMS used? 
 
From DSA, I was asked to mention some advanced concepts I am familiar with. I said DP, and 
then he asked me questions on DP. 
 
He also asked about the club I am part of, which I had mentioned in my resume. 
 
Managerial and behavioral questions: 
●​ Why Apple? 
●​ Why you? 
●​ If you are in a team of 2 and the other member is not working on the project, what will 
you do? Will you report it to the team lead? 
 
The interview ended with me asking a few questions. 
 
10 students were selected for the SDE Internship. 
 
General Tips: 
 
●​ Know your resume thoroughly; interviewers can ask questions from anything you 
mentioned.  
●​ When answering DSA questions, first explain your approach and edge cases before 
starting coding. 
●​ Practice DSA mostly in Java or C++ 
 
Materials I used to prepare:  
 
●​ DSA: Striver’s A2Z DSA Sheet, Kunal Kushwaha, Abdul Bari, Jenny’s Lectures  
●​ CS Fundamentals: GeeksforGeeks (Computer Networks, OOP, OS, DBMS) 
 
 
 
Keep learning, keep growing, and give your best at every stage. All the best!
