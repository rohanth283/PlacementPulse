About NatWest 
NatWest Group is a major British financial institution formed in 1970 from 
the merger of National Provincial Bank and Westminster Bank. It is a 
leading bank in the UK offering a wide range of services to personal, 
business, and corporate customers through brands like RBS and Coutts. 
Headquartered in Edinburgh, Scotland, the company has grown through 
the merger of over 200 smaller banks. NatWest is committed to financial 
support, fostering innovation, and building sustainable value for its 
customers and shareholders. 
 
Interview Rounds 
The selection process for the Software Engineer Intern role consisted 
of two stages: 
1. Online Assessment 
2. Technical + HR Round 
A total of 23 students were shortlisted after the first stage for the final 
interview. 
 
1. Online Assessment 
The assessment comprised: 
 Coding Questions (2) 
1. Sort the array of elements based on frequency. 
2. Group the odd numbers first and even numbers next in an 
array of integers. 
(Both were relatively easy and straightforward to solve.) 
 Verbal Aptitude & General Aptitude 
o Topics: profit & loss, percentages, logical thinking, pattern 
finding. 
o Difficulty: Moderate but time-consuming. 
 SQL MCQs 
o Covered basic SQL concepts. 
o Manageable if you are confident with SQL fundamentals. 
 
2. Technical + HR Round(30-40 min) 
This was a combined technical and HR interview. 
 Introduction & Projects Discussion 
o Began with a self-introduction. 
o Explained all three projects in detail; the interviewer 
appreciated the clarity and depth of explanation. 
 Technical Questions 
o Why use MongoDB over SQL? 
o Write an SQL query involving JOINs based on my project’s 
database schema. 
 HR Interaction 
o Asked if I had any questions for them. I enquired about the 
interviewer’s personal experience at NatWest. 
o Unlike my panel, some other interviewers also asked 
candidates DSA and behavioral questions. 
At the end of the process, 9 students were selected for the internship 
— I was fortunate to be one of them. 
 
Key Takeaways 
1. Practice DSA problems daily to stay sharp. 
2. Have a thorough understanding of your projects — being able 
to explain them confidently makes a strong impression. 
3. Communication is key: express your thoughts clearly and 
confidently. 
4. Be genuinely curious to learn. Even if you don’t know something, 
show eagerness to understand. 
5. Start preparing early, and know your resume inside out — every 
point should be explainable. 
 
Overall Experience 
The NatWest interview process was a very positive and enriching 
experience. It tested my problem-solving ability, technical knowledge, 
and communication skills. More importantly, it highlighted the importance 
of being confident about your projects and fundamentals rather than just 
focusing on DSA. I thoroughly enjoyed the process and was delighted to 
secure the internship opportunity.
