Round 1: Online Assesment 
 
 
There were 4 questions totally. 1 easy, 2 medium and 1 hard. 24 students 
from CEG and MIT students were selected for the Technical round.  
 
Round 2 : Technical Round 
 
 
The Technical round primarily focused on networking, databases, and 
computer fundamentals, along with some personal discussion. The interviewer 
began with general questions about my college  whether MIT comes under Anna 
University, where it’s located, and how many companies had visited the campus. 
 
The discussion became conversational  we spoke for nearly 15 minutes about 
football, one of my interests, and about movies with good direction and 
cinematography. The interviewer and I connected well over that.  
 
Then, we moved on to core questions in Computer Networks such as: 
 
• How routing works and what the Internet is. 
• What happens on the backend when a query is typed into Google. 
• How local and global area networks are connected using ISPs and bridges. 
 
In DBMS, I was asked to write a query using UNION. I admitted I wasn’t sure 
and asked the interviewer to explain it  which he did. He asked me few questions 
on Big data as he was working on that currently. He asked me few questions on 
API , though I didn’t know the answer at first I was sure that I would be able to 
deduce it, so I asked the interviewer some time to think and answered it. 
Throughout the interview, I maintained openness to learning even when my 
answers were incorrect, I was eager to understand the correct ones. The 
interviewer appreciated that attitude. 
 
In DSA, I was asked to write a program to reverse a string in any language on 
paper (I used C, though the interviewer was familiar with Python).  
 
Other questions included real-life examples of queues and stacks. He later 
mentioned that my basics were strong and communication was very good, and 
that I had great learning potential. Half of the interview was personal and the 
remaining half was technical.  
 
This round lasted over 40 minutes and 10 of us were selected to the second 
round.  
 
Round 3: HR Round 
 
In the HR round, I was asked to explain my projects in detail. I described 
both my software and hardware based projects thoroughly, which impressed the 
interviewer. Many questions were asked over my projects and I answered most 
of it. When asked why I worked on a hardware oriented project while others 
focused on web development, I explained how hardware projects also help 
computer science students expand their technical understanding and my interest 
over hardware based projects.  
 
He then asked me what an Operating System is and why we need it. My 
explanation caught his attention, and he even introduced me to the head of the 
Pershing branch at BNY to appreciate the way I had explained it. That moment 
emphasized how clarity in basics can make a strong impact. 
 
Finally, among the few behavioural questions the interviewer asked “Why 
BNY?”, I connected my interest in finance with the company’s domain. I shared 
my personal experience with hedge funds, forex markets, stocks, trading firms, 
clearance, investments, bonds, futures and commodities  even mentioning that I 
had done a few trades myself. He encouraged me to add that experience to my 
resume, saying, “You know what kind of company we are right ,why don’t you 
include this in your resume. This gives you a strong edge.” 
 
Finally 6 of us were offered internships.  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Key takeaway’s :  
 
• Be thorough with your resume and projects. Don’t mention anything you 
don’t know in resume. 
• Be strong with your basic DSA, DBMS, OOPS and OS.   
• Stay calm and confident even when you don’t know something. The way 
you handle unknown questions matters more than having every answer 
right. It’s okay to ask for some time to think or say you don’t know. Show 
them you’re willing to learn. 
• Learn about the company and try to speak if you have any personal interests 
over the company’s domain (Finance).  
• Try to connect with the interviewer, both professionally and personally. Try 
to have a conversation with them instead of an interview.
