INTERNSHIP INTERVIEW EXPERIENCE AT KLA TENCOR 
 
About the Company: 
KLA Corporation (NASDAQ: KLAC) is a global leader in process control and yield 
management solutions, specializing in advanced metrology and inspection 
technologies for the semiconductor, data storage, LED, and nanoelectronics 
industries. Headquartered in Milpitas, California, KLA operates globally with 
customer and service centers worldwide. Its technologies enable defect 
detection smaller than a wavelength of visible light, aiding in the production of 
high-performance chips. 
Internship Role: 
• Position: Software Engineering Intern 
• Duration: 8-10 weeks 
• Location: Chennai, India 
• Eligibility Criteria: 
o Only female candidates 
o CGPA: 7.5 or above, with no standing arrears 
• Stipend: Rs 90,000 for undergraduate students. 
Interview Process: 
The interview process for the Software Engineering Internship at KLA consisted 
of four rounds in total. It began with an online test and was followed by three 
interview rounds conducted at their premises. 
Round 1: Online Test 
This round was an online coding test with two questions, and the time limit 
was 1 hour. The questions were designed to test algorithmic thinking and 
problem-solving efficiency. 
Question 1: 
The problem involved distributing API requests across N servers (labelled from 
1 to N), ensuring that the workload remained balanced. Each request added to 
the server's workload, and the goal was to determine which server would 
handle each request. If two servers had the same minimum workload, the one 
with the smaller label should handle the request. 
Hint: My approach used a min-heap to efficiently track the least-loaded server, 
ensuring optimal load distribution for each request. 
Question 2: 
The second problem required minimizing the time to transfer a subsequence of 
size M from an array. The transfer time was the product of the first and last 
elements of the subsequence, and the task was to find the subsequence that 
minimized this product. 
Hint: My approach was to sort the array, iterate over subsequences, calculate 
the product of the first and last elements of each subsequence, and track the 
minimum transfer time across all valid subsequences. 
To excel in the online test, practicing regularly on coding platforms like 
LeetCode and HackerRank is essential. Consistency helps in understanding 
problems quickly and improves efficiency. Solving problems on these platforms 
also enhances your ability to develop optimal solutions under time constraints. 
A week after the test, I was informed that 8 candidates, including myself, had 
been shortlisted for the next rounds. 
Round 2: Technical Interview 1 (45 minutes) 
This technical round took place in person and required solving three problem 
statements on paper. The problems were primarily focused on basic data 
structures and algorithms (DSA), covering topics such as arrays, strings, and 
queues. 
• Start with a brute-force approach, and explain your thought process to 
the interviewer. The key is to keep the interviewer engaged by talking 
through your ideas and explaining the reasoning behind each line of 
code. 
• Before diving into the solution, it's important to explain the 
understanding of the problem to the interviewer, ensuring it's 
interpreted correctly. Actively communicating the approach, asking 
clarifying questions when needed, and explaining the reasoning at each 
step demonstrates critical thinking and collaborative skills. Avoid staying 
silent. 
• It's crucial to solve all three questions correctly to increase your chances 
of advancing to the next round. Most successful candidates completed 
all the problems, so ensure you attempt every question and explain your 
approach clearly. 
Time management is crucial - allocate enough time to attempt all three 
questions, and explain your changes as you go. 
Out of 16 people who were interviewed, 10 of us were selected for the next 
round. I was fortunate to be one of them. 
Round 3: Technical Interview 2 (45 minutes) 
The second technical round involved more advanced DSA topics such as trees 
and graphs. This round had two problem statements, and I was required to 
solve them within the allotted time. 
The interviewer was supportive and helped guide me through the problem, but 
it was important to explain each step clearly and concisely. 
In addition to the technical aspect, communication was key in this round. They 
were also assessing my ability to explain solutions clearly and think logically 
under pressure. It was important to stay calm and manage time well to attempt 
both problems. 
Round 4: HR Interview (20 minutes) 
Out of the 8 candidates remaining, 6 of us advanced to the final HR round. This 
round was more relaxed and conversational, focusing on personality, 
communication skills, and cultural fit. 
• This was the first time I was asked to introduce myself during the entire 
interview process. I delivered a well-prepared and confident 
introduction, aiming to leave a strong first impression. I explained my 
contributions to the projects on my resume, emphasizing my teamwork 
skills, and ensured I had complete knowledge of it since the interview 
was based on it. 
• I had researched KLA’s history, recent achievements, and values. I 
explained how the company’s vision aligned with my personal career 
goals, which helped me stand out.  
• This part of the interview allowed me to showcase my interests outside 
of academics, presenting myself as a well-rounded individual. 
• When asked if I had any questions, I made sure to ask thoughtful ones 
that demonstrated my enthusiasm for the role and the company. It’s 
important not to say "no" to this question, as it provides an opportunity 
to leave a strong final impression. 
Throughout the HR round, I maintained a positive attitude and projected 
enthusiasm for the opportunity at KLA. 
Final Thoughts: 
• Allocating time wisely during technical tests was crucial. 
• Strong knowledge of Data Structures and Algorithms is key. 
• Being on time is essential for professionalism. 
• Ensure your resume reflects true experiences.
