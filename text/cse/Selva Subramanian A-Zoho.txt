Round 3: Long Coding 
Problem Explanation: 
 
Design an inventory management system for an organization that purchases products 
from vendors, stores them in a warehouse, and sells them to customers. The system 
maintains two lists: a purchased list (orders from vendors) and a sales list (orders to 
customers). 
 
Each order has a status attribute with a default value of "paid". When a purchase 
order's status changes to "received", the warehouse stock quantity must increase. When a 
sales order's status changes to "delivered", the warehouse stock quantity must decrease. 
This status-driven mechanism ensures real-time inventory synchronization based on order 
fulfilment events. 
 
Additional Functionalities: 
1.Total Stock Holding for Each Product 
2.Record of Orders Purchased from Vendors 
3.Record of Orders Delivered to Customers 
4.Number of Orders to be Received from Vendors 
5.Number of Orders to be Delivered to Customers 
6.Details of Orders Placed to Vendors Within a Date Range 
7.Details of Orders Placed by Customers Within a Date Range 
They assessed only how you approach the problem rather than implementation. For 
me, they asked to design a database with normalized. Start with a low-level class diagram 
and explain your thoughts to them, making sure to interact with them frequently.
