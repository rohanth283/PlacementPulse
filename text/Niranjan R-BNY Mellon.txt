BNY MELLON – INTERNSHIP INTERVIEW EXPERIENCE 
 
BNY Mellon (The Bank of New York Mellon Corporation) is a global financial 
services company headquartered in New York, formed in 2007 through the 
merger of The Bank of New York and Mellon Financial Corporation. The firm 
focuses on asset servicing, investment management, and treasury services, 
supporting clients in more than 100 markets worldwide. With trillions of 
dollars in assets under custody and management, BNY Mellon is a trusted 
name in the financial industry. The company employs thousands of 
professionals across the globe, with strong technology and operations hubs 
in Pune and Chennai, where much of its innovation and digital 
transformation is driven. 
The Interview process consisted of 3 rounds: 
1. Online Assessment 
2. Technical Interview 
3. Technical + HR Interview 
 
Round-1: Online Assessment 
The test was conducted in Hackerrank platform. There was a total of 4 
questions and we were given 2hrs 45mins to solve them. 
1. Maximizing the Redundancy 
You are given n servers represented as a binary string, where 1 means 
operational and 0 means not operational. At most one operation can be 
performed: choose a consecutive segment and flip all values (1 → 0, 0 → 1). 
The task is to find the maximum redundancy, defined as the number of 
unique possible counts of operational servers after performing the 
operation. 
Sample Input: 0011 
Sample Output: 4 
 
2. Server Clusters 
You are given an array serverProp representing the properties of n servers. 
Two servers at indexes i and j are considered connected if the greatest 
common divisor (GCD) of their properties, serverProp[i] and serverProp[j], is 
greater than 1. Connected servers form clusters in the network. 
You need to return an array of integers where the i-th value represents the 
size of the cluster to which the i-th server belongs. 
Sample Input: n = 3, serverProp = [1, 2, 4] 
Sample Output: [1, 2, 2] 
 
3. Minimum Cost 
There are n points on the x-axis (1, 2, …, n), where each point i has a cost 
associated with it cost[i]. 
Starting from position 0, you can jump forward up to k steps at a time. 
Whenever you land on a point, you incur its cost. The goal is to find the 
minimum cost required to reach point n. 
Sample Input: n = 5, cost = [4, 3, 9, 3, 1], k = 2 
Sample Output: 7 
 
4.Largest Subset 
In a scheduling system, there are n events represented by two arrays, start 
and finish, each of size n. 
 The i-th event begins at start[i] and ends at finish[i]. 
 A subset of these events is considered high-priority if there exists at 
least one event in the subset that overlaps or intersects with every 
other event in the subset. 
Your task is to determine the maximum size of a high-priority subset of 
events. 
Sample Input: n = 4, start = [1, 2, 3, 4], finish = [2, 3, 5, 5]   
Sample Output: 3

Round-2: Technical Interview 
The second round took place at CUIC. A total of 26 candidates were 
shortlisted from the OA and we were split across 6 panels with an 
interviewer in each.  
The interviewer in my panel was very friendly and chill and it helped me 
initially as I was nervous about the Interview. The interview went for about 
30-40 mins. After settling in, the interviewer started browsing through my 
resume and asked me to give an introduction. The interviewer then moved 
on to the technical questions and asked me to choose any of the core 
concepts I had mentioned in my resume for which I chose Object Oriented 
Programming (OOP)  and he started asking some questions about OOP: 
 What are the 4 pillars of OOP? 
 Explain about any one of them with a real life example. 
 Some questions related to relationships in Inheritance. 
Then, he moved on to my projects and asked me to explain about the Real 
Time Chat Application project which I had done using MERN stack. I 
explained my part of the project which I worked with. After that he asked 
me to explain about any one of the projects I had done as per my 
preference, I explained about the Java project (Spaceship Game) in great 
detail (use case, functionalities, etc.). Then he tested my knowledge in DSA 
with a problem: 
 I don’t remember the exact question but it went like this. How would 
you design a parking lot by checking if a slot is free for the car to park 
or not and assign them the free slot. 
Initially, I was not really sure whether it was a coding question so I asked the 
doubts I had and told him my approach and wrote it down in a paper. Even 
though I made few mistakes in my solution, he corrected me and was trying 
to test my thinking skills. Then he asked me to explain about any one sorting 
technique of my choice. 
Finally, he asked me if I had any doubts for which I asked “What kind of 
work we would be assigned to during the Internship?” and “How well did I 
perform in the interview?” for which he pointed out my strengths and 
weaknesses of what he had seen of me in the Interview. I asked some other 
questions as well. 
I was one of the 14 candidates who got shortlisted for the next round. 
 
Round-3: Technical + HR Interview 
It took place sometime after the second round got over. I had a long 
conversation for about an hour with the interviewer. The interview started 
with self-introduction. During the very initial stages, the interviewer pointed 
out and asked why I had mentioned programming languages in a random 
order as he assumed it was in the order of my preference and told me most 
of the people would read it in that way, I was told this in the previous round 
also so avoid this mistake. 
Then, the interviewer asked me various doubts regarding my MERN stack 
project and asked various hypothetical questions while developing a project 
and how I would handle the situation. To give a basic idea, the questions 
were like: 
 How would you deal with a customer when you have time constraints 
on meeting the deadline of a project? 
 How would you deal with a situation when your project members are 
not available? 
I told how I would handle the situations and he further complicated the 
situation of the question and it went on like that. Then, he asked me to 
name the aggregate functions and some inbuilt functions from DBMS. 
Moving on to DSA he asked me 2 questions: 
 4 friends go to a restaurant and pay the bill in a disoriented ratio, how 
would the split be between each of them and he added further more 
which I don’t really remember much. 
 A question related to obtaining a substring from a string. 
For the first question, I wrote my approach in a paper logically explaining 
him and he asked me write the code as well in a language of my preference 
and I wrote in C++. Although my answer was not perfect, I gave the logical 
idea with coding approach and he helped me further and taught me how to 
solve the missing part. For the second question, I gave him a sliding window 
approach for which he agreed with.  
For major part of the interview, I was asked about my projects and many 
hypothetical questions. The interviewer also asked me some practical 
questions related to Software Engineering Concepts like SDLC. He also asked 
me how well do I know Spanish as I had mentioned it in languages in my 
resume. 
Finally he asked me if I had any questions for him, and I questioned with 
various questions like “How did he handle the situation when he was at my 
place (candidate)?” and so on. It was a friendly and casual conversation 
which went on for an extended duration. In the end, he mentioned to me 
that he had a great time conversing with me and the interview ended. 
 
The results were announced after a prolonged wait, and 6 of us were 
selected for the Internship ( 4, 2 from MIT, CEG respectively). 
Tips: 
 Be 100% thorough with all things mentioned in your resume. 
 Try not to be anxious and be as confident as possible during the 
interviews. 
 Don’t panic and stutter when you don’t know the answer for a 
question, such questions are asked to test your decision making under 
pressure and rather reply with similar things relevant to the question 
(“I am not sure on that but I know about this”, “I will look into it”, etc.) 
think for sometime and answer I DON’T KNOW only if you have no 
idea about the question. 
 Be honest with the interviewer. 
 Respond with creative and thoughtful answers which come to your 
mind at that moment. 
 Present yourself showing your genuine interest for the job 
opportunity. 
 Be curious and ask the interviewer various questions when asked to 
making the conversation interactive. 
 Be thorough with the core concepts you have learned up until at the 
time of interview especially DSA, DBMS, OOP. 
 Be prepared on what you tell for self-introduction, this is crucial as the 
interviewer gets keen and curious about the strengths, hobbies and 
other things you mention. 
 Create a habit of practicing DSA problems regularly in coding 
platforms like LeetCode to become more familiar with DSA concepts. 
 
Don’t get vexed if you are not hired, try your best and the good luck, results 
will eventually find you in the future.
