1. Restaurant Queue Management
A restaurant has n seats. An array price is given, where price[i] represents the amount the guest is willing to
pay for being seated. An array logs is given, which represents the order of arrival of the guests. First entry of a
guestId in logs refers to their arrival and the second entry refers to their departure. Constraints to be followed
are,
• A guest can visit multiple times but they will be charged only once.
• A guest has to wait if all the seats are occupied
• If the guest loses patience waiting on the queue, he will leave the queue
• The scheduling has to follow FIFO. [not explicitly specified]
The objective is to find the profit of the restaurant.
Sample Input : n = 2, price = [2, 5, 8, 7], logs = [0, 2, 1, 3, 0, 3, 1, 2]
Output : 15
Source code: github.com/ZhaLabs/AppleInterview/Restaurant
2. Tennis Score
Simulate the scoring system of a Tennis game given the name of player1 playerA and player2 playerB and an
array series where series[i] represents the name of the player who won the ith serve. Constraints for defining
the state of the game are
• A player wins if he has atleast 4 points & leads by atleast 2 points
• If both players have >= 4 points & tied, then its DEUCE
• If both player have >= 4 points & a there’s a 1 point margin, then its Advantage for the player in Lead
• Points progresses as 0 -> 15 -> 30 -> 40
Further reference :
• Article: Tennis scoring system - Wikipedia
• State diagram: Tennis state diagram
Sample Input : playerA = "Jon Snow", playerB = "Tyrion Lanister"
series = ["Jon Snow", "Jon Snow", "Tyrion Lanister", "Jon Snow", "Tyrion Lanister", "Tyrion Lanister", "Tyrion
Lanister", "Jon Snow", "Tyrion Lanister", "Tyrion Lanister"]
Output : "Tyrion Lanister WINS"
Source code: github.com/ZhaLabs/AppleInterview/TennisScorer
Resume Shortlisting
After the Online Assessment, candidates were shortlisted based on their score on the OE as well as their
resume[implicit]. Shortlisted names were announced 15 days before the Technical interview. I wish to let you
know that the flow of Technical interview & Techno-managerial interview and questions asked will depend on
various factors including your resume, your recruiter, the team you have been chosen for[can be indentified from
the linkedin profiles of your recruiter], the environment. In the upcoming segment i will sharing the experience
from my perspective & leave to the readers to make inferences on the Interviewing process of Apple after reading
all other interview experiences.
Technical Interview
The interview started with self introduction and then we proceeded to my project. I was asked the role I played in
the project and later to explain the socket communication which we used in our project. Then I was asked core
concepts from topics including Multi-threading, Context-switches, Normalisation, TCP handshake. Later I was
asked my favorite subject. As I said DSA, I encounter questions on Trees, Time complexities and was asked to
explain the working of Heap using Canvas on the Coderpad, operations & prove the time complexities of the ops &
to implement the same. Later I was asked the preferred language to solve a problem. As I’m comfortable with
multiple languages, I asked the interviewer to let me know the problem as I am problem-centric. He asked about
the TwoSum problem. Once I explained him the two approaches, he asked me to implement the efficient one. I
thought the interview was as we were already running out of the scheduled time. But asked me to solve another. I
was supposed to find the longest common prefix between any pair of strings in an array of strings. I gave him the
brute force solution[L * n2]. As he asked, I optimized it using sorting[L * n log n]. He asked me to optimize it
more. So i used Trie to make it efficient [L * n]

Techno-Managerial Interview
As I was in a hurry, Interview asked me reason behind that. As I confessed about my examination, we went on
sharing each others’ college life. He asked my commitments later on the day even before asking self-introduction. I
felt it had set a comfortable stage for open talks. Then I was asked the following questions.
• Tell me mistake/setback that you see in Apple
• Explain your project.
• What was your role in the project?
• Tell me about your interview experience
• Given the following attributes[Name[duplicates present], Contact[0..*], Courses[0..*]], design a relational
schema
• What the challenges you faced during your project
• Explain the socket programming
• Explain an algorithm that you very comfortable with
• Recent breakthrough in the algorithm[MST] I explained
• What are you key strengths?
• Prove me your leadership with a scenario
• How you handled a time where your teammate was not able to cope up with your project’s progress?
Even after all these question, there was 10 mins remaining in the scheduled time. I wished to utilize the time to
prove myself. So I kept questioning him for more than 20 mins from the inferences I made from his LinkedIn
profile. Results were announced after 4 from the last day of Interview process.
Personal Advice
• Use the self-intro part to set the perfect tone for the rest of the interview.
• Navigating the interviewer to your comfort zone was very hard when comparing to interviews I attended in
other companies.
• My interviewers had 9 & 20 years of experience respectively. So they will find if you pretend to project
yourself as expert in the topic.
• Recruiter were very experienced. So don’t prove you explicitly. Just give them cues. They will scan you.
• During pair-coding think loudly i.e express your thought on the problem to the interview
• As said earlier, questions depends on the interviewers. When asked to solve problems, try to ask the problem
first then let him know language of your choice. I’m repeating because, a friend of mine who very much
equipped with the required skillset, encounter a question which i felt unfair. He chose C++ but was supposed
to store objects of different type in same container and was supposed to do object destructuring.
