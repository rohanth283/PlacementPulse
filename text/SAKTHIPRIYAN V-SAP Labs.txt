Questions asked for me: 
1) Problem: Cover the Holes with Wood 
You are given a table with holes at certain positions. Your task is to cover all the holes using 
exactly two wooden planks of the same length. You have woods of infinite size and also the 
table has infinite length, but you want to minimize the wooden plank size. 
• An integer array holes[] representing the positions of holes on the table. 
• 1 <= holes.length <= 10^5, 0 <= holes[i] <= 10^9(unique) 
Eg: holes = [1, 3, 4, 7, 9], Output: 5 
 
Explanation: One plank of length 5 can cover holes at positions [1,3,4], and other plank for the 
remaining holes.  
My thoughts: It is basically a medium-easy qns which can be solved using binary search. 
 
2) Problem: Graph Disappearance Time 
You are given a graph which may or may not be connected. Each second, any node with at most 
one edge disappears, and all edges connected to it also disappear. Your task is to determine the 
minimum time required for the entire graph to disappear. 
Eg:  
Input: n = 5, edges = [[1,2],[2,3],[3,4],[4,5]] 
Output: 3 
 
Explanation: 
- Second 1: Nodes 1 and 5 disappear (each has 1 edge), edges [1,2] and [4,5] removed. 
- Second 2: Nodes 2 and 4 now have 1 edge each, disappear, edges [2,3] and [3,4] removed. 
- Second 3: Node 3 disappears. 
Minimum time for full disappearance = 3 
My thoughts:  This is a perfect medium qns, can be solved using topo-bfs in graph. 
 
A total of 89 students were shortlisted for the interview. The results were announced just one 
day before the interview giving me roughly 12 hours to cram a month’s worth of prep. 
 
 
Round 1 (Technical round 1): 
The interview kicked off with the usual self-intro. As expected, he dove straight into my resume 
projects, skills, everything I had put down. The first part was basically a deep dive into my ML 
project: 
• What does it do? 
• How did I implement it? 
• Which model did I use and why? 
• Why did u choose  this? 
• And, of course, the results. 
The interviewer didn’t stop there; he went through all my other projects with the same level of 
curiosity. He also asked, how I implemented “Interest based pair pooling” which I have done in 
my project (like pairing some anonymous users to interact based on interest, similar to omegle 
but with filters). It was like a rapid-fire quiz: one question, one-line answer, bam, immediate 
follow-up. But somehow, I survived. 
Then came a simple DSA question: reverse the words in a sentence. Easy right? I went from 
brute force to optimal solution, and he hit me with a follow-up: “Now reverse the letters inside 
the words”. Literally twisting the whole string-reversal logic!! I answered that too. 
Suddenly, the Tech round felt more like a funny HR one. He asked about my native place, MLA 
there , obviously we discussed about TVK and some politics. Later, He asked about SAAS, which 
I had on my resume. I ended up explaining  Techofes, Agni in detail ... felt like a proud moment 
as an Organizer. We spent more time in this part. 
Then context switching happened in real life HR qns again turned into Technical qns where it 
was left off, he threw in some OS questions: system calls, CPU scheduling, scheduling types and 
he asked me to explain FCFS, Priority and Round Robin Scheduling and other related concepts.. 
He also asked some cool things about cpp, asked me to explain about pointers, its advantages 
and its limitations. He also asked much more OS questions. 
Finally, the round ended with a puzzle: I have three jugs => 5L, 8L, and 12L. How do I measure 
exactly 6L with minimum swap operations? At the end, the interviewer asked: “Do you have 
any questions for me?” I didn’t ask him any. But U guys Don’t skip it. Ask meaningful questions 
(about role, team culture, learning opportunities, etc.). It creates a positive impression. 
After this round, I was shortlisted for the next stage. Out of all the participants, around 25 
students were shortlisted for Technical Round 2.

Disclaimer: 
All the above has been toned down to a professional version, removing the humorous elements 
of my interview. In reality, my interview was much more humorous. Honestly, it felt like all the 
rounds were HR-oriented, from the very first technical round to the last. I think, it is up to us to 
decide the flow and direction of the interview.  I cracked many jokes in this interview and 
surprisingly that worked too.. Pls  Don’t try this at home, these kinds of stunts are depends 
entirely on interviewers and their personalities, Thank God I had some cool guys. 
My Advice: 
My advice to juniors is to approach interviews with a calm and confident mindset. Be chill, 
Don’t panic, interviews aren’t boss battles in Soul Games, they’re just conversations (well, 
mostly). Avoid overthinking, and instead, focus on showcasing your passion and consistency, as 
both are key to long-term success . Be thorough with your resume and ensure you are well-
prepared to discuss every detail you have included. Confidence, clarity, and composure will 
help you stand out. Finally, remember that not being selected is not the end there will always 
be more opportunities ahead. Choose your company wisely.
