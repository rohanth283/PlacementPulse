Role: Software Development Engineering Intern 
Interview Process: 
The selection process comprised the following stages:  
1. Online Test (Eliminatory) 
2. Resume Shortlisting  
3. Interview Round 1: Technical & Eliminatory (Eliminatory)  
4. Interview Round 2: Techno-Managerial 
Online Test: 
The first stage was an online test designed to filter candidates based on problem-solving 
ability and core technical knowledge. It included both coding challenges and concept-based 
questions. Only shortlisted candidates moved forward. 
Coding problems: 
1. Restaurant Gain Calculation 
Problem Statement: 
A restaurant has a fixed number of seats (nbSeats). Each guest is associated with an 
amount they are willing to pay (payingGuests[id]). The sequence of guestMovements 
represents guests entering and leaving the restaurant. 
o The first appearance of an ID → arrival. 
o The second appearance of the same ID → departure. 
o The task is to calculate the total gain of the restaurant for the day, ensuring that at 
any given time the number of seated guests does not exceed nbSeats. 
 
2. Tennis Game Scoring Simulation 
Problem Statement: 
Implement the logic for scoring in a tennis game based on standard rules. 
 
o Scores progress as: 0 → 15 → 30 → 40. 
o When both players have ≥3 points and are tied → Deuce. 
o When both have ≥3 points but one leads by 1 → Advantage (Player X). 
o A player wins if they have at least 4 points and a lead of 2 → Win (Player X). 
o The task is to simulate the game and correctly output the score/state after each 
point. 
Technical Concept Questions: 
The test also had objective type of questions from core CS domains such as: 
o Programming fundamentals 
o Operating Systems  
o Databases  
o Data Structures & Algorithms  
o Time Complexity & Analysis 
o Computer Networks  
Interview Round 1: 
This round tested my problem-solving skills, how effectively I communicated and explained 
my approach, and how well I articulated my thoughts.  Since the interviewer had knowledge 
about the models (that were used in my project) mentioned in my resume, the discussion 
started with questions related to my project work after I had introduced myself: 
 Project-related Questions: 
o Basics of Natural Language Processing (NLP). 
o Tools and techniques like spaCy, transformers, and lemmatisation. 
o Understanding of tokenization, embeddings, and NLP pipelines. 
 Coding Problem: 
o Merge Two Sorted Arrays: Given two sorted arrays, merge them into a single 
sorted array without using extra space. This tested both algorithmic thinking 
and in-place array manipulation skills. 
This round was eliminatory, so solving the coding problem efficiently and explaining the 
project concepts clearly was crucial to move to the next round. 
In the evening of the same day, I received an email and a call from Apple confirming that I 
had cleared the first round and would be appearing for the next round the following day. 
Interview Round 2: 
This round was a mix of behavioral, managerial, and technical questions designed to evaluate 
not only technical depth but also communication, teamwork, and problem-solving approach 
in real-world scenarios. 
 The interviewer started with her introduction and asked me to introduce myself, 
which set the tone for the discussion. 
 The conversation then moved to my internship experience, including the projects I 
worked on, challenges I faced, and how I overcame them. More than half of the 
discussion focused on these experiences, testing collaboration, adaptability, and 
conflict-resolution skills. 
 Some behavioral questions included: 
o What are your strengths? 
o How would you handle a situation where a teammate is struggling to keep up 
with the pace of a project? 
o Scenario-based technical questions, e.g., “What would you do if a search takes 
more time?”—testing knowledge of indexing, query optimization, and caching 
strategies. 
This round assessed how well I could articulate my approach, reflect on past experiences, 
and apply learning from real projects, combining both behavioral insight and technical 
understanding. 
After completing all the rounds, 10 students were selected for the role.
