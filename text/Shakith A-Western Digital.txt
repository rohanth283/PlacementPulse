Western Digital – Internship 
About the Company: 
Western Digital Corporation (WDC), commonly known as Western Digital or WD, is an 
American computer drive manufacturer and data storage company headquartered in San 
Jose, California. It designs, manufactures, and sells data technology products, including data 
storage devices, data center systems, and cloud storage services. Western Digital has a long 
history in the electronics industry as an integrated circuit maker and storage products 
company. It is one of the largest computer hard disk drive manufacturers and also produces 
solid-state drives and flash memory devices. Its competitors include Seagate Technology and 
Micron Technology. 
Interview Process: 
There was no coding round. I was shortlisted based on my resume, which includes an IoT 
project that I completed in my 4th semester as part of the Naan Mudhalvan curriculum. In 
total, 8 candidates were shortlisted for the technical interview, including 3 from CEG (2 from 
CSE and 1 from ECE) and 5 from MIT. 
Technical Interview: 
• 
Duration: 30 Minutes 
• 
Location: CUIC 
During the technical interview, I began by introducing myself and elaborating on the 
positions of responsibility and projects detailed in my resume. The interviewer then posed 
two technical questions related to linked lists. The first question required me to explain the 
logic for merging two linked lists, while the second focused on removing duplicates from a 
sorted linked list. For both questions, I was asked to illustrate the logic on paper rather than 
writing code. Following the technical discussion, the interviewer asked why I was interested 
in joining Western Digital and inquired about my family background. The interview 
concluded with an opportunity for me to ask questions. Out of the 8 students who 
participated in this round, 3 were selected for the next stage. 
HR Round: 
• 
Duration: 10 Minutes 
• 
Location: CUIC 
During the HR round, the interviewer first asked me to describe my strengths. This was 
followed by a question about why I chose Anna University for my bachelor’s degree. I was 
then asked to discuss my weaknesses and provide an example of a situation where I worked 
effectively as part of a team. The interview concluded after addressing these topics. 
All 3 of us were selected for the 2-month summer internship. 
Notes: 
1. Answer confidently. Even if you don’t know the exact answer, take them through 
your thought process. 
2. Don’t fake your resume and be clear about what you include. 
3. Review concepts in DBMS, OOP, OS, and DSA to excel in technical interviews. 
4. Research the company thoroughly before the interview; it creates a good impression. 
5. If you really want to join the company, mention relevant projects like DFCO or IoT 
projects in your resume.
