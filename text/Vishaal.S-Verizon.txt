Verizon Interview Experience 
Verizon is a leading global communications and technology company, ranked as the #1 
wireless provider in the United States and a global leader in next-generation technologies 
such as 5G and the Internet of Things (IoT). Verizon is recognized for its innovation, 
advanced network technology, and enterprise solutions. In 2024, Verizon was ranked as the 
8th Best Workplace Worldwide by LinkedIn, highlighting its strong culture, employee 
development, and leadership in technology. Working at Verizon provides opportunities for 
professional growth, exposure to cutting-edge tools, and being part of a globally respected 
brand. 
Recruitment Process 
1. Online Assessment (Aon Cocubes) 
The online assessment evaluated multiple skills: 
• 
Coding Round (2 Questions): 
1. Check if the sum of digits of a number N is prime  
2. Given N, T, energy gains , and jumps  calculate the minimum hours required 
to reach or exceed target energy. 
• 
Essay Writing: Tested critical thinking and writing skills. 
• 
Language Proficiency: A 4-minute rapid-fire section to answer as many grammar, 
spelling, and vocabulary questions as possible. Wrong answers had negative marks, 
but skipped questions carried no penalty—speed and accuracy were key. 
• 
Puzzle Section: Medium-level puzzles in a rapid-fire format tested logical reasoning 
and problem-solving speed. 
Outcome: 82 candidates were shortlisted for the GD round. 
2. Group Discussion (GD) 
• 
Topic: How India Serves as a Consumer Base for Global Market Industries 
• 
Format: 9 participants per panel, 20 minutes. 
Tips: 
• 
Focus on quality points, not quantity. 
• 
Don’t dominate but participate meaningfully. 
• 
Use examples or data to support your ideas. 
• 
Acknowledge and build on others’ points politely. 
• 
Maintain confident body language and show active listening. 
 Outcome: 50 candidates were shortlisted for the next round. 
3. AI Prompting Round 
This round was not preliminary but a key part of the process. Candidates solved problem 
statements (Realtime System failure concepts, Marketing Solution)using Prompt Engineering 
techniques such as one-shot prompting, role-based prompting, and structured prompts. 
Tips: 
• 
Understand the problem clearly before writing your prompt. 
• 
Use concise instructions with context. 
• 
Experiment with role-based prompts for better results. 
• 
Practice different prompting strategies in advance. 
• 
Stay updated on advancements in AI tools and methods. 
 Outcome: No eliminations took place in this round. 
4. Technical Interview 
My technical interview lasted 45 minutes, but durations can vary. If the panel finds you 
reliable and knowledgeable, they may extend the questioning. 
Key Areas Covered: 
• 
Programming Languages: Be thorough in the languages you mention. 
• 
Java Concepts: Core Java, OOP principles, and sometimes interface-related coding 
tasks. 
• 
Generative AI and Prompt Engineering: Questions on GenAI models, use cases, and 
prompt strategies. 
• 
Projects: Detailed discussions on project schemas, architecture, challenges, and 
contributions. 
• 
Panel Variation: Questions may vary depending on the panel. 
Tips: 
• 
Know your resume inside-out—anything you list can be asked. 
• 
Be well-prepared for programming fundamentals and AI advancements. 
• 
Understand your project schema and architecture in detail. 
• 
Revise Java basics, data structures, DBMS, and OS. 
• 
Be honest if unsure—explain your thought process. 
• 
Connect your experiences to Verizon’s technologies and values. 
 Outcome: 32 candidates advanced to the HR round, and all were finally selected. 
5. HR Round 
The HR round was casual and friendly, focusing on cultural fit. 
Questions Asked: Family background, hometown, interests, and flexibility for relocation. 
Tips: 
• 
Be genuine and confident. 
• 
Show enthusiasm for Verizon’s culture and opportunities. 
• 
Prepare thoughtful questions about growth and company culture. 
Outcome: Confirmation mails and shortlisted names were shared after HR—not immediate 
offer letters. 
 
Additional Tips for Success 
• 
Coding: Practice competitive programming, focus on time complexity and edge 
cases. 
• 
GD: Stay informed on current affairs and global market trends, especially India’s role 
in global trade. 
• 
AI Prompting: Learn few-shot, zero-shot, and role-based prompting. 
• 
Technical: Be thorough with programming languages, project schema, and AI tool 
advancements. Expect extended questioning if you’re deemed reliable. 
• 
General Advice: Stay calm, answer confidently, and maintain a positive attitude—
confidence matters as much as correctness.
