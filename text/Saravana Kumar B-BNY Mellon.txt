First Round – Online Assessment 
The first round was an online coding assessment. It consisted of 4 coding 
questions 2 medium and 2 hard and the total time given to solve them was 2 hours and 
45 minutes. 
 Question 1: Optimal Team Selection (Medium) 
You are given n developers with unique skill levels and two arrays: lowerSkill and 
higherSkill. 
lowerSkill[i] shows how many teammates with lower skill a developer can work 
with. 
higherSkill[i] shows how many teammates with higher skill a developer can work 
with. 
You need to find the largest possible team where every developer’s limits are 
satisfied. 
Sample Input: n = 6, lowerSkill = 1,1,2,2,3,5, higherSkill = 5,4,3,2,1,0 
Sample Output: 5 
Explanation: The largest team that satisfies everyone’s limits has 5 developers. 
 Question 2: Maximum Redundancy of Servers (Medium) 
You are given a binary string where 1 means the server is working and 0 means it 
is not. You can choose one continuous segment of servers and flip them (0 to 1 
and 1 to 0). 
You need to find the maximum number of different possible counts of working 
servers after flipping at most once. 
Sample Input: servers = 010 
Sample Output: 3 
Explanation: After flipping the best segment, there are 3 possible counts of working 
servers. 
Sample Input: servers = 111 
Sample Output: 4 
Explanation: Flipping one segment can give 4 different possible counts of working 
servers. 
 Question 3: Largest High-Priority Subset of Events (Hard) 
You are given n events with start and finish times. A high-priority subset is a group 
of events where at least one event overlaps with every other event in the group. 
Find the largest size of such a subset. 
Sample Input: n = 4, start = 1,2,3,4, finish = 2,3,5,5 
Sample Output: 3 
Explanation: The largest subset where one event overlaps with all others has 3 events.

Key Takeaways: 
 Problem Solving & DSA: Start solving problems as early as you can. In interviews, 
start with a brute-force approach first and then gradually optimize to an efficient 
solution. 
 Have a clear understanding and hands-on experience with all the projects you 
have worked on. This plays a crucial role in interviews. 
 Be ready to answer all kinds of questions from your resume confidently. 
 Be strong in DBMS, OOPs, and other core subjects, as technical questions can 
cover these areas. 
 If you don’t know the exact answer, don’t just say “I don’t know.” Try to reason it 
out or provide a logical approach, even if it’s not fully correct, interviewers want 
to see your thinking process. 
 
Don’t get discouraged if you don’t get shortlisted. Keep putting your best efforts, stay 
consistent, and never lose hope. The right opportunity will come to you, just keep trying 
and trust your journey!
