PROCESS 
 
Total Rounds: 4 (Each round was an eliminatory round- Once a round was cleared by the 
candidate, they received a link(with time slot mentioned) for the next round.) 
 
Round 1 : ONLINE ASSESSMENT (HACKERRANK – 18th sep 2025) 
 
This was a 90-minute round that consisted of both multiple-choice questions (18–20 questions 
focused on DSA, OOPs, DBMS, CN, and OS) and two coding problems (listed below). 
           1) First Lady of Software - You are given: n_processes: number of processes (for example, 2 
processes — A and B), n_intervals: number of time intervals (for example, 3 intervals). At each time 
interval, one process runs. You need to find how many different valid schedules are possible to assign 
the processes to these intervals. 
           2) Score of an array – You are given an array of integers. You can delete any elements                        
you want in any order. After all deletions, the array reindexes (positions shift). The score is the 
count of elements where the value equals its position (1-indexed). Find the maximum possible score 
you can achieve after any number of deletions. 
 
Round 2 : TECHNICAL INTERVIEW (MICROSOFT TEAMS – 29th sep 2025) 
            The interview round commenced with a brief self-introduction from both the interviewer and 
me. It then proceeded to a discussion of my projects, where I was asked to explain one in detail. The 
interviewer subsequently evaluated my understanding of core subjects, focusing on intermediate-level 
concepts. Following this, my programming proficiency was assessed through a coding problem 
(stated below) in my preferred language, along with three subsequent modifications to the same 
problem. The interviewer appeared satisfied with my responses (approaches) and concluded the 
session by inviting me to ask any questions. 
 
            Coding problem: Given an integer array a and an integer d, determine whether the array 
contains two identical elements such that the distance between their indices is at most d. If such a pair 
exists, return True; otherwise, return False. 
            This round lasted for approximately 30 minutes. 
Round 3 : TECHNO- MANAGERIAL ROUND (MICROSOFT TEAMS – 30th sep 
2025) 
       This round began with a self-introduction from both the interviewer and me. He then asked me 
to provide an overview of each of my projects before moving on to the coding section. In this part, he 
set a time constraint for solving four coding problems(stated below) but deliberately did not disclose 
the duration, mentioning that he wanted to observe my problem-solving pace. Fortunately, I was able 
to complete all four problems ahead of time (trick:I started coding as he began explaining the 
problem). The interviewer appreciated my performance, informing me that I had finished the tasks in 
nine minutes, whereas the allotted time was twenty minutes. He appeared quite impressed by this. 
       Afterward, he asked a few personal questions about my hobbies, hometown, family background, 
and some project-related managerial questions, along with a few about the company. The interviewer 
maintained a friendly and engaging demeanor throughout the session, which made the interaction 
smooth and enjoyable. Finally, he concluded the interview by asking if I had any questions for him. 
      Coding questions : Missing Number , Prime factors of a number, Volume of sphere , cube and 
cylinder,  Max Consecutive Ones. 
      This round lasted for 30-40 minutes. 
Round 4 : HR ROUND (MICROSOFT TEAMS – 1st  oct 2025) 
     This round started with a self-introduction. I was then asked to explain my project in a non-
technical manner, focusing on aspects such as teamwork, conflicts, and challenges. The interviewer 
also asked in-depth questions about my contributions and experiences in each club, which were quite 
challenging and required me to think critically and respond on the spot. Additionally, she asked a few 
situational questions and asked me to share one piece of advice that I would give to my juniors or 
siblings. She seemed delighted with my responses and communication skills(interaction). The 
conversation concluded with her asking if I had any questions for her, followed by a discussion about 
my location preferences. 
     This round lasted for 20-30 minutes. 
A Few Tips from My Experience: 
       Be consistent in practicing Data Structures and Algorithms, and make sure to cover all core 
subjects such as OOPs, DBMS, CN, OS, and System Design. Explain every concept with real-life 
examples to build strong conceptual clarity. Keep revising them. Dive deep into your resume and 
explore everything you’ve mentioned - be prepared to discuss any project, achievement, or experience 
in detail. During interviews, keep the conversation engaging by maintaining good communication 
with the interviewer. If you fail in any interview, don’t be disheartened - take it as motivation to 
improve and keep moving forward. Never give up on this journey until you achieve your goals. Face 
peer pressure with optimism and remain confident, consistent, and truthful to your preparation 
process. Work hard, and trust that success will come to you at the right time. 
                                        Don’t chase results - master the process. All the best!
