BANK OF AMERICA  
 
The Bank Of America Corporation (often abbreviated BofA or BoA) is an American Multinational 
Investment bank and Financial services holding company. The interviews in our college were for the 
GET (Graduate Engineer Trainee) roles. Bank Of America has its offices in Mumbai, Chennai, 
Gurugram, Gandhinagar (GIFT), Hyderabad in India. 
 
ONLINE TEST 
It consisted of two sections: 
• 
2 coding questions (to test programming skills) on basic topics like arrays, strings. 
• 
3 video questions (to test communication skills): 
o  Tell how you are the best fit for the role. 
o  Explain your approach of solving the second coding question. 
o  Explain the projects you have done so far. 
 
INTERVIEW 
Round 1 (around 30 mins): 
This was a face-to-face interview round held at CUIC. This was a technical + HR round.  
• 
Introduction 
• 
OOPS concepts with code (Objects, classes, polymorphism, overriding, constructors). 
• 
DBMS (SQL queries- group by, joins, count function). 
• 
Coding (Finding max, min of given numbers, binary tree lowest common ancestor)-Brute, 
better, optimal, time and space complexity. 
• 
HR - What would you do if you face any conflicts or difference of opinions in your team? How 
do you resolve it? 
• 
Extra curricular from Resume (NSS in my case) - What all have you done at NSS? 
• 
Five languages mentioned in Resume (just short friendly talk on how I learnt them). 
• 
Asked me if I have any questions for them to answer. 
 
Round 2 (25 - 30 mins): 
This was also a face-to-face interview round held at CUIC. I was called immediately in five mins after 
the first round. This was also a technical + HR round. 
Completely on tech stack mentioned in Resume (both in skills and projects section from Resume): 
• 
First they asked me to give brief explanation of all the tech stacks I know. 
• 
API ( How did u fetch data from it , write code) 
• 
XML, HTML, CSS, React ( All basic questions with code, both from projects as well as general 
questions on them). 
After these questions, I was asked the following: 
• 
Questions on SQL again (All types of joins and for the given qn why do we use that particular 
join). 
• 
DBMS - SQL - Why do we say it’s a relational DB? 
• 
With my project, since tech stacks like React or XML (JSX), or API cannot have relational 
domains then how do u integrate with relational domain like SQL? 
• 
The interviewer gave two tables and asked why it was necessary to split them into two 
instead of a single table. 
I was then asked HR questions: 
• 
What would you do if a member of your team doesn't respond well or complete their work? 
• 
What would you do and how would you learn if a new tech is given to you in a project? 
Then asked me if I have any questions for them to answer. 
 
TOPICS TO BE FOCUSSED 
Good knowledge in Coding, OOPS, DSA, OS, Reactjs, Nodejs, SQL. An in-depth understanding about 
the projects listed on the resume and related concepts. 
 
TAKEAWAY 
The interviews went smoothly, and the interviewers were very supportive. Having a solid 
understanding of all the projects listed on my resume and their related concepts proved invaluable. 
Clear communication of my ideas played a crucial role, especially during the technical rounds when I 
didn’t have precise answers. Be confident, interactive and share your thoughts throughout.
