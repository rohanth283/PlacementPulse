Interview Phase 
The interview process consisted of three rounds, two being technical and one HR. 
Round 1 (Technical – 30 mins): 
The interviewer began by asking me to introduce myself, including my interests, the projects 
I had listed on my resume, and how I approached learning new skills. He also inquired about 
my experience with platforms like LeetCode and other coding practice tools. 
After discussing these topics, he presented me with a logical reasoning problem: I had to 
identify the odd ball among eight balls, where seven balls were of the same weight, and one 
was either heavier or lighter. The challenge was to find the odd ball using a balance scale 
with just two weighings. 
Following this, I was asked to write pseudocode for a simple coding problem involving 
arrays. The task tested my ability to break down a problem logically and present a clear, 
step-by-step solution. The focus was on understanding my thought process and how I 
approached problem-solving in coding scenarios. 
 
Round 2 (Technical & Behavioral – 15 mins): 
This round was more relaxed and focused on my favorite and least favorite subjects. The 
interviewer asked technical questions related to the languages I was comfortable with, 
testing my in-depth knowledge. I was also asked about the time complexity of Quick Sort. 
 
Round 3 (HR – 10 mins): 
This round was a standard HR interview where I was asked about my background, goals, 
and why I wanted to join Appian. The interviewer inquired about my future plans, including 
whether I intended to pursue a Master's degree, and discussed my resume in detail. 
 
 
 
Tips: 
● Be Honest: If you don’t know the answer to a question, take a moment to think about 
it. If after 5 or 6 minutes you still can’t solve it, it’s better to be upfront about it. 
● Time Management: Don’t spend too much time on questions you are unsure of. 
Focus on answering what you know well. 
● Be Prepared: Have some questions ready for the interviewers, as this can leave a 
positive impression. 
● Research: Make sure to research the company before attending the interview. This 
helps in answering questions related to your interest in the company and its work 
culture.
