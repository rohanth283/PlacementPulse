EUCLOID INTERVIEW EXPERIENCE (Intern + FTE) 
About the Company: 
Eucloid is a premier Data/AI organization that leverages state-of-the-art AI and Data 
Science technologies to deliver high-impact projects. It creates business-critical solutions for 
Fortune-100 clients and industry leaders across various sectors, including Hi-tech, BFSI, D2C, 
Healthcare, SaaS, and Retail. Their deep partnerships with leading platforms like Databricks, 
Google Cloud Platform, and Adobe enables to build and deploy cutting-edge data products

top-tier institutes and leading global organizations, thrives in an environment that prioritizes 
growth, innovation, and excellence 
 
Total Rounds:  
1. Online Assessment  
2. Technical Interview  
3. Interview with Leadership Team (Technical + HR) 
 
 
1. Online Assessment - Virtual : 
 
The first round was an online assessment consisting of 30 multiple-choice questions 
and 2 coding problems, designed to evaluate candidates’ analytical, logical reasoning, 
quantitative aptitude, and problem-solving skills. 
 
 
• Logical reasoning 
• Quantitative aptitude 
• 2 Coding questions -String, Array manipulation -Easy level 
2.Technical interview: 
 
              All the candidates who attended the online test were invited to the pre-placement 
talk, where the company representatives provided an overview of Eucloid Data Solutions. 
The session also featured a virtual appearance by the founder, and we had the opportunity 
to interact with our seniors currently working at Eucloid. 
The list of shortlisted students for the technical round was announced at 11:45 AM 
— out of 150 participants, 32 were selected. My technical interview was scheduled virtually 
through CUIC at 12:30 PM. 
The technical interview lasted for around 30 minutes. It began with a brief self-
introduction followed by a detailed discussion about my projects. Since one of my projects 
was deployed, the interviewer asked about the tech stack used, the purpose of using Docker, 
and other related aspects. She then assessed my SQL knowledge with conceptual questions 
such as: 
• 
What is the difference between RANK and DENSE_RANK? 
• 
What is the difference between DROP and TRUNCATE? 
• 
What is the difference between UNION and UNION ALL? 
• 
What is the difference between WHERE and HAVING clauses? 
I explained each question with suitable examples. 
Next, I was asked to solve a few SQL queries: 
1. From the Employees table, find departments where the average salary is greater than 
70,000. 
2. Write queries to: 
o Find the top 3 highest spending customers. 
o Fetch product IDs without duplicates. 
o Retrieve the 2nd highest purchase amount. 
I wrote and explained my answers clearly to the interviewer. 
The session then moved to the coding section, where I was asked to type and run two 
programs using an online compiler with different test cases: 
1. Majority Element – (Moore Voting Algo) 
Input: [2,2,1,1,1,2,2] → Output: 2  
2. Longest Substring Without Repeating Characters – (Sliding Window +Hashing) 
 Input: "abcabcbb" → Output: "abc" 
I explained my logic step-by-step and demonstrated the correct outputs. Towards the 
end, I had the opportunity to ask a few questions about the role, and she responded with 
detailed technical insights. 
With that, the first round concluded, and a total of 10 candidates were shortlisted 
for the next round. 
 
3. Interview with Leadership Team (Technical + HR): 
The final round took place after a day and was conducted by the Senior Director. He 
began the interview by directly diving into my full-stack project and asked me to share my 
screen since the project was deployed online. It was a great opportunity to demonstrate my 
technical and presentation skills. 
I walked him through each page of the project, explaining the purpose, design logic, 
and technical reasoning behind every feature. He showed keen interest in the functionalities 
and was particularly impressed by the customized features we had implemented. 
After the project discussion, he gave me a Camel and Banana Puzzle. I used MS Paint 
to illustrate my approach with a diagrammatic explanation. Before I could reach the final 
answer, he mentioned that he was satisfied with my reasoning and problem-solving 
approach. 
Towards the end, he invited me to ask questions, and I took the opportunity to learn 
more about the company’s operations, clients, and the type of data-driven solutions they 
provide. 
This round lasted for about 25 minutes and went very smoothly. 
 
The results were announced two days later, 8 were selected. I’m delighted to share that I 
was one among them.

POINTS TO SAY: 
This was my 10th interview, and the journey wasn’t easy. I faced several rejections 
— sometimes because I couldn’t provide an optimal solution to a hard coding question, 
sometimes due to topics outside my resume, and a few times for no clear reason at all. I’ve 
been rejected in the first round, the second round, and even after clearing all rounds . 
There were moments of disappointment, self-doubt, and frustration, but every 
rejection taught me something valuable. 
 
 
 
To everyone preparing — remember this: 
“You didn’t come this far to only  come this far.” 
Keep believing in yourself, trust the process, and never give up. 
There’s always something greater waiting ahead
