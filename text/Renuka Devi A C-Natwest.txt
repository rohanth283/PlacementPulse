2. ONLINE ASSESMENT 
 
It consists of the following sections: 
1. Verbal Aptitude (15 mins) 
This section includes reading comprehension, identifying the correct tense form, and 
synonyms and antonyms. If you are good at grammar, you can clear this with ease. 
2. General Aptitude (30–40 mins) 
This section covers: 
• 
Representing given data in a pie chart 
• 
Pattern finding 
• 
Logical thinking 
• 
Calendar scheduling 
• 
Profit and loss problems 
• 
Percentages 
The interface showed the number of questions solved and unsolved in one corner for this 
section, but I wasn’t focusing on it. Because of this, I wasn’t sure how many questions I had 
attempted or left unanswered, and I couldn’t complete all of them within the given time. 
PS: Don’t forget to carry a calculator. 
3. Technical MCQs (15 mins) 
This section mainly focuses on SQL and Database concepts. Some questions were based on 
choosing the correct query, while others tested SQL fundamentals. If you are thorough with 
SQL basics, this part will be manageable. 
4. Coding Questions (30 mins) 
I was given two simple scenario-based questions on strings: 
• 
Finding the count of consonants 
• 
Returning a string with no vowels 
Basic coding knowledge is enough to crack this section. 
I believe the General Aptitude section carried more weightage in this round, as many of us 
performed on a similar level in the other three sections. 
From this round, 23 of us were shortlisted for the interview. 
 
3. TECHINICAL AND HR INTERVIEW : (25-30 mins) 
The interview was scheduled the next day after the announcement of shortlisted candidates. We first 
attended the PPT session, which was followed by the interview. The panel consisted of two 
interviewers, and the discussion mainly revolved around my resume. Both interviewers had a copy of 
it. 
Introduction 
They began by asking if I would like to introduce myself. Never miss this opportunity if given, because 
you can guide the interviewers about yourself. A short and crisp introduction that defines you always 
makes a strong impression. 
Projects 
They started with my projects and asked me to explain one of the projects mentioned in my resume. 
Technical Questions 
Before diving into the details, I had mentioned the following technical skills in my resume: 
1. Programming Language – Java 
2. Database Management – SQL 
3. Core Concepts – Data Structures and Algorithms 
They asked me how strong I was in SQL, and I confidently said I was pretty strong. I was then asked to 
write queries for two moderately difficult problems involving JOINS, UNIONS, and AGGREGATE 
FUNCTIONS. For the second one, I made a small syntactical mistake, which I realized only after 
handing over the paper. However, I understood that ‘what matters is not just what you write on 
paper, but how clearly you explain your approach’. My explanation greatly convinced them, and they 
moved on to test my programming knowledge. 
When asked which language I was most comfortable with, I answered Java. They gave me a problem: 
Given an array of Strings, retrieve unique strings that do not have all letters in lowercase. 
I first suggested using a Set collection, but they added that the result should be ordered. I then 
answered that I would use a List and check on each update, but they expected me to use a 
LinkedHashSet, which I wasn’t aware of at that time. On realizing this, they asked me to explain my 
approach using a List. Here, they were testing how well I knew the language, since they repeatedly 
asked me about built-in functions to support my explanation. 
Later, they asked me to explain a sorting algorithm of my choice. I chose Merge Sort and explained 
what it is, why it is used, its time and space complexities, and also drew its working process. They 
were particularly interested in checking whether I truly understood the concept or had just 
memorized it. 
With this, the technical interview came to an end. 
HR Questions 
Next, they asked me how I would handle team conflicts. I struggled a bit to put it into words but 
made sure my answer reflected honesty and my willingness to handle situations positively. I also 
related it to my experiences working in projects and clubs. 
At the end, they asked if I had any questions for them. This step is as important as your self-
introduction—it’s always good to ask thoughtful questions that show you are genuinely interested in 
the role. 
(For some panels, candidates were also asked DSA problems and puzzles.) 
The results were announced the same evening. Out of the shortlisted candidates, nine were selected 
for the internship, and I am truly grateful to be one among them. 
 
PERSONAL TIPS : 
• 
Make it a habit to practice DSA regularly. I would recommend choosing at least 1 DSA 
problem + 1 SQL problem each day, with the difficulty level adjusted to your schedule. 
Practice coding on platforms like LeetCode, HackerRank, and GeeksforGeeks. 
• 
Also practice aptitudes on platforms like IndiaBIX. 
• 
Also, be comfortable coding from scratch, since some companies may ask you to write code 
starting from the main function itself. 
• 
During interviews, maintain good body language and avoid panicking, as it creates a positive 
impression. Always stay optimistic, greet the interviewers, and acknowledge them at the 
right moments—this leaves a lasting impact. 
• 
Before your interview, go through GeeksforGeeks archives, CSEA or other placement 
experiences to get a clearer idea of what to expect. Keep in mind that not all interviewers 
will be friendly or make it easy for you; some might intentionally test how you handle 
pressure, similar to dealing with difficult clients. 
• 
Do thorough research on the company beforehand—it shows genuine interest in the role 
and will definitely work in your favor. 
• 
Be honest about everything you have mentioned in your resume. As you can see from my 
experience, my interview was almost entirely tailored to what I had listed in mine. 
• 
Most importantly, don’t lose hope if you don’t get through a particular company—the best is 
yet to come.  
• 
Avoid self-doubt. Remember, all candidates are at a similar level. Confidence and presenting 
the best version of yourself is already halfway to success.
