Aptiv Interview Experience 
(Intern+FTE) 
 
Role: Software Engineer 
Pre-requisites – C/C++ 
Online Assessment (mix of MCQ & Subjective questions around technology, 
aptitude & basics of coding)-Online 
The interview process consisted of three rounds(online) 
Technical Interview 
Techno Managerial Interview 
HR Interview 
(All the above 3 rounds are eliminatory) 
 
About the Company 
Aptiv is a global automotive technology company focused on building safer, 
greener, and more connected mobility solutions. It develops advanced software 
and hardware systems like autonomous driving tech, electrical architectures, and 
connected vehicle platforms. Headquartered in Switzerland, Aptiv operates in 
49+ countries with major tech centers including India. The company aims for zero 
accidents and zero emissions, playing a key role in shaping future mobility. 
 
140 members were shortlisted for Round1(Technical) 
Meeting links were shared to respective mails and was conducted in TEAMS. 
 
Round 1- Technical Interview (30 min) 
 
1) Self-Introduction. 
2) Tell about one of your best projects. 
3) Questions about projects. 
4) Networks-http vs https, how does DNS works, TCP and UDP and some other 
questions related to networks. 
5) DBMS-Normalization, Primary Key, Foreign Key. 
6) OS-Thread vs Process, Deadlock and its conditions, Deadlock Avoidance. 
7) Some of the git commands were asked as I mentioned in my resume. 
(eg. Fetch vs Pull) 
8) He asked about my preferred domain to work in. I said AI, since it is an 
evolving field and my projects are aligned with it. 
9) Cloud-based questions were asked as the interviewer works in the cloud 
domain. (I didn’t know all the answers, so I shared what I knew and honestly 
said that I’m not aware of some, but expressed my interest in learning more 
about them)  
10) Asked about my responsibilities as I mentioned in my resume. 
11) Any questions for him. 
 
Round 2- Techno Managerial Interview (30 min) 
(Around 45 members attended this interview) 
 
The interviewer asked about the project I am going to do in my final semester-
what algorithms I am going to use, how I approach the problem, and how I am 
going to implement it. He also asked which programming language I used to code 
and how I would rate my skills in those languages. There were some situational 
questions too, like how I would handle a team if other members were not working, 
or how I would deal with a new algorithm that no one knew. He asked about the 
IDE I use for Python and also asked about my extracurricular activities, 
responsibilities, and certifications. And asked any questions for him. 
 
In both rounds of my interview, it was fairly easy. Some panelists asked basic 
coding questions and about Aptiv protocols, along with other company-related 
topics. 
Overall, be confident in your answers, even if they are not completely correct. 
Share your thoughts honestly if you are unsure. 
 
Tip: Answer questions practically, not just theoretically. Use examples of your 
work to explain your points.  
 
Round 3- HR Interview (20 min) 
(Around 30 members attended this interview) 
The interviewer was very relaxed. He asked me to tell my college journey from 
the first year to the final year as a story. He also asked where I come from and 
the challenges I faced. He asked about my area of interest and what inspired me 
from the company’s presentation. 
Then he asked how others would describe me. I said they would call me a good 
motivator. After that, he gave me a situation: he said he got rejected by 5 
companies, has no more on-campus placements, and is feeling depressed. He 
asked to motivate him. I motivated him by saying that everything will happen at 
the right time, that one should prepare persistently and give their full effort, and 
the rest will fall into place. The discussion went around for about 5 minutes. He 
was very curious and listened to me carefully. Finally, he asked if I had any 
questions for him. 
 
25 members got selected finally including me. 
8 members from CS, 7 from IT, 10 from ECE. 
 
Key Takeaways: 
• Focus on DBMS, Computer Networks, OS (mainly), and OOPS. 
• Be thorough about everything you mention, especially the projects. 
• I followed the TakeUForward YouTube channel and regularly solved 
problems on LeetCode or GeeksforGeeks. 
• Focus on patterns, not quantity: Understand problem-solving patterns 
instead of just solving a large number of problems. 
• Research about the company and its domain before the interview. 
 
It was my second interview, and I felt really happy when I got selected! The 
interview process spanned two days, but I was lucky to complete all my rounds 
on the first day itself. Some candidates had to wait for the second round results 
until the next day. I was also the first one from the CS department to move on 
to the HR interview. The final results were announced after a week, right after 
the Pooja holidays. 
During placement drive, I noticed that many people attend multiple interviews 
and feel upset if they aren’t selected. It can be disappointing, but I believe 
whatever is meant for you will come at the right time. There’s no use in feeling 
low-talk to your friends when you’re down, and take care of your health, and go 
out to get some fresh air. With consistent preparation, the right opportunity will 
come. Everyone has talent, and you’ll get a chance to showcase yours!
