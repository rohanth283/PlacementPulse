Gokul.V – Technology Engineer Interview Experience
at Thirdwave Japan

About the Company

Thirdwave  Japan  is  a  renowned  technology  solutions  provider  focused  on  innovation  in  hardware  and

software systems. Headquartered in Tokyo, the company has a global presence and offers cutting-edge

tech solutions in embedded systems, AI, robotics, and industrial IoT. With a strong emphasis on quality,

precision,  and  continuous  improvement,  Thirdwave  collaborates  internationally  to  develop  smart

solutions aligned with Japan’s legacy of technological excellence.

About the Role

Technology  Engineer  at  Thirdwave  Japan  involves  hands-on  contributions  to  software  development,

embedded  systems,  and  R&D;  initiatives.  The  role  is  expected  to  contribute  toward  real-time  system

development, debugging, and deployment in live environments. Collaboration with Japanese engineers

is a core part of the role, promoting cross-cultural work ethics, continuous learning, and problem-solving

through advanced tools and techniques.

Interview Process

The  selection  process  was  simple  and  direct,  with  a  single  interview  round  where  4  candidates  were

interviewed together in a group setting.

The evaluation was based primarily on:

- How well we communicated our thoughts and ideas

- How confident we appeared while answering

- The level of willingness and enthusiasm we showed toward working and adapting to life in Japan

The process was interactive and focused more on personality, clarity, and passion, rather than technical

depth.

Interview Questions

The panel mainly focused on understanding:

- Self-Introduction: A crisp summary of education, key strengths, and career aspirations.

-  Internship  Experience:  Described  the  domain,  responsibilities,  tech  stack,  outcomes,  and  technical

learning.

- Major Project Explanation: Covered the problem statement, tools used, challenges, and results.

- Interest in Working in Japan: Expressed admiration for Japanese tech culture, learning work ethics, and

enthusiasm for innovation.

Interview Experience

- The atmosphere was formal but encouraging.

- Though it was a group interview, each candidate was given a fair chance.

- The panel appreciated clarity in answers, sincerity, and willingness to adapt to Japanese culture.

- No technical coding test was conducted; the focus was on communication and technical clarity.

My Preparation

1. Covered all the basic concepts like OOPs, DSA, SQL, OS, and Computer Networks.

2. Developed a structured mind map for the projects mentioned in my resume, which helped me explain

them effectively during the interview.

3. Attended mock interviews to build confidence and improve communication skills.

resume.

5.  Learned  more  about  the  company  and  gave  my  own  few  ideas  that  could  be  implemented  for  their
