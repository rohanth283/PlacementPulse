**PLACEMENT EXPERIENCE:**

**ROUND 1:(Online assessment at CUIC)**

* The assessment was conducted on the HackerEarth platform.
* It included 5 technical MCQs covering topics like OS, DBMS, OOPs, with a mix of debugging and output-based questions, along with 2 coding problems.
* **Coding Question** 1: Transform string A to B with a given cost, then B to C with another cost. Calculate the total minimum cost. UseGreedy approach or Dynamic Programming for optimal transformations.
* **Coding Question 2:** Find the number of distinct substrings in the input string. Use Hashing (HashSet) to efficiently track and count unique substrings.

**Key tip**: While solving the questions, focus on maximizing the number of passing test cases and manage your time effectively by balancing between code accuracy and speed.

**ROUND 2:(Technical interview at CUIC)**

The technical interview was held at CUIC. It began with the typical "Tell me about yourself" question, after which they focused on my key projects. They asked me to explain one project in detail, particularly the algorithm I used, and requested me to demonstrate it on paper. Be well-versed in the algorithms or tech stacks used in your projects.

Next, they inquired about my favorite subject and asked questions related to it. This was followed by a DSA question involving counting overlapping intervals for events in a calendar. I was asked to start with a brute-force approach and optimize it later. They refined the question by asking for the event with minimal overlaps and also for all non-overlapping events of that particular event.

**Key tip**: Stay clear with project details, focus on DSA practice, and ensure you listen carefully to avoid any miscommunication and time wastage during the interview.

**ROUND 3:(System design and Managerial Round at CUIC )**

In the system design portion of the interview, I was asked to design systems based on the projects listed on my resume. This required detailing the architecture, components, and technologies used for each project. Additionally, I was given a hypothetical scenario and asked to create a system design for that situation, which involved applying my knowledge of system design principles to solve the problem effectively.

The managerial questions focused on real-life scenarios, including managing team dynamics and making decisions in complex situations. I was also questioned about my experience in organizing events, including how I managed various aspects of these activities and the challenges I faced.

Finally, the resume-based questions required me to provide detailed explanations and reflections on the experiences and projects listed on my resume. This included discussing specific challenges, solutions, and the impact of my contributions.

**Key Tip**: Demonstrate confidence and assertiveness while discussing your experiences and answers.

**ROUND 4 :(HR ROUND at CUIC)**

The HR round began with questions to assess my knowledge of the company, where I was asked to share what I knew about its operations, technologies, and projects. Following this, the HR representative provided a detailed overview of the company's technologies, projects, and opportunities for growth. They then asked standard HR questions, such as why I wanted to join Trimble and where I saw myself in five years. Additionally, they explained the specifics of internship and full-time employment (FTE) opportunities. The interview concluded positively, with a clear understanding of the company’s expectations and future opportunities.

**Key Tip**: Research and understand everything about the company before the interview to demonstrate genuine interest and alignment.
