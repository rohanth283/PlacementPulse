Question 1:

Given an array of product costs, the task is to find the number of pairs
(i,j) where i < j and the sum of the costs is divisible by a given number x.

Example:

Input: x=10, costs = [3, 7, 27, 23]

Output: 4

Explanation: (3,7), (27,3), (7,23), (27,23) these 4 pairs divisible by 10

Question 2:

Given an array of regex strings containing lowercase letters and wildcard
characters ('?'), the task is to find a common pattern with the minimum
number of '?' characters. This pattern must intersect with all the given
strings, meaning the '?' characters can be replaced to form a matching
string across all patterns.

Example:

Input: n = 3 , Patterns[] = {“bug” , “gub” , “g?a”}

Output: 2

Explanation: Choose “?u?” with 2 question marks.

•  When I came to know that I had been shortlisted for the next round, I

began researching previous interview experiences on Glassdoor and GFG
archives. This gave me an idea of the areas they focus on.

•  Additionally, in the pre-placement talk, they mentioned that they would

implement the optimized one. Ensure you have a solid understanding of
