**Must Do in Coding interview**

**Think Out Loud**: Verbalize your thoughts and reasoning throughout the problem-solving process to keep the interviewer engaged and informed about your approach.

**Ask Clarifying Questions**: Before diving into the solution, ensure you fully understand the problem by asking any necessary clarifying questions. This demonstrates your attention to detail and prevents misunderstandings.

**Start with a Brute-Force Approach**: Begin by discussing the brute-force solution, as it shows that you can identify a basic starting point. Then, work towards optimizing it, if possible.

**Use First Principles Thinking**: Break down the problem into its fundamental components and analyze them. Explain your observations and thought process to the interviewer as you work through the solution.

**Keep the Interviewer Engaged**: Avoid long periods of silence. Keep the conversation flowing by sharing your thoughts, asking for feedback, and explaining each step of your approach.

**Focus on Thought Process, Not Just the Optimal Solution**: Interviewers are often more interested in how you approach the problem than whether you arrive at the most optimal solution. Demonstrate a clear and logical thought process.

**General Advice While Preparing:**

Focus on **problem-solving** rather than just memorizing patterns from SDE sheets. While SDE sheets are a valuable tool for revising concepts, the best way to truly learn data structures and algorithms (DSA) is through active problem-solving. If you have ample time, consider starting with platforms like Codeforces, where you can work on ad-hoc and greedy problems. These problems help build strong problem-solving skills that will be invaluable when tackling standard LeetCode-type questions. Above all, be consistent in your practice, regardless of immediate results. Consistency is key to long-term success.
