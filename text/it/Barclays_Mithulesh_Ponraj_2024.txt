Technical Round

Around 45 students were selected for the technical interview, and I was among them. My

interviewer is working as a Vice President. When I introduced myself, mentioning my role

as the NSS General Secretary, the interviewer seemed intrigued and asked me to share a

problem I solved in that role. After that, he shifted to technical questions and wanted me to

explain one of my projects. I chose to explain the more complex one.

The interviewer was keen to know more about my project, including the tech stack we used

and the purpose behind it. Since it was a departmental project and I only handled a part of

it, he asked about the software methodology we used. I mentioned Agile, and he probed

further about how often we held reviews and why Agile is advantageous compared to other

methods.

As we continued, he inquired about cloud management, speciﬁcally regarding the type of

storage and security groups I used in Azure, and also asked some questions about NodeJS.

Then came two SQL questions: one about joins and another about aggregation, including

some follow-ups.

Next up were coding questions,

including two about binary trees. The ﬁrst was about

checking if a binary tree is balanced, and the second was about ﬁnding the Least Common

Ancestor. For both, I had to write out the code. He also asked me to explain a question from

a contest where I had won a prize.

To wrap things up, he asked a few questions about Barclays, such as why I wanted to work

there and what my plans for higher studies were. These were the types of HR questions

which were asked.

The company conducted only one technical interview round. 14 students were ﬁnally

selected based on their performance and I was one of them.

P. S. Don’t expect simple questions. In interviews with other companies like Oracle and

BNY, I have encountered questions on topics such as multithreading in Java, dynamic

programming, recursive SQL queries and procedures, designing database schema,

system design, and operating systems (like LFU cache implementation).
