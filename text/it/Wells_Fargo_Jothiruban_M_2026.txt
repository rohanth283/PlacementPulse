Preparation Strategy:
I started my preparation on DSA only on summer intern drive and I was not consistent. I
would suggest to start as early as you can and be consistent.
Don’t wander around materials and many youtube sources. Fix onto one resource.
DSA: As everyone says Strivers Sheet.
SQL: Hackerrank SQL course (Awesome one).
Core Concepts: Your academic books (Yes, they cover in depth which is more than
enough). Last minute revision – Geeks for Geeks.
Projects: Know what you have done and why you have done. Even if you feel your
project is simple learn everything in it.
Resume: Know everything in it. Don’t add stuffs that you are not confident about. It
should showcase who you are. Add your leadership and extra-curriculars. This helped
me a lot in my interview.
Interview Tips:
Have a clear mind on the day of interview. Practise “Tell me about yourself” atleast 5
times. I did it for around 30mins and It boosted my confidence. Be cool, don’t frustate.
Result is the secondary thing, focus on delivery first.
Have a smile on your face inside the panel. If you are not sure what they are asking or
what they are expecting, make yourself clear asking “Could you be more specific
sir/ma’am? Or Do you want me to explain on this?”. Clarifying makes a good
impression.
Learn the values of the company and act accordingly and try to align yourself to it as
much as you can.
Prepare questions to ask your interviewer.
It’s all in you on what question you can get next. I drove the interview in such manner
that my answers can lead to another question that I have an idea on.
For Instance:
In my first round I was asked on, how to calculate the height of a binary tree. I wrote the
code and sent the answer in chat in python. Then he started questioning on python.
When I was asked questions on react I told him that it’s been a year I have worked on it.
So he stopped asking from react.

I told I have used triggers and implemented security features and so he asked questions
on them.
