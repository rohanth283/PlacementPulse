About The Company

American Express (Amex) is an American bank holding company and a global financial
services corporation specializing in payment cards. It leverages advanced data
analytics, cloud infrastructure, secure APIs, and machine learning to deliver
personalized experiences. A hybrid cloud environment ensures secure, seamless, and
efficient operations. Amex invests heavily in fraud prevention, regulatory compliance,
and customer engagement, making it a leader in the digital finance space.

Position: Technology Intern/Apprentice
No. of Applicants: 472
Students Selected for Interview: 16
Students Selected for Internship: 4

Out of 472 students who applied for the internship at American Express, only 16 were
shortlisted based on their performance in an Online Coding Round. I was fortunate to
be one among them. The interview process then comprised two rounds – a Technical
Round and an HR Round, both conducted online.

Online Coding Round

1.  Largest Subarray Sum with Equal Ends – You are given an array of positive

integers. Find a subarray (at least 2 elements) where the first and last elements
are the same. Among all such subarrays, return the maximum possible sum.

2.  Trash Collection Time – There are N houses on a street, each storing bags of

trash: Plastic (P), Glass (G), and Metal (M). Three trucks (one per trash type) start
from the beginning, collect their respective bags, and return. Travel between
houses takes given times, and loading each bag takes 1 minute. Find the
minimum time needed until all trucks finish.

3.  Assassin Sneak on Guarded Board – You have an N×M board. Each cell may be:
empty ., obstacle X, a guard facing <, >, ^, or v (seeing straight until blocked), or
an assassin A. The assassin can move up, down, left, or right on empty cells.
Determine if the assassin can reach the bottom-right cell without being seen by
any guard.

HR Round

The HR round was conducted by a Director of Software Development at American
Express. The environment was friendly, and the questions were mostly resume-based
and situational.

Key HR Questions:

•  Explanation of my Machine Learning project (Hate Speech Detection) and

justification for the chosen model.

•  Why I used Bootstrap in my e-commerce frontend project and how I made the

website responsive.

•  Questions related to my awards and justification of soft skills like leadership,

time management, and team management.

Situational Questions:

1.  If you are on the way to catch a flight for an interview, but due to traffic, you can

either break rules and reach on time or miss the flight, what will you do?

2.  In a team project, if there is a conflict where two innovative solutions are
presented, both equally good, which solution will you choose and why?

Personal Questions:

•  Asked about my 12th-grade scores and whether I regretted losing marks.

•  Resume-oriented discussions on achievements, skills, and experiences.

Tips for Students

I started my internship preparation during my 4th semester holidays, but I strongly
recommend you to start right from the 4th semester itself. Early preparation helps you
build consistency and reduces stress later.

Here are some key takeaways from my experience that might help future aspirants:

1.  Strengthen Your Fundamentals – OOP, DSA, DBMS, and core programming
concepts are always tested. Don’t just memorize; understand them deeply.

2.  Follow a Structured Plan + Practice Consistently – I used Striver’s A–Z DSA

Sheet for a systematic approach and solved problems regularly on LeetCode to
get hands-on practice.

3.  Solve at Least One Problem Daily – Consistency matters. Even one problem

every day improves your problem-solving skills over time.

4.  Be Comfortable Explaining Your Code – Interviews often involve live coding.

Practice explaining your thought process while solving problems.

5.  Know Your Resume Inside-Out – Projects, skills, and achievements you
mention will definitely be asked. Be prepared to justify your choices.

6.  Prepare for Project-Related Questions – Even if you did the project yourself,
you should be ready to answer any question about it. Anticipate questions and
prepare in advance.

7.  Work on Soft Skills – Communication, teamwork, and decision-making are

equally important in HR rounds.

8.  Prepare for Situational Questions – Think logically and ethically when

answering real-life scenarios.

9.  Ask Good Questions – Show curiosity about the company’s work culture and

values. It leaves a strong impression.

10. Stay Calm and Confident – Even if you don’t know an answer, explain your

approach. Confidence matters as much as correctness.

11. Learn to Question – As the HR said, questioning isn’t negativity; it’s about clarity,

ownership, and professional growth.

12. Take Every Interview as a Learning Opportunity – Whether selected or not,

each experience adds value to your journey.
