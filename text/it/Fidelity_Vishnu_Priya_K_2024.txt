Internship Experience at Fidelity Investments

                                                                                                              -By Vishnu Priya K (IT)

About the Company:

Fidelity is one of the leading financial investment companies in the U.S., known
for promoting a perfect work-life balance. It fosters an environment where
there's ample opportunity to learn and explore, with innovation and interest
being key drivers for success. The team is highly supportive and inclusive.

About the Role:

I was assigned as a Data Engineer, where I worked with a tech stack that
included Python, Snowflake, Oracle DB, PowerApps, and Git. I focused on
mastering databases, data warehousing, and ETL processes in this role. I
regularly applied Python and SQL, gained hands-on experience with cloud
service platforms, and worked on real-world projects involving end-to-end data
pipelines, significantly enhancing my technical skills.

Preparation Strategy:

To prepare, I focused on solving competitive programming questions on
platforms like LeetCode and HackerRank, and worked through Striver's DSA
sheet to strengthen my understanding of Data Structures and Algorithms. I also
dedicated time to mastering DBMS concepts (Geek For Geeks) and practicing
SQL. Additionally, I practiced logical reasoning to improve puzzle-solving and
problem-solving skills. I referred to the intern preparation document provided
by IT department and ACM.

Internship Drive Experience:

Round 1:

Coding and MCQ Assessment (HirePro Platform): This round included multiple-
choice questions related to Java, Computer Networks, APIs, DSA, and Logical
Reasoning. Additionally, there were two DSA coding questions: one medium-
difficulty question and another hard-level question focused on Tree data
structures.

Outcome: Approximately 20-25 students were shortlisted for the next round.

Round 2:

Technical Round (40-45 mins): This round involved in-depth technical
questioning, primarily focusing on the candidate's understanding of core
computer science concepts and problem-solving abilities.

Outcome: Around 10-12 students advanced to the final round.

Round 3:

Technical + HR Round (20-25 mins): This combined round evaluated both
technical skills and cultural fit. The discussion also included HR-related topics.

Outcome: 6 students received internship offers.

My interview rounds covered a range of topics, including:

▪  OS, Computer Architecture, and Compiler Engineering concepts
▪  SQL queries
▪  Tree and Heap Data Structures-related coding questions
▪  Discussions on Machine Learning models and concepts
▪  Concepts and implementation logic involved in my project work

Tips and Tricks:

▪  Develop confident communication skills; clear articulation of your

thoughts can make a significant difference.

▪  Focus on understanding core concepts rather than just memorizing

them.

▪  Practice coding regularly to improve problem-solving speed.
▪  Be familiar with the company's tech stack and prepare accordingly.
▪  Review your projects thoroughly, as in-depth questions might be asked.
