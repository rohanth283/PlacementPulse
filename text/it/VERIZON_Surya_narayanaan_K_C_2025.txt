Placement experience – VERIZON

-  Surya narayanaan KC

About the company:

Verizon  Communications  is  a  prominent  telecommunications  company  and  a  leader  in
technology and network solutions, consistently ranked among the Fortune 500. Established at
the turn of the century, Verizon offers a range of services including wireless, broadband, and
5G connectivity, catering to both businesses and consumers globally. Verizon India has offices
in major cities like Chennai, Bangalore, and Hyderabad, where it plays a crucial role in driving
technological advancements and supporting Verizon's global operations.

About the Role:

The position they were hiring for was Engineer 1 (Software Development). According to the
HR, the role involves working with various technologies and across multiple domains. It’s a
dynamic position that provides exposure to a broad range of technical areas.

Placement Process

Round 1: Online Assessment

The online assessment was divided into three parts. The first part required us to write a 200-
word paragraph on a general topic. The second part was a coding round, which included two
easy-to-moderate level questions. I received one array question and one string-based problem.
The third part tested our vocabulary and grammar skills, ensuring we had a strong grasp of
language proficiency.

Round 2: Group Discussion:

In the group discussion round, we were divided into groups of 13. The panellist gave us a
general topic to discuss, while other panels were assigned specific topics, such as Generative
AI. The person leading my panel was very approachable, allowing everyone sufficient time to
speak. I spoke twice during the discussion.

It’s important to remain confident and speak clearly. Listening to others is equally crucial, and
interruptions should be avoided. Be respectful of others' opinions while expressing your own.
If you’re unsure about the topic, stay calm and contribute thoughtful points.

Round 3: Technical Interview

The technical interview started with the interviewer reviewing my resume and asking about my
extracurricular activities. To my surprise, he was familiar with a school near mine and asked
me about my involvement in the AUSEC club, my role, and my achievements during school.
Afterward, he inquired about my projects in detail. It’s essential to have a solid understanding
of the tech stack you’ve used and to be able to explain it in-depth.

The interviewer then posed scenario-based questions related to networking. He asked about IP
addresses and DNS servers. Additionally, he gave me a scenario to modify a database according
to specific requirements. Throughout the interview, the interviewer was very patient, giving me
time to recall the topics and providing helpful hints when needed.

Round 4: HR Interview

The  HR  interview  was  relatively  easy.  The  HR  representative  asked  about  my  location
preferences, my  family  background, and what  I knew about  the company. It was more of a
casual conversation to understand my background and motivations.

Preparation Method I Followed

1.  I  focused  on  practicing  problems  on  platforms  like  LeetCode  and  GeeksforGeeks

(GFG) to improve my problem-solving skills

2.  For DSA, I referred to Striver’s A2Z sheet. It provided a clear roadmap for mastering

the essential concepts and algorithms.

3.  I made sure to equally concentrate on core subjects like DBMS, OOPs, and Networking.
These  are  crucial  for  technical  interviews,  and  a  solid  understanding  of  these  topics
helped me perform well in scenario-based questions and interviews.

Tips for the Interview:

i.  Remember, the interviewer knows that you're a fresher, so don't panic. Stay calm and

ii.

approach the interview with a positive mindset.
Instead of treating the interview like a strict Q&A session, try to have a casual, friendly
conversation. It helps in building rapport and makes the interview more comfortable.

iii.  Be well-versed with everything on your resume. Most of the questions will likely stem
from your past experiences, projects, and extracurricular activities, so be prepared to

iv.  Confidence is key! Trust in your preparation, stay confident while answering, and don't
