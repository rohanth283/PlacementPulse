About the Company – Aptiv

Aptiv is a global technology company focused on making vehicles safer, greener,
and more connected. It operates primarily in the automotive and mobility domain,
developing advanced solutions for modern and future vehicles.

Aptiv operates across two major business segments:

  Advanced Safety and User Experience: Advanced Driver Assistance Systems

(ADAS), autonomous driving support, and in-vehicle infotainment and
connectivity solutions.

  Smart Vehicle Architecture and Electrical Systems: wiring systems, high-

voltage and low-voltage architectures, and vehicle connectivity platforms.

The company plays a key role in enabling next-generation, software-defined vehicles
by combining software, hardware, and data-driven technologies. Aptiv works closely
with leading OEMs (Original Equipment Manufacturers) worldwide to design
scalable, reliable, and future-ready vehicle platforms.

Aptiv has a strong global presence, operating in nearly 50 countries with
manufacturing and engineering centers across North America, Europe, Asia, and
South America, including India. The company employs around 190,000 professionals
globally, with a significant focus on engineering, innovation, and research.

Preparation Strategy for Aptiv

Coding:
• Focus on basic coding questions rather than very complex problems. Practice
consistently on platforms like LeetCode and HackerRank. Prepare bit manipulation
topics.

Core CS Fundamentals:
• Have a strong conceptual understanding of OS, OOPS, DBMS, and CN.
Interviewers mainly test concept clarity, not definitions.

Resume:
• Keep the resume simple, honest, and relevant to the role.
• Be prepared to explain everything mentioned in the resume, especially projects.

Round 1: Online Coding Assessment (1 hour 30 minutes)

The first round was an online coding assessment conducted on the test platform.

Assessment Pattern

  20 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

  1 Coding Question

The MCQs mainly tested core programming fundamentals, including:

  C / C++ basics

  Data structures

  Logical reasoning

  Basic problem-solving concepts

Coding Question

  The score of an array is defined as the number of elements whose value is

equal to their 1-based index.

  Given an array of integers, we are allowed to delete any element any number
of times (as long as the array length is greater than 1), without changing the
order of remaining elements.

  The goal was to determine the maximum possible score that can be achieved

after performing optimal deletions.

Round 2: Technical Interview (30–40 minutes)

The interviewer began by giving a brief self-introduction and asked me to introduce
myself as well. He asked about my favorite programming language, to which I replied
C++. Then, he started asking questions related to C programming concepts.

The questions covered (as far as i remember)

  Storage classes in C
  Pointer-related concepts

He then asked me to rate my coding skills out of 5, followed by a few coding
questions.

After that, I was asked to explain my projects in detail. The interviewer patiently
listened to my explanations and waited until I completed each answer without

interrupting. Toward the end, I was given time to ask my questions, and the round
concluded smoothly.

Round 3: Techno-Managerial Interview (60 minutes)

The interviewer introduced himself and mentioned that he has 18 years of industry
experience. I then gave my self-introduction.

He asked me to show proof of my project work, such as a project video or report. I
shared my GitHub account and showed the video demo, report of my project
available there.

Most of the discussion revolved around my projects He presented a problem
statement and asked how I would approach it. The interview was very interactive, he
kept asking “what if” scenarios and followed up on my answers to understand my
thinking process and decision-making ability.

He also asked a logical puzzle:

There are 3 switches on the ground floor and 1 bulb on the first floor. How will you
identify which switch controls the bulb in just one attempt?

Later, he asked:

  What I know about Aptiv

I answered his question, also mentioned the previous interviewer’s role, his role, and
explained how my projects align with this role. He asked about my location
preferences as well.

The interview was very engaging and conversational. He listened attentively,
appreciated my answers, and even praised my approach. I asked a few questions at
the end, and the round concluded on a positive note.

Round 4: HR Interview (20–30 minutes)

The HR interview was conducted by a Senior HR. This round was non-technical.
After introductions, the questions were mostly based on my responses.

The HR asked about:

  My family background
  What I look for when choosing a problem statement
  How I approach and solve problems
  What are the two skills you would develop before joining Aptiv, and why?

Although the interviewer maintained a serious expression, I answered confidently
and with a smile. At last, she asked about my location preference.

The final results were announced after one week.

Preparation Resources

DSA:
LeetCode Top 150 Questions (Easy and Medium) and Striver’s SDE Sheet for
building strong problem-solving skills and coding fundamentals.

SQL:
LeetCode Top 50 SQL Questions to practice queries and understand database
concepts.

Core Subjects:
Platforms like InterviewBit and GeeksforGeeks to prepare OOPS,OS,DBMS,CN, with
a focus on concept clarity.

Be confident in whatever you are answering, relate your projects to the company and
the role, and be honest throughout the interview. Make sure you are aware of and
can confidently explain every word mentioned in your resume, as most questions will
be based on your answers and the content of your resume.

If you do not get selected, it does not mean you didn’t work hard or give your best.
Focus on doing your duty by attending the placement process confidently and
sincerely, without being attached to the result.
