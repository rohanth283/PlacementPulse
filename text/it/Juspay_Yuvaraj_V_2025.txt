Yuvaraj V - Off-Campus Interview Experience

About Juspay

Juspay is a Indian-based fintech established startup focused on providing seamless
and  secure  payment  experiences.  It  powers  millions  of  transactions  daily  with  its
robust payment solutions used by top players in the Indian digital economy.

How I Applied

I  got  the  recruiting  information  through  LinkedIn  and  applied  via  Juspay’s  career
site.

Reason to switch:
Job dissatisfaction during my 6-month internship period at Athenahealth.

Round 1: HR Telephone Confirmation

The HR called to inform me that I was shortlisted and asked whether I was open to
proceeding, since I was currently interning at Athenahealth. I confirmed my interest
and provided additional details such as education background, company name, and
stipend. She said I’d receive a link for the online assessment test (Round 2).

Round 2: Online Assessment - 1 (MCQ, 45 mins)

Consisted  of  15  MCQs  from  CS  fundamentals  (OOPs,  DBMS,  DSA,  OS),  and
surprisingly, some basic +2 level Physics questions too.

I cleared this round and got a call the next day for the next DSA round.

Round 3: Online Assessment - 2 (DSA, 1h 30min)

Three DSA questions:

1.  Easy: Prefix sum-based problem – solved completely.

2.  Medium:  Tree  (non-BST)  problem  related  to  office  hierarchy  –  partially

solved (some TLE).

3.  Hard: Couldn’t complete – only half the cases passed.

Despite the tough round, I got selected for the next stage.

Round 4: Technical Interview - 1 (DSA Focused, 1h)

Conducted via Google Meet.

  1st  Problem: Rainwater trapping  –  I  already knew  the  approach,  so it  went

very well.

  2nd  Problem:  Matrix  distance  calculation  among  same  numbers  –  started
with brute force, then optimized it using a derived equation. Interviewer was
impressed, but due to network issues, we couldn’t continue.

Later, I received a call saying I was selected for the next round.

Round 5: Hackathon - Part A (3h)

A 3-hour online DSA round.

Only one problem related to M-ary Tree – a very common one in Juspay interviews. I
was well-prepared and solved it.

Got a call after a week that I qualified for Part B.

Round 6: Hackathon - Part B (8h)

A full-day 1-on-1 hackathon.

A  company  buddy  was  assigned  to  assist.  They  asked  questions  based  on  my
previous  M-ary  tree  solution  and  also  pushed  me  to  implement  concurrency  and
threading  concepts.  Though  syntactically  relaxed,  they  expected  high-level
knowledge.

Round 7: System Design Interview (Turning Point)

This was the toughest round.

Questions asked:

  System design scenario:

In your real-time IPL auction project, how would you ensure minimal latency
if 1 user from the US and 1 from India are playing?
(I mentioned CDN and related solutions, but he expected deeper, real-time
focused strategies.)

  DSA Problem:

"Assume you have an array but no built-in way to get its length. How will you
find it?"
(I gave the binary search for infinite array approach – he was satisfied.)

  Theoretical questions:

o  What is a clock cycle? (Expected a basic, intuitive answer – I couldn’t

answer well.)

o  How does a compiler work?

o  Describe  the  entire  data  transmission  process  from  one  network  to

another (I struggled here).

  Puzzle:

21 sticks puzzle – I solved it correctly.

  DSA Theoretical:

o  Queue vs Stack

o  How Priority Queue is implemented

  Other

Asked about AI/ML project experience.

Topics:

Despite  some  good  points,  I  couldn’t  impress  him  in  many  areas.  He  seemed  very
knowledgeable in multiple domains.

Role Change & Compensation Difference

Because of my average performance in this round, though I originally applied for an
SDE  role,  they  offered  me  the  Product  Engineer  -  1  (PE-1)  role  considering  my
problem-solving skills.

After  discussing  with  a  few  seniors,  I  came  to  know  that  the  primary  distinction
between the two roles is compensation:

  SDE Role: ₹27 LPA CTC with ~₹17 LPA base

  Product Engineer Role: ₹21 LPA CTC with ~₹14 LPA base

Both roles are quite similar in responsibilities and learning opportunities.

Final Round: HR Discussion

A phone call where they confirmed:

  Relocation readiness

  Role clarification

  Notice period details



Joining date

Finally, they confirmed my offer for the Product Engineer - I role.

Such long and intense processes are only followed by a few companies like Juspay.
Don’t worry guys, on-campus processes are usually simpler and shorter.
