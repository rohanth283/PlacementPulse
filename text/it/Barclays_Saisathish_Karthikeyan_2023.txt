ABOUT THE COMPANY

London.  With  a  history  dating  back  over  325  years,  Barclays  operates  in  more  than  40  countries,
offering products and services across personal, corporate, and investment banking. The company is
committed  to  helping  customers  and  clients  achieve  their  financial  goals  through  innovation  and  a
focus on responsible banking.

Barclays  in  India:  Barclays  has  established  a  strong  footprint  in  India,  where  it  operates  through
various  business  units.  The  bank  provides  services  in  areas  such  as  investment  banking,  where  it
advises companies on mergers, acquisitions, and capital raising; corporate banking, offering tailored
financial  solutions  to  large  corporations;  and  wealth  management,  catering  to  high-net-worth
individuals with bespoke investment strategies.

Barclays  Global  Service  Centre  (GSC):  Barclays  GSC  is  an  integral  part  of  Barclays’  global
operations, providing critical services that support the bank’s business worldwide. Barclays GSC is a
network of centres that support the bank's operations worldwide, with a focus on delivering technology
and  operational  services.  These  centres  are  critical  to  Barclays’  ability  to  operate  efficiently  and
innovate in the competitive financial services industry.

At Barclays GSC Chennai, where I work, the focus is on delivering technology-driven solutions and
operational support that are crucial to Barclays’ global business operations. The centre specializes in
areas  like  data  engineering,  software  development,  and  operational  risk  management.  Teams  here
collaborate with global counterparts to ensure the smooth functioning of Barclays’ operations across
various regions, playing a pivotal role in the bank's success.

ABOUT THE ROLE

Internship Role: During my internship at Barclays GSC Chennai, I worked as a UI Developer. My
primary responsibility was to develop a documentation portal intended for internal use by other team
members.  The  team  I  was  part  of  specializes  in  creating  UI  libraries  for  other  departments  within
Barclays.  The  portal  we  developed  served  as  a  comprehensive  documentation  resource  for  these
libraries.

The project involved using front-end technologies such as React.js and HTML to create a dynamic and
user-friendly  interface.  Additionally,  we  utilized  a  tool  called  Docusaurus,  which  automates  the
creation of documentation websites by generating content from codebase documentation. This was not
just a static website; it dynamically pulled and displayed documentation, making it easier for users to
access and understand the UI components. I collaborated closely with another intern on this project,
and we successfully delivered a functional and well-documented portal over the course of our two-
month internship.

PREPARATION STRATEGY

To prepare for the placement/intern process, I focused on strengthening my problem-solving skills and
revisiting  key  technical  concepts.  I  spent  time  practicing  coding  problems  on  HackerRank,  which
helped me brush up on essential DSA topics. Additionally, I reviewed the basics of DBMS, SDLC,
computer networks as well as all the other core subjects. These areas were crucial for the online test
round.

The online test round included two coding questions of easy to medium difficulty, mainly focused on
basic DSA concepts, and several multiple-choice questions (MCQs) covering various topics such as
DBMS, networks, and SDLC. Preparing for these topics allowed me to approach the test confidently.

Once  I  cleared  the  online  test,  I  intensified  my  preparation  by  revisiting  core  subjects,  including
DBMS, SDLC, and DSA, as well as other fundamental topics. This thorough review was essential for
the next stage of the selection process—the interview.

TIPS AND TRICKS

1.  Strengthen Core Subjects and Fundamentals

Revisiting and mastering the fundamentals of your core subjects is crucial. Whether it’s DBMS,
Operating Systems, or Networks, having a solid understanding of these topics will set you up for
success, especially during technical interviews.
2.  Focus on Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA)

DSA concepts are instrumental in clearing the first round—typically the online test—of most
placement processes. This round is often the most challenging, so regular practice of DSA
problems is essential. Use platforms like HackerRank or LeetCode to hone your skills.

3.  Build a Strong Portfolio with Project Demos

A well-rounded portfolio with diverse projects is a great way to showcase your skills. Whenever
possible, have demos ready for your projects, as this can significantly enhance your profile
during interviews. Working on various projects across different tech stacks not only broadens
your knowledge but also boosts your confidence.

4.  Craft a Simple and Clear Resume

Your resume should be concise and focused, ideally fitting on a single page. It should clearly
outline your skill set, projects, experience, and education. Don’t forget to include any
certifications, technical events, or competitions you’ve participated in.

5.  Participate in Technical Events and Hackathons

Engaging in intra- and inter-college technical events, as well as hackathons, is highly beneficial.
These experiences not only help you learn new concepts but also enhance your resume. Plus, they
provide valuable experience working under pressure and in team settings.

6.  Be Honest and Confident in Interviews

During interviews, confidence is key. Speak clearly and be honest about what you know. If you
don’t know the answer to a question, it’s better to admit it than to try and bluff your way through.
Authenticity goes a long way in making a positive impression.

7.  Research the Company

Before any interview or even the online test round, take the time to research the company. You’ll
likely be asked why you want to join the company, so being well-prepared with knowledge about
their mission, values, and recent projects will help you answer confidently and convincingly.
