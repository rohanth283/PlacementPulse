About Tekion

Tekion  Corp  is  a  cloud  technology  company  founded  in  2016  by  former  Tesla  CIO  Jay
Vijayan.  The  company  is  headquartered  in  Pleasanton,  California,  with  a major technology
center in Chennai, India.

The  platform,  Automotive  Retail  Cloud  (ARC),  is  an  end-to-end,  cloud-native  solution
designed for automotive dealerships. ARC integrates multiple modules such as CRM, DMS,
digital  retail,  service  management,  analytics,  and  AI-powered  insights.  Tekion  also  offers
Automotive  Enterprise Cloud (AEC), which connects OEMs and dealerships through digital
tools like configurators, online payments, and advanced analytics.

My Interview Process and Experience

The  Tekion  recruitment  journey  was  a  well-structured  process  spread  across  multiple  rounds,  each
designed to evaluate not just my technical skills but also my ability to think critically, solve problems
efficiently, and stay composed under pressure.

Round 1: Online Coding Round

●  10 MCQs covering DSA, DBMS, and Operating Systems.
●  1 SQL query to test database knowledge.
●  1 medium-level DSA coding problem.

From this round, I was shortlisted for the next stage.

INTERVIEW DAY

The  big  day  started  with  a  surprise—two  consecutive  coding  rounds that turned out to be
elimination  stages.  These  rounds pushed us to prove our skills in both frontend design and
backend logic, highlighting our ability to think fast and stay focused.

Round 2: Coding Round - 1 at CUIC

This was the first round on the interview day. It had three parts:

●  A frontend-based task.
●  One medium-level DSA coding question.
●  One hard-level DSA problem.

Around 20 candidates (including me) were shortlisted from this stage.

Round 3: Coding Round - 2 at CUIC

This round tested my ability to write efficient code and reduce time complexity. I solved:

●  A string-based DSA problem. Similar to leetcode problem:
https://leetcode.com/problems/word-break/description/

●  Another problem where my first solution used arrays, but I realized it was

inefficient. To optimize, I restructured my logic using a queue, which reduced the
overall time complexity and made the solution much faster.

From here, 10 candidates moved ahead, and I was one of them.

Round 4: Technical Interview

The technical round was conducted by two interviewers and focused on testing both my core
fundamentals as well as the depth of my project knowledge.

●  Frontend related questions.
●  Code snippet analysis – explain the output and logic.
●  C++ and Java concepts-based questions.
●  Projects  mentioned  in  my  resume  –  with  focus  on  APIs,  tech  stack,  and

implementation.

●  Questions about my certifications and learnings.

This  stage  was  very  interactive  and  practical.  Out  of  10,  5  candidates  including  me were
shortlisted for the final HR discussion.

Round 5: HR Interview

The  HR  discussion  was  more  about  my  personality,  career  goals,  and  motivation  to  join
Tekion. Questions included:

●  Self-introduction.
●  What do you know about Tekion?
●  Why do you want to join Tekion?
●  Behavioral questions like:

○  Importance of mentorship.
○  My perspective on teamwork and team spirit.

At  the  end  of  the  day,  the  selected  candidates  were  announced.  I  was  among  them  and
received the offer.

I received:

●  A 6-month internship.
●  Conversion to Full-Time Employment (FTE) upon successful completion.

Key Suggestions and Tips from My Experience

Preparation

●  Don’t lose hope – be confident in what you know.
●  Be the best in the areas you’re strong at and highlight them.
●  Practice LeetCode problems regularly.
●  Revise last-minute concepts from GeeksforGeeks and other online resources for a

quick brush-up.

●  Balance coding with subject knowledge – parallel preparation in core subjects like

DBMS, OS, and Computer Networks is equally important.

Interview Strategy

●  Be  very  clear  about  everything  you  include  in  your  resume  –  expect  questions  on

each point.

●  Answer in crisp, concise words.
●  Using the right technical keywords is more impactful than long explanations.
●  Stay confident and structured in your responses.
