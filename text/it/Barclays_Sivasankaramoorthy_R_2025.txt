My preparations and Key insights:

•

•

I started my coding preparation during my 4th semester holidays.
While it felt a bit late, I reminded myself that it's never too late to
begin, and I focused on working through data structures and algorithms
(DSA) while recalling everything before my interview.
It's essential to have a solid understanding of the company you're
interviewing with. For instance, Barclays has values encapsulated in
the acronym RISES, and they expect candidates to align with these
values.

•  Being truthful about your resume is crucial. Highlight only the most
relevant experiences and skills, and approach the interview with
confidence, as if you truly believe you are the right fit for the position.
I utilized ACM CODE documents for my preparation and participated
in numerous coding competitions, which I found incredibly beneficial.
I highly recommend engaging in all kinds of technical events, as they
provide valuable experience.

•

•  Don’t hesitate to ask questions if you’re unsure about something. This
shows your eagerness to understand and learn, which can positively
attract the interviewer’s attention.

•  When answering HR-related questions, ensure that you provide the

best possible solutions. Take your time to formulate your responses .
•  Develop your communication skills. Even if you feel nervous and start
to ramble, make sure to convey enough information for the interviewer
to understand your thoughts clearly. Being articulate can leave a
lasting impression.

•  Ultimately, my honesty regarding my resume and the skills I

demonstrated during the interview played a significant role in securing
my internship.
