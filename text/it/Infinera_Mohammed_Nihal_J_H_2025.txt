              Infinera India Placement Process

About the Company: Infinera India, located in Bangalore, is a key research and
development center for Infinera Corporation, a global leader in intelligent
transport networks. The Bangalore center focuses on designing and developing
innovative networking solutions that enable service providers, cloud operators,
and data center operators to optimize their networks with scalability, efficiency,
and performance. Leveraging advanced technologies in optical networking and
software-defined networking (SDN), Infinera India plays a pivotal role in
enhancing global communication infrastructure.

About the Role: The role offered at Infinera India is that of a Software
Developer. This role involves working on cutting-edge technologies related to
optical networks and network management software. Software Developers at
Infinera are expected to design, develop, and maintain software solutions that
enhance network performance, scalability, and efficiency. This involves working
with cross-functional teams to deliver high-quality, robust software solutions
that support the company’s mission of enabling next-generation networking.

Overview of the Process: The placement process for Infinera India consists of
the following stages:

1.  Online Test: Conducted on the DoSelect platform, featuring around 40

questions that assess aptitude , technical
mcqs(oops,os,dbms,networking) and one coding question(easy level).

2.  Interview Rounds:

o  Technical Round 1: Focuses more on OOPs(20-25

questions),Os(around 8 questions),Networks(around 5
questions),linkedlist Implementation in C language and Socket
Programming in Java

o  Technical Round 2: Technical Explanation of the Project(any one

they would pick) ,OS(checked in depth understaning),DBMS(as it is
being used in my project) and 2 dsa question(1 medium and 1
hard )

o  HR Round: Evaluates communication skills, cultural fit, and

alignment with the company's values and objectives.

Interview Round 1: [1:15hrs]

In the first technical interview round, I was extensively questioned on Object-Oriented
Programming (OOP) concepts, where I encountered around 20 questions focusing on
different aspects of OOP.

Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) Questions

1.  What are the four pillars of Object-Oriented Programming? Explain each with

examples.

2.  What is the difference between inheritance and polymorphism?

3.  Can you explain the concept of encapsulation and why it is important in OOP?

4.  What is the difference between method overloading and method overriding?

5.  What is an abstract class? How is it different from an interface?

6.  Explain the concept of constructor and destructor in OOP.

7.  What is multiple inheritance? Is it supported in Java or C++? Why or why not?

8.  How does exception handling work in OOP? Provide a brief example.

9.  What is a singleton class, and how can you implement it in a language like Java or

C++?

10. What is the difference between a shallow copy and a deep copy in OOP?

Operating Systems (OS) Questions

1.  What is process scheduling, and what are different types of scheduling algorithms?

2.  Explain the concept of deadlock. How can it be prevented or avoided?

Networking Questions

1.  What is the difference between TCP and UDP? Provide use cases of it.

2.  Explain the OSI model and the functions of each layer.

Interview Round 2: [1-1:15 hrs]

In the second technical interview round, the focus was on providing a deep technical
explanation of one of my projects. The interviewer picked a specific project from my resume
and asked for a detailed discussion on the technical aspects, including the design,
architecture, technologies used, challenges faced.

1.  Operating Systems (OS): The interviewer checked my in-depth understanding of OS

concepts, such as:

o  Process Management: Differences between threads and processes, inter-

process communication, and synchronization mechanisms like semaphores
and mutexes.

o  Memory Management: Paging, segmentation, virtual memory.

2.  Database Management Systems (DBMS): Since DBMS was relevant to my project,

questions were focused on:

o  Normalization and Denormalization: Concepts, advantages, and scenarios for

use.

o  SQL Queries: Writing queries involving JOINs, subqueries.

3.  Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA): Two DSA questions were asked, one medium

and one hard, testing problem-solving skills:

o  Medium Question: Finding the nth element from the last in the linkedlist

(only one traversal can be done).

o  Hard Question: ( Longest Common Increasing Subsequence between two

strings)

It was a new one , Haven’t been encountered it in any platform,

Struggled to implement it .

Interview Round 3:[20-25mins]

The third interview round was an HR round where the focus was on understanding my
knowledge about the company, my alignment with its values, and insights from the PPT
presentation provided during the recruitment process. Some of the key questions asked
included:

1.  What do you know about Infinera and its core values? How do they align with your

career goals?

2.  Why do you want to join Infinera India, and how do you see yourself contributing to

the company?

3.  Can you discuss a few key takeaways from the PPT presentation provided during the

recruitment process?

4.  Describe a challenging situation you faced in a project or internship and how you

handled it.
