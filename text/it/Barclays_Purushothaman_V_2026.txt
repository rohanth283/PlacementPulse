Selection Process

The recruitment had three main rounds:

1. Online Assessment

  Total 25 questions.
  20 MCQs:

o  Topics included C++, Java, OOPs concepts, DBMS, and output prediction

from code snippets.

  5 SQL questions:

o  Required writing queries (joins, subqueries, etc.).

This round tested both programming fundamentals and database knowledge.

2. Technical Interview (20–25 minutes)

The panel started with an introduction and project discussion.

Questions I faced:

  Tell me about yourself.
  Explain your projects.
  Why did you choose the tech stack for your project?
  Project-related follow-up questions.
  Write two SQL queries on paper:

o  One using JOIN
o  One using a SUBQUERY

 (I was able to answer both).

  Do you know Python? (I said I’m at an intermediate level, and they asked a few basic

Python questions).

3. HR Interview (taken by another panel member, lasted ~30 minutes)

The HR was also technical in nature, but with additional general questions.

Questions I faced:

  Tell me about yourself again.
  More questions about my project.
  Questions about  GitHub .
  Given a bar chart of developer workloads, I was asked to write Python code .
  General HR questions like:

o  What do you do in your free time?
o  Difference between ChatGPT and DeepSeek.
o  Do you use AI? Do you think it is useful?

Preparation Tips (From My Experience)

1.  DSA & Programming Basics

                          a. DSA Preparation: Follow Striver's DSA sheet for DSA Preparation

                           b.Revise OOPs ,OS,DBMS fundamentals.

2.  SQL Practice

a.  Be very comfortable with JOIN, GROUP BY, SUBQUERIES.
b.  Practice writing queries .

3.  Projects

a.  Know your project inside-out.
b.  Be ready to explain why you chose your tech stack and what could be

improved.

4.  Python & AI Awareness

a.  If you mention Python/ML in your resume, expect practical questions.
b.  Stay updated on AI tools and current tech trends.

5.  HR Prep

a.  Prepare a clear “Tell me about yourself”.
b.  Be ready for general questions about hobbies, teamwork, and using AI tools.
c.  Answer confidently, even if you don’t know everything.

Final Note

The interviewers were very friendly and made the whole process stress-free. My biggest
takeaway is that Barclays focuses not only on technical skills but also on how you
explain your projects and adapt to new technologies like Python and AI.
