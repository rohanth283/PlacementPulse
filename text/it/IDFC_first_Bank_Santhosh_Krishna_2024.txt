I had a very standard strategy for placements. Some of them include:

* Revising resume projects and skills, etc.
* Strong fundamentals
* Practicing important DSA questions
* Covering frequently asked questions in interviews in all major topics

In my opinion, this will help clear any regular interviews.

**Placement drive experience:**

The online test was comparatively easy. We had 10 MCQs and two coding questions (one easy and another medium).

The interview was held in two rounds. The first round was pretty simple, they asked about our interests, favorite subject, internship experience, and achievements.

Most of us were short-listed for the second round. This was also a simple round. They asked us about the same but with some fundamental questions on programming.

**Tips and tricks:**

* Be honest and straight-forward
* Have clear understanding of yourself (strengths, weakness and interests)
* Be mindful of your answers during the interview
* Speak clearly and slowly. The interviewer should be able to hear what you’re trying to say, and that is more important that what you are saying.
* Take your time when required. You’re not assessed based on how fast you can answer.
