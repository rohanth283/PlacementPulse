About the Role: They came for 16 job roles.

Overview of the Process: The selection process for this included:

1.  Technical Interview Round 1 (Coding and Problem-Solving)
2.  Technical and HR Interview Round 2 (Project-Based and Behavioral Questions)

Interview Round 1 (Technical): This round was fully technical, testing coding and
problem-solving abilities. Here are some key aspects:

●  DSA Questions: The interview focused on data structure and algorithm questions that
required coding in Python and C++. The problems covered essential DSA topics and
tested my ability to implement efficient solutions.

●  Aptitude Questions: There were questions related to profit and loss, which tested my

analytical and problem-solving skills.

●  Project Discussion: I was asked about the projects on my resume, where I explained

the tech stacks used and the project scope.

Tips for Technical Round 1: Focus on mastering data structures and algorithms and practice
implementing solutions in multiple programming languages (Python and C++). Also, be
prepared to explain your projects in detail, particularly in terms of technical challenges and key
learning outcomes.

Interview Round 2 (Technical and HR): The second round was a combination of technical and
HR questions.

●  Resume-Based Questions: This round focused on my resume, with questions about
projects, internships, and leadership experiences. I was asked about my role in each
project, the technologies used, and how I addressed specific challenges.

●  Simple Coding: I was asked to write simple code, such as checking for palindromes.
●  HR Questions: Group project my role in that, how to resolve conflicts.

Preparation Strategy:

●  DSA and Dynamic Programming: I practiced solving data structure and algorithm

questions, with a focus on dynamic programming.

●  Aptitude Preparation: I reviewed basic aptitude questions.
●  Research on Bank of America: I researched Bank of America’s work culture, recent

Tricks and Tips I Followed:

1.  Complete Each Round Quickly: Focusing on accuracy and speed was key in the

coding test.

2.  Review Company Background: Learning about Bank of America’s values, mission,

and focus areas helped me connect my answers to the company’s goals.

3.  Ask Relevant Questions: Showing genuine interest by asking questions about projects,
the tech stack, and the work culture in the bank helped demonstrate my enthusiasm and
curiosity.
