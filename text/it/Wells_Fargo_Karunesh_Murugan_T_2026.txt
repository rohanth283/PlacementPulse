**1) Online Test ( SHL Platform ) :**

The test comprised 4 rounds: 2 Coding Sections, 1 Verbal Aptitude and 1 Business Aptitude. Navigation was strictly linear – going back to previous question/section is not allowed.

* **Section 1 (Coding – 1 Hr) :**

For this Coding Section alone, **Java was the only programming language allowed**. The question did vary for others.

**My Question (Simplified Version) was:**

“ Given a currency value ( Ex: ₹2700), find the minimum number of notes( ₹2000, ₹500, ₹200, ₹100, ₹50, ₹20, ₹10, ₹5, ₹1 Notes Possible) and display them in the specified format ”.

**I/P :** 1950

**O/P :**

500 | 3

200 | 2

50 | 1

**Solution Logic:** Can be solved through 2 methods: One is using mod (Fast/Efficient) , and another is repeated subtraction till 0 (Easier to Understand)

* **Section 2 ( Verbal Aptitude – 12 Mins ) :**

10 Questions. This section tested English vocabulary and reading comprehension. Were asked to do:

* Synonyms and anonyms (6 Questions) for underlined words
* Given a comprehension passage, some questions(4 questions) related to that one passage.
* **Section 3 ( Business Aptitude – 25 Mins ) :**

12 questions in total. This round felt like the deal-maker among all sections. 4 Graphic Visualization problems were given:

Tables, Venn Diagrams, Bar Charts, and Candlestick charts.

Due to time constraint, I was able to attempt only about half the questions. It required quick math and logical relations.

* **Section 4 ( Automata – 40 Mins ) :**

2 Questions with any preferred languages allowed. (Again, questions varied for others) Questions I got was (Simplified) :

**Q1)** **Given m days and n profit entries, return the max profit for each day.**

**Number of Test Cases:** 14

**Q2)** **Very lengthy. Can recollect the total question:**
“ A house can be represented as a cell. Thus, a group of houses with each house having adjacent house can be said as group of cells, where each house can be either active (1) or inactive (0) . When it’s the next day, if only one of its either neighbour is active/inactive (not both), then it will be turned active. If both of it’s neighbours are active/inactive, it will be turned inactive. It can be assumed the left neighbour and right neighbour of 1st cell and last cell respectively are inactive always. Provided an array of n cells state and m days, find and return the cells state after m days. “

**Sample I/P :**

1 (No of days)

1 0 1 1 0 0 1 (Stream of data)

**Sample O/P :**

0 0 1 1 1 1 0 (States after 1 day)

**Number of Test Cases:** 122

**Approach I took:** I went for a simple logic, which is to find the XOR of each cell’s neighbours ( both left and right ) to determine its next day state. For the first and last cells, which have only one neighbour, I directly used that neighbour’s state (XOR of 0 and x = x).

**2) Interview Rounds:**

In total 3 rounds. **Every round required a self-introduction** and some question related to your resume was asked by the interviewers.

* **Tech Round:**

The interviewer began by asking what course subjects I liked the most. I mentioned Advanced Data Structures and DBMS. He followed up with questions from DBMS, including concepts like **ETL**, **ELT**, **MDM**, and **CDC**.

Then, we shifted to **Data Structures**. I was asked to open a notepad and write a pseudo code to check whether a given string is a palindrome using pointers. As I typed, I explained the logic and reasoning behind each line.

Satisfied with my explanation, He moved on to the difference between Singly Linked List and Double Linked List - how it differs actually in code, their use cases and real-world examples.

Then, we discussed sorting. He asked for a detailed comparison between bubble sort and merge sort, including procedures , strategies and time complexities. I explained both in Notepad with examples.

* **Tech + HR Round:**

The HR reviewed my whole resume and asked me to elaborate on my previous intern experience and some of the projects I worked on.

Toward the end of the discussion, he asked a single question:

“What’s the use of a pointer? From Programmer’s Perspective”.

He clarified he wanted an answer that’s not the usual one “To store it in a variable” and that a layperson can - without diving into topics like Operating Systems or Computer Architecture.

In a nutshell, I explained pointers by starting with a university system example—where we don’t know how many students will enrol in advance. First, I Gave a simplified, real-world explanation of pointers, finally tying it back to dynamic memory management. It went more like a formal conversation with some back-and-forth.

* **HR Round:**

The HR round was quite relaxed. After a brief self-introduction, they asked some questions related to my resume. Towards the end : two questions, “Why should we hire you?” and “Why choose us?”. I answered both together by stating my interest and my desire to contribute in finance domain through this role.

Before wrapping up, they advised me to **improve my Java Skills** as 80% of their systems rely on Java.

**Interview Advice:**

* Most interviews are quite chill. Just stay calm and focused throughout any interviews and you will do good.
* My interviewers weren’t worried much about not knowing some technical concepts. For me, they were looking at how well you could communicate and whether you could explain a topic clearly to anyone.

**My Preparation Strategy:**

* I actually started my intern prep quite late – just about a month before the whole process kicked off. While most of my peers had been practicing since the start, I knew I had to work in that month itself.
* My plan wasn’t rigid or structured. I took a few courses (Free ones) and relied heavily on GeeksforGeeks & ACM questions for brushing up concepts and solving problems. What I took seriously was not just solving questions—but **understanding them deeply**. I made sure I knew why a solution worked, not just how.
* My mindset was kind of simple: Keep working until you can crack the test. And at the same time, I made sure my communication skills are good enough, like I said, that mattered the most out of anything else.
