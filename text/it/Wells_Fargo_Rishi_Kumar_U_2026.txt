Preparation Strategy

●  Data  Structures  &  Algorithms  (DSA):  The  online  test  included  a  medium-hard  dynamic
programming  problem.  While  the  interview  DSA  question  was  basic  (Height  of  a  Binary
Tree), a strong foundation is essential to clear the initial screening. Practice consistently on
platforms like HackerRank or LeetCode.

●  Core  CS  Fundamentals:  This  is  non-negotiable.  Interviewers  repeatedly  tested  concepts
from  OOPS,  DBMS,  Operating  Systems,  and  Computer  Networks.  Ensure  you  have  a
deep and clear understanding of the fundamentals.

●  Full-Stack  Development:  The  technical  interviews  heavily  emphasized  my  development
projects.  Be  prepared  to  discuss  your  tech  stack  (React,  Node.js,  Express),  backend
design, database schema, security policies, and API design in detail.

●  SQL:  The  online  test  featured  a  dedicated  SQL  question,  and  database  concepts  were
discussed in interviews. You must be comfortable writing complex queries involving joins,sub
queries and aggregate functions.

●  Software  Development  Life  Cycle  (SDLC):  This  topic  was  brought  up  in  every  single
interview  round.  It's  critical  to  understand  the  different  phases  of  SDLC  and  be  able  to
explain how you applied them to your projects.

●  Resume Deep Dive: Be prepared to explain every single line on your resume. Interviewers
will  ask  follow-up  questions  about  your  projects,  internships, hackathons, and skills. Know
your story and your work!

Placement Drive Experience

The entire process consisted of four rounds conducted sequentially.

Round 1: Online Test (HackerRank)

This round was purely focused on coding and problem-solving, deviating from the previous pattern of
including aptitude questions.

The test consisted of three problems:

1.  Regex:  Write  a regular expression to find all words where the starting and ending letters

are the same.

2.  Dynamic Programming (The Taxi Driver Problem):

A taxi driver receives ride requests, each with a pickup, drop, and tip. The earning for a ride
is calculated as:
earning=(drop[i]−pickup[i])+tip[i]
The driver can only accept non-overlapping rides (a new ride's pickup time must be greater
than  or  equal  to  the  previous  ride's  drop  time).  Given  the  three  arrays,  find  the  maximum
possible earnings.

3.  SQL Query:

Write  a query to find the total usage time (in seconds) for each task. The usage time is the
sum of the differences between end_dt and start_dt for all its associated processes. Return
each task's hash and its total usage time, ordered descending by the usage time.

Tips and Tricks

●  Honesty  is the Best Policy: During my first interview, the interviewer was trying to assess
how  honest  I  was  with  my  answers.  Don't  bluff.  If  you  don't  know  something, it's better to
admit it.

●  Know Your Resume: This cannot be stressed enough. Every single word on your resume is
fair  game  for  a  deep-dive  question.  Be  prepared  to  defend  and  elaborate  on  everything
you've written.

●  Master  the  Fundamentals:  Wells  Fargo  places  a high value on strong core CS concepts.

Don't just focus on DSA; revise OOPS, DBMS, OS, and Networks thoroughly.

●  Connect  Projects  to  SDLC:  Simply  building  a  project  is  not  enough.  Be ready to explain
how  you  built  it  using  principles  from  the  Software  Development  Life  Cycle.  This  shows
maturity and a professional mindset.

●  Be  Communicative  and  Confident:  The  interviewers  were  chill  and  appreciated  a
conversational style. Don't just give one-word answers. Explain your thought process clearly
and confidently.

●  Ask  Insightful  Questions:  Prepare  a  few  good  questions  to  ask  your  interviewers.  This

shows your interest in the role and the company.
