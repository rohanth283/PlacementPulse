**Overview of the Process**

● Round 1: Online Assessment

● Round 2: Technical Interview – at CUIC

● Round 3: HR Interview – at CUIC

**Online Assessment**

The assessment consisted of four sections on English Proficiency, Technical, Coding and Algorithm.

**Section 1:**

Contained questions that assessed reading comprehension, sentence arrangement, synonyms, and other related skills.

**Section 2:**

Technical multiple-choice questions covering a range of topics such as Computer Networks, Operating Systems, Object-Oriented Programming, C++, Output questions, and Data Structures & Algorithms.

**Section 3:**

This section had 2 coding questions; primarily focused on logic and manipulation of Arrays and Strings, testing candidates' coding skills.

**Q1)** A string is given, and you need to perform string manipulation. Based on the sample inputs, identify the pattern and return the converted string as the output.

**Q2)** Two numbers are given. You need to find the sum of the digits of both numbers, then return the product of these two sums.

76 students were shortlisted for the offline interview.

**Technical Interview:**

The interview panels were assigned randomly, and my round lasted for about 30 minutes. I was first asked to introduce myself, after which the interviewer moved on to the projects I had listed on my resume. They questioned me about the technical aspects of those projects.

Since I mentioned that C++ is my favorite language, the interviewer asked me several OOP-related questions, such as the problems with multiple inheritance (diamond problem). They also asked me about data structures—what kind of data structure I would use for different purposes. Specifically, I was asked about hashing, how collisions can be resolved, and the time complexity of hash operations with and without collisions.

Finally, I was given a linked list DSA question. The problem was based on a circular linked list where the tail node was connected somewhere in the middle of the list. I was asked to make the tail point back to the head, and I explained my approach to solve it.

**HR Interview:**

This round was mainly conducted to evaluate my interpersonal skills and overall suitability for the organization. The interviewer focused on assessing how I interact and communicate effectively.

The HR first asked me to introduce myself, followed by a few questions about my parents and siblings. Then, the discussion moved on to my role preferences. Towards the end, the HR asked if I had any questions for him.

12 students were offered the internship.
