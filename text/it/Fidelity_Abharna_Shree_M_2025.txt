Online Assessment in Hirepro platform

The assessment consisted of four sections on English Proficiency, Technical,

Coding and Algorithm. Each of the sections were separately timed.

Section 1: The questions were based on English vocabulary such as fill in the

appropriate word, synonyms, sentence rearrangement and passage

comprehension.

Section 2: MCQ’s on Operating Systems, Data Structures and Algorithms,

Computer Networks, OOP, SQL, including code snippets and output/error

analysis. There was no negative marking, yet I didn’t attend all questions in this

section.

Section 3: This section had 2 coding questions.

•  First question was to find the extreme number by swapping the digits

once (solved with brute force approach)

•  The second question was to print the string of count of consecutive

patterns in the input i.e. input – “%%%$$%%” output - “322”

My code passed all test cases for both the questions.

Section 4: We were asked to type out two Algorithms.

•  Recursion Algo for subsequence (selection/rejection)

•  Infix to prefix conversion with stack

As I spent more time on the first algo, I couldn’t complete the second one

within the time limit.

54 students were shortlisted for the offline interview.

Technical Interview

There were several panels for this round and the questions and the duration

varied depending on the panel. On a general note, the focus was on discussing

projects from the resume and technical questions related to CS fundamentals.

My interview lasted for about 40-45 minutes.

•  The interviewer introduced himself and asked me to introduce myself.

•  He asked me to explain one of my projects on my resume.

•  He asked if had studied OS as part of my coursework and questioned

about kernel in OS.

•  What is inheritance and abstraction in OOPs.

•  I was given two tables and asked to write a simple SQL query (join) for

the specified result.

•  He noticed that I had hosted a website in Render from my resume and I

offered to show it on my phone. He was interested and waited till I came

back.

•  I was asked if had knowledge of cloud storage. Though I had anticipated

the question, I mentioned that I had only a basic understanding, and he

then asked to name a few cloud service providers.

•  He asked about my hobbies and other co-curricular activities mentioned

in my resume.

•  I asked about his journey at Fidelity and how will my day at fidelity be,

when offered an internship.

The questions were basic and I was able to answer all of them.

Tips and Tricks

•  Go through previous years’ intern interview experiences from online

platforms and seniors. I prepared topics like cloud storage and data

mining, apart from regular CS fundamentals, after reading experiences on

platforms like Geeks for Geeks.

•  Dress up in formal decent attire for the interview to make a good first

impression.

•  The HR interviewer greeted each interviewee with a handshake, make

sure to give a confident handshake as it reflects your personality

•  Ask peers who have their interviews before you about their experience to

get an idea of the questions. There are chances you might get a similar

question.

•  Do not mention anything you are not confident about in your resume.

•  Communication skills are of great importance. How you present what you

know is equally important to what you know!

My Preparation Journey

•  I started to solve the weekly practice questions released from ACM

CODE and I attended most weekly contests in 4th semester holidays. I

managed to solve all easy and just more than half of medium-level

questions in most topics.

•  I tried to solved 4 to 6 questions every day for about two weeks. While I

couldn't maintain my daily streak every day, I remained consistent. I

would have no idea on a question, after 10 minutes of trying to figure it

out on my own, I got YouTube help. I couldn’t wrap my head around

some algorithms on the first attempt, so I set a time limit of one hour per

question. Even if I didn’t understand the solution completely, I would

move to the next question. I couldn’t cover all topics I had initially

planned as I had to work on my summer project as well.

•  Also, Preparation and experience of previous company interviews came

in handy.
