Normally, if an online test is conducted in SHL platform, it would be easier than Hackerrank, Hackerearth or any other platform of the company itself. So don’t worry about the difficulty of the contest. Unlike other Companies SAP’s coding round did not have any aptitude section.

You can come back and revisit the first question after answering second question. No negative markings.

**Both questions were simple.**

1. Given a string input remove the continuous(adjacent) occurrence of a given character say “\*”, now return the count of “\*” in the string.
   **NOTE: remove only adjacent occurrences of “\*”, not all the occurrences.**
2. Given 2 arrays/list namely num1 and num2 (not in sorted order), return the array/list of non-common elements in both num1 and num2 in sorted order.

**TIPS for Online Test:**

Always remember that if a test is easy, you have to race with time. If more people solve the given questions, then obviously the deciding criteria would be either how fast you did than others or who wrote the most optimised code in terms of time (how long did your program take to run).

* **priority 1: Getting All test cases passed**
* **Priority2: Race with Time (submit it as soon as all test cases are passed, sometimes need not even worry about the optimised code/brute force code. If your code passed all the test cases that would suffice for getting shortlisted in the test)**
* **Priority3: Code that runs faster (efficient and optimised code)**
* **NOTE: Space complexity doesn’t matter much here.**
* **Sometimes Brute force = optimal (not aways though)**

Whether you do in brute force or in an efficient way, have an eye on timing. Both the questions I got was very much simple and straight forward, so simple, clear code would suffice because there were no 3 methods of solving it like brute, better, optimal anol **(In the case of questions that I got brute force were the only and optimal method to solve it)**. The moment a question t passed all the test cases, I finished the question and moved on to next. I submitted the test within first 15 minutes, which greatly helped me get selected for the next round.

**TRICKS THAT I FOLLOWED**:

* As said earlier, I completed the online test as soon as possible
* During an online interview always share screen and present your projects, that will be impressive to the interviewer.
* The moment I came to know that I was selected for the interview, I started preparing Company specific DSA questions from Geeks for Geeks.
* I did read the SAP internship interview experience from various websites like Glassdoor.
* Did gather knowledge about what kind of company it is? Where is their headquarters? That’s when I got to know about SAP’s products.
* In every round, they’ll ask “Do you have any questions for us?” as the last question.
  Never say that I do not have any question, always do ask some(max 2 or 3) meaningful and reasonable/genuine questions.
  + What kind of softwares do they develop or what what exactly do they do?
  + Tech stacks they use
  + kind of projects that we do in intern either a stand-alone new project or working as a part of already existing project?
  + Work location
  + Work culture in the company in your perspective, etc
  + Your questions will determine your eagerness to work in the company.

**MY PREPARATION STRATEGY:**

* I didn’t do extensive coding prep in my 4th sem in fact did not know it’s seriousness during semester holidays. I had spent my holidays in my summer project, a bit on intern preparation and in leisure. Only After the schedule of internships and placements were given by the CUIC, Reality hit me hard.
* I was neither a naturally quick learner nor a very brilliant student with quick grasping power.
* I took my time and did prepare harder and dedicated. I wasn’t able to complete my intern prep when I sat for first few companies especially I found it challenging to complete trees and graphs. But I made sure to prepare constantly. Take SDE sheets from the internet and solve different types of questions in every topic.
  + Array/Linkedlist (single/double/circular)
  + Stack/queues (valid paranthesis/ LRU cache etc)
  + Trees (inorder/preorder/postorder/lowest common ancestor/Height and diameter of the tree)
  + Graphs (BFS/DFS/Level order)
  + Recursion/DP/Greedy
  + Sorting/Searching
* I used ACM CODE documents for my preparation that comes topicwise.
* Even though I wasn’t able to solve much of harder problems mentioned in it, I made sure I solved all the easier ones and atleast some of the medium ones in every topic.
* I did prepare using TUF (Take you forward) website and YouTube videos where you have comprehensive explanation of brute-force, better and optimal approach.
* I approached seniors for their inputs and sometimes even to rant. Have good set of friends and family who support you through the rough time.
* Before every online test/ interview, I would read the past internship experiences of that particular company. Learn from mistakes, never repeat the same mistake you already did.
* Rejections and failing an interview or not clearing an online test did take a toll on self-confidence. But **whenever you fall down, make sure you come back stronger and built better than before.**
* Internship and placement seasons would be stressful and might even lead to self doubts at times.
* But hold one belief working hard honestly will always pay you back.
