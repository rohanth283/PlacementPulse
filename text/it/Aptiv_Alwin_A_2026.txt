Aptiv Interview Experience

About the Role:

Associate Embedded Software Engineer

There are various roles including software, hardware,
and AI/ML. I have been selected for the position of
Software Engineer – PM. This role involves working
on the infotainment systems which we see in the car.

Selection Process:

• Online test

• Technical interview

• Techno managerial interview

• HR interview

Round 1: Online Assessment

[90 mins]

Round 1 was an online HackerRank assessment. It
included a few MCQs from core subjects like
dbms,os,oops. Apart from the MCQs, there were two
questions. For some candidates, both questions were
coding problems, with one being mandatory in C and
the other allowed in any programming language. For
others, like me, there was one coding question and one
SQL question. The coding problem was of medium
difficulty, and the SQL question had to be solved using
window functions.

Round 2: Technical Interview                      [50 mins]

All the interview rounds were conducted online.

In my second round, which was the technical interview,
I was asked questions from multiple domains. The
interview began with a coding question based on
LeetCode Problem 3: Longest Substring Without
Repeating Characters. After that, I was asked to write a
basic SQL query involving a JOIN. The interviewer

provided the table names and attributes, and I had to
write the query. I was also asked conceptual questions
related to primary keys and foreign keys and how they
are used in different scenarios.

                    Next, the discussion moved to computer
networks and security, where I was asked about HTTPS
and how certificates are received and validated. This
led to questions on cryptography, including symmetric
key encryption, asymmetric key encryption, and
hashing, along with examples for each.

Overall, the interview lasted for about 50 minutes and
covered a wide range of technical topics.

Round 3: Techno managerial interview      [35 mins]

                         In the techno-managerial round, the
discussion was mainly based on my resume and the
projects I had worked on. I spoke about a project
related to autonomous vehicles, and the interviewer
asked me to explain it in detail. He also discussed my
second project mentioned in the resume and asked
follow-up questions about it.

                         In addition, I was asked behavioral
questions, including whether any conflicts had occurred
within my team. I described a real situation where a
conflict arose and explained how I handled it in my role
as a leader.

Overall, this round was focused on understanding my
attitude, values, teamwork, leadership skills, and
decision-making approach, rather than purely technical
knowledge.

Round 4: HR Discussion                        [15 mins]

                        The interviews were originally planned
to be conducted over two days. However, in my case,
the process extended to a third day, which was actually
a holiday, and only five members had their interviews
on that day. Usually, the interviews are completed
within two days.

                          Some candidates may start and even
complete their HR round on the first day itself, while
others might not have even started their technical
rounds by then. So it is important to wait patiently and
stay calm throughout the process. In my case, I was
the last person to attend the HR discussion. My
technical interview happened on the second day in the
evening, and the techno-managerial round was
conducted on the second day at around 7 PM. The final
interview took place the next day, even though it was
not part of the original schedule.

                           It is also important to note that
there can be rejections even in the HR round at Aptiv.
So, be confident in the way you speak and maintain
good communication skills, as these play a crucial role

in clearing the HR round. Additionally, having a good
understanding of the company and asking relevant,
company-related questions to the interviewer can leave
a positive impression. After 5 days the results are out
and 25 of us is selected from different backgrounds for
different roles.

My Preparation Strategy:

                           I focused on general preparation by
practicing LeetCode problems and HackerRank SQL
questions. I followed Striver’s DSA sheet and referred
to YouTube videos to strengthen my understanding of
data structures and algorithms. I revised core concepts
using GeeksforGeeks, including OOPS. For Operating
Systems, I studied from the Neso Academy YouTube
channel, and for DBMS, I referred to Jenny’s Lectures.

Key Suggestions and Tips from My Experience:

                   Do not feel pressured by seeing others get
placed early. Everyone’s journey is different, and your
opportunity will come at the right time. There is no
such thing as being “too late” — what matters is
consistent effort and patience. Focus mainly on
strengthening your data structures and core concepts,
as they form the foundation for most technical
interviews.

        Also, be honest with your resume. Do not include
anything that you are not confident about or cannot
explain clearly. Make sure you understand every line
you write, because interviewers can ask questions from
any part of it. Stay focused, keep practicing, and trust
the process.
