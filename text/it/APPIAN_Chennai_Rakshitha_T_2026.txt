# **Selection Process**

### **1️ Technical Online Test**

### **2️ Technical Interview – Round 1**

### **3️ Managerial / Leadership Round (US Head) – Round 2**

### **4️ HR Round – Round 3**

All three interview rounds were conducted **on the same day** after shortlisting from the online test.

# **Technical Online Test**

**Focus: DSA (3–4 Coding Questions)**

The online assessment was purely technical and DSA-focused.

### **Topics Covered:**

* Arrays
* Strings
* Recursion
* Sorting
* Hashing
* Time & Space Complexity

The questions were easy - medium level and required clear logic building.

Strategy that worked for me:

* Started with brute-force approach
* Clearly optimized step-by-step
* Paid attention to edge cases
* Managed time effectively

Shortlisted candidates moved to interviews.

# **Round 1: Technical Interview (Core + Resume + OOPS)**

**Duration: ~40–45 minutes**

This round was conducted offline.

### **🔹 Part 1 – Resume Deep Dive (First 20 Minutes)**

The interviewer spent significant time discussing my resume:

* Detailed explanation of my projects
* Tech stack used
* Architecture decisions
* Challenges faced
* My leadership role as **President of IEEE WIE**
* Research wing initiatives under IEEE

They were particularly impressed with:

* My research initiatives
* My club leadership experience
* How I balanced technical and organizational responsibilities

They evaluated:

* Depth of understanding
* Ownership
* Communication clarity
* Real contribution vs superficial involvement

### **🔹 Part 2 – OOPS (Python Focus) (Next 20 Minutes)**

They asked me which language I was comfortable with. I chose **Python**.

Interestingly:

* They already had a **printed sheet of OOPS questions in Python**.
* I was asked to **write code on paper** in front of them.
* It was a very basic fill in the blanks type of question. There were pre-defined Classes and I was asked to write functions for those classes.

### **OOPS Topics Covered:**

* Classes and Objects
* Inheritance
* Polymorphism
* Encapsulation
* Constructors
* Method Overriding
* Practical implementation scenarios

They checked:

* Concept clarity
* Syntax awareness
* Logical structuring
* Clean code thinking

This round tested **fundamentals more than complexity**.

# **Round 2: Managerial / Leadership Round**

**(Google Meet with US Head – Conducted at CUIC)**

This round was more conversational.

Questions asked:

* Why do you want to join Appian?
* What are your future career plans?
* Do you have any other offers?
* What motivates you?

I clearly explained:

* My interest in product-building
* My long-term goal of becoming a **Product Manager**
* How Product Engineering at Appian gives strong product exposure

They appreciated:

* Clarity of vision
* Honest communication
* Long-term alignment

# **Round 3: HR Round**

This round focused on:

* Role explanation
* Offer intention confirmation

I mentioned that:

I aim to transition into Product Management in the future, and starting as a Product Engineer at Appian is the perfect path to build strong technical-product foundations.

The HR appreciated the clarity and ambition.

# **Key Takeaways from My Experience**

### **1️ Be Very Clear About Your Resume**

They can question anything mentioned.
Never bluff.

### **2️ Fundamentals > Fancy Knowledge**

OOPS clarity mattered more than advanced frameworks.

### **3️ Leadership Matters**

My role as IEEE WIE President and research initiatives added strong weight.

### **4️ Career Clarity Impresses**

Having a long-term goal (PM path) showed maturity.

### **5️ Personal Branding Helps**

Something as simple as a QR resume card can differentiate you.

# **Preparation Strategy**

### **DSA:**

* Solved topic-wise problems
* Practiced [neetcode.io](http://neetcode.io) 150 questions
* Focused on explaining approach verbally

### **OOPS:**

* Practiced implementing concepts in Python
* Reviewed inheritance, overriding, polymorphism deeply

### **Resume:**

* Prepared explanations for every single bullet point
* Prepared impact statements for each project
* Don't put anything on your resume that you can't vouch for or explain.

# **Final Advice**

* Start preparation early
* Master fundamentals
* Build leadership exposure
* Be authentic
* Think long-term
* Add small differentiators

Preparation + Clarity + Personal Branding = Strong Impact
