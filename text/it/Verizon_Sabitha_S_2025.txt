Role offered: Software Developer

Placement Drive Experience:

Round 1: Online Test

The online test had 3 sections:

1: Written English Test – A topic was given, and we needed to write an essay.
My topic was "What Should Adults Learn from Children."

2: Coding Test – Two medium-level coding questions.

3: Gamified Test

Round 2: Group Discussion

In this round, we were divided into groups of 12. The topic was "Is AI
Decreasing Our Creativity and Impacting Our Security?" I focused on the
quality of my points rather than the quantity.

Round 3: Technical interview

The technical questions were primarily based on my resume, including the
projects I had worked on, the challenges I encountered, and the technical
concepts involved. I was asked about difficulties I faced in my life and how I
managed them.

The interviewer asked me to write code for counting duplicate elements in an
array and some SQL queries related to aggregate functions, joins, and
subqueries. Additionally, they asked me to explain OOP concepts, cloud
computing, MVC, real-world examples of data structures, and a few questions
about Splay trees. I was also asked basic questions on APIs, their types, and
NLP. The interviewer made me feel comfortable, which allowed me to answer
calmly.

Round 4: HR interview

The HR interview began with a self-introduction, followed by questions about
my background and a few behavioral questions. I was also asked why I chose
Verizon and if I had any questions for the interviewer.

Overall, I had a positive experience.

Tips:

•  Be thorough with your resume, as most questions will come from it.

Ensure you understand the concepts you mention, especially in projects.

•  Stay calm while answering questions; if you don’t know a concept,

politely say you're not familiar with it.

•  Have some knowledge about the company you are interviewing with
•  Prepare a few questions to ask the interviewer at the end.
