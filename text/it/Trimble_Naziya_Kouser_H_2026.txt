Tips for your interview:

▪ Understand Your Resume Thoroughly:
 Be prepared to discuss every line on your resume — projects, internships, skills, and
achievements. The interviewer may pick any point to test your depth.

▪ Be Clear About Your Strengths and Weaknesses:
 Have 2–3 genuine strengths and 1–2 weaknesses ready. Be honest, but show that
you’re working to improve your weaknesses.

▪ Practice Basics from Core Subjects:
 Revise your fundamentals — the interviewer often checks clarity in core concepts rather
than advanced topics.

▪ Know Your Projects Well:
 Be ready to explain your project flow, tools used, your contribution, and the impact.
Practice explaining it in simple, confident language.

▪ Dress Professionally:
 First impressions count. Wear neat, formal clothes and maintain proper grooming.

▪ Be Honest When You Don’t Know:
 If you don’t know an answer, admit it politely instead of guessing wildly. Say something
like “I’m not sure right now, but I’d love to learn more about it.”

▪ Keep a Positive Attitude:
 Even if one round doesn’t go well, stay positive for the next. Interviewers notice how you
handle pressure and setbacks.

▪ Ask Thoughtful Questions:
 At the end, when given a chance, ask about the company’s work culture, team, or
upcoming projects. It shows curiosity and enthusiasm.
