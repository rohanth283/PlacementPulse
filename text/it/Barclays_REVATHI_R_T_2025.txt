 BARCLAYS INTERN EXPERIENCE

About the Company – Barclays :

Barclays is a global bank offering banking, credit cards, wealth, and other
forms  of  financial  provision.  It  is  one  of  the  companies  majorly  focusing  on
innovation  and  technology.  The  company  utilizes  young  talents  and  provides
them with an opportunity to work on scalable applications, microservices, and
functional solutions in an interactive environment.

Role -  Technology Summer Intern :

In the role of Barclays internship, application and microservices should be
designed  and  developed  in  a  scalable  manner.  Server  components  and  the
functional solution should be created and implemented. Its main tasks include
collaborating with product owners and the end-users to elicit requirements and
do unit testing and planning the release of the software. The role also includes
code  reviews  as  well  as  support  given  for  business-as-usual  (BAU)  activities
related to the applications.

Duration: 8 weeks

Overview of the process:

The selection process began with an online coding round that tested different
skill sets in three sections:

1.  Section 1: 12 multiple-choice questions (MCQs) covering object-oriented
programming  (OOP)  concepts,  code  output  predictions,  and  error
detection.

2.  Section  2:  One  medium-level  Data  Structures  and  Algorithms  (DSA)

question related to strings.

3.  Section 3: One SQL query-based question involving a table structure.

How I Cleared the First Round :

I  prepared  for  this  round  by  working  extensively  with  the  Standard
Template  Library  (STL)  and  solving  problems  on  platforms  like  LeetCode.
Although I wasn’t deeply into competitive programming (CP), I gradually learned
to solve problems more efficiently. By focusing on simpler techniques and using
STL containers effectively, I was able to enhance my problem-solving approach.

This  not  only  improved  my  understanding  of  algorithms  but  also  helped  me
utilize these techniques for solving complex problems.

I  particularly  enjoyed  solving  SQL  queries,  which  allowed  me  to  apply  new
techniques and gain practical insights. With a better grasp of efficient problem-
solving strategies, I cleared the online round, where 29 students were shortlisted
for the interview.

 Tips for Future Applicants :

1.  Research the Company: Make sure to thoroughly research the company’s
background,  work  culture,  and  values.  This  will  help  you  confidently
answer HR questions like “Why Barclays?” and give you an edge in making
a positive impression.

2.  Technical  Preparation:  Focus  on  both  DBMS  and  DSA  topics,  as  many
technical questions in interviews may revolve around these areas. Revise
key  concepts  and  practice  regularly  on  platforms  like  LeetCode  to  gain
exposure to  a wide  variety of problems. Solving problems in  a  step-by-
step manner using simpler techniques can help in tackling more complex
challenges later. Make use of STL (in C++) for efficient coding, especially if
you’re  into  competitive  programming  (CP).  Additionally,  regularly  solve
SQL queries to enhance your database knowledge.

3.  Mock Interviews: Practice mock interviews focusing on both technical and
HR  questions.  Be  prepared  for  situational  questions  to  showcase  your
problem-solving and interpersonal skills.

4.  Resume Preparation: Ensure your resume is clear, concise, and formatted
well.  It  should  effectively  present  your  strengths  and  make  it  easy  for
interviewers to grasp your experience.
