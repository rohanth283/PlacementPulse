**About the Company:**

Fidelity is one of the leading financial investment companies in the U.S., known for promoting a perfect work-life balance. It fosters an environment where there's ample opportunity to learn and explore, with innovation and curiosity being key drivers for success.

**Overview of the Process:**

The overall process consisted of:

* An Online coding contest.
* Interview Round 1 (Technical) – at CUIC
* Interview Round 2 (HR) – at CUIC

**Coding Test:** (Hirepro Platform)

This round had four sections.

* **Section 1:** Multiple-choice questions on English.
* **Section 2:** Multiple-choice technical questions covering topics such as Computer Networks, Operating Systems, OOP, C++, and Data Structures & Algorithms (DSA), Database Management Systems.
* **Section 3:** Coding questions: There were two problems, one of medium difficulty and another of a hard level, focused on Arrays and Strings.

1. Form the smallest number using at most one swap operation.
2. From strings.

* **Section 4:** Algorithm: We were asked to write algorithm for two problems. The time for this section was more limited compared to the others. (10 minutes)

1. Infix to prefix.

2. Generate combinations.

54 students were shortlisted for the next round

**Interview Round 1 –Technical (60 minutes):**

* What is Interprocess communication? Explain it in brief.
* What is DBMS? DBMS VS file systems.
* What is normalisation? Explain all the normal forms with real life examples.
* What is transaction? Explain properties of transaction.
* What is Index in DBMS?
* Use of pipeline.
* Bootstrap in OS.
* A problem on linked list and much more.

25 students shortlisted for the next round.

**Interview Round 2–HR (30 minutes):**

* Scenario based questions.
* Pros and cons of teamwork
* Some discussion on my portfolio and about the analysis I did to buy them (because I mentioned about stocks in my interests).
* Previous internship experience.
* Problem statement and solution we proposed in a hackathon (that got us the “Best team in Fintech”).
* Some general family related questions.

And more.

12 students were selected.

**Preparation Strategy:**

* **Competitive programming:**

I used materials of ACM and strivers topic wise playlists.

Watch youtube videos when you are not able to solve the problem for more than 30 minutes.

* **DBMS, OS, OOP:**

These are covered in the curriculum. For revision I used ‘Last minute notes’ of Geeks for Geeks.

**Tips from my side:**

* Get to know the optimised solution even after all the test cases passes.
* Be strong in the fundamentals of OS, DBMS, OOP.
* Good to have projects relevant to the company in your resume.
* Read the previous interview experiences of the company from Geeks for Geeks interview archives.
* If your interests align with the company’s work, make sure to include it in the conversation to increase the chances of getting selected.
* Research about the company before going to the interview.
* Practise tests in timed environment.
* Be confident when answering in the interview.
* The types of questions varied across panels. Most panels asked about the projects listed on the resume, while some focused on fundamental concepts of DBMS, OS, and OOP.
* If you didn’t know the answer for any question, don’t panic. Tell the interviewer that you are not sure about it now, but will definitely be able to learn it.
