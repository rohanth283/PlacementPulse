Placement Drive Process:

Round 1 : Online Assessment

The initial assessment was an online evaluation that tested my coding skills and proficiency
in English communication. It included a medium-level coding question focused on data
structures and algorithms (DSA), along with evaluations of logical reasoning and critical
thinking. I found that preparing with platforms like LeetCode and HackerRank was extremely
beneficial for this round.

Round 2: Group Discussion

The second round was a group discussion, where we were split into teams of 12 for the
same. We were presented with two topics: one focused on the impacts of COVID-19 on
education and the other on the effects of generative AI on creativity. We chose to discuss
the latter.

During the discussion, I made sure to articulate my points clearly and concisely, ensuring
that my contributions were well-understood by all participants. I actively engaged with my
peers, adding relevant points and building on their ideas, while also allowing space for
others to share their perspectives. Staying updated with current news and trends
significantly aided my contributions, as I could reference relevant examples and data to
support my arguments. This preparation allowed me to maintain a balanced and informed
dialogue throughout the discussion.

Round 3: Technical Interview

The next round was technical interview where the interviewer conducted a thorough review
of my resume. It was essential to be well-acquainted with every detail, as the interviewer
delved deeply into my internship experiences and the projects I had mentioned. I was
questioned on all relevant topics associated with my work, so a solid understanding of the
technologies used in my projects was crucial.

Following the resume review, we moved on to several questions related to data structures
and algorithms, databases, and machine learning, particularly since I had completed a
project in that area, cloud computing, cryptography which included questions on linked lists,
normalization, transactions, encryption algorithms, keys in encryption, machine learning
models applied to speech and image data, etc.

Additionally, I was presented with a logical thinking puzzle,

There are three light bulbs inside a room and two switches outside the room. You are

allowed to enter the room only once and could use the switches only twice. The challenge
was to determine which switch corresponds to each bulb.

Overall, it was a pleasant and positive experience. The interviewer was very approachable
and calm, genuinely listening to my responses and creating an encouraging atmosphere
throughout the discussion.

Round 4: HR interview

The final round was an HR interview that focused on my background, motivations, and
career aspirations. This discussion was more generalized, allowing me to share my personal
journey and I appreciated the opportunity to articulate my passion for the industry and how
my goals align with the company's vision.

The conversation was engaging and provided a platform for me to ask questions about the
company's future and opportunities for growth. It was a constructive and enlightening
experience.

Key takeaways:

1.  Familiarize yourself with data structures, algorithms, and the technologies

mentioned in your resume to excel in technical assessments.

2.  Stay informed about industry trends, particularly in areas like 5G, IoT, and AI, to

enhance your contributions during group discussions.

3.  Be well-versed in the projects listed on your resume and prepare to discuss the

experiences you highlight.

4.  Emphasize how your personal and professional goals align with Verizon’s mission of

innovation and customer-centricity.

5.  Be ready with a few questions about the company or the role to ask the interviewer.
