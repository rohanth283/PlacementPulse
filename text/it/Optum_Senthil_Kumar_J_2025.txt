| 3. Technical |        | Interview |          |     |     |     |
In the technical interview, which took place in the afternoon, I covered a lot
about my projects and technical skills. I started with a quick introduction about
myself and my background before diving into my project experience.

Project Discussion:
● First Project: I talked about a summer project we worked on during my
2nd year. I explained the tech stack we used, some challenges we faced,
and the collaborative side of working as a team. We used MongoDB for
the project, and I shared why we chose it over PostgreSQL, especially
emphasizing MongoDB's sharding feature, which allows better data
distribution.
● Next Project: Then, we discussed another project from my resume. I
walked the interviewer through the algorithms and features we
implemented, using diagrams to give a clear idea of the implementation.
He seemed interested in the details, which made it easy to explain our
approach and the reasons behind our design choices.
Technical Questions:
● Authentication vs. Authorization: I gave an overview of both concepts,
breaking down how each one works in a typical application.
● Favorite Subject (Computer Networks): Since I picked networks as my
favorite, he asked about specific layers. I explained the network and data
link layers and how IP and MAC addresses operate within them.
● SQL Problem: He gave me a query to solve, which I initially approached
using subqueries. Then, I used joins to make it more efficient.
● DSA Problem: For the DSA question, I outlined my approach and coded
the solution on paper, explaining its space and time complexity. I also
pointed out an additional optimization, which he appreciated.
Learning New Tech Stacks: The interviewer was curious about how I learn
new tech stacks. I shared that I usually combine team insights with my own
research. For example, when I learned React, I took advice from teammates and
also did my own deep dive before reaching a conclusion.
Why Optum?: Finally, we discussed why I was interested in Optum. I
mentioned the points from their presentation that stood out to me—like their
work in health technology and how their projects impact real people.
We wrapped up with a quick chat about the role of new technologies, including
ChatGPT, in improving workflows. It was a positive way to end, and we both
agreed on the power of tech in making life easier.
