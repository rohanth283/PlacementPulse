EUCLOID DATA SOLUTIONS – INTERVIEW EXPERIENCE 2024
Role : Data Engineer

Eucloid  visited  our  campus  for  recruitment  for  the  first  time.  The
process started with a PPT, followed by an online test. The next day,
we had the interview. (All process Virtual)

Round 1: Online Test

As mentioned, the first round was an online test consisting of basic
multiple-choice  questions.  The  questions  were  designed  to  assess
analytical  thinking,  logical  reasoning,  and  problem-solving  skills.
Topics included aptitude, mathematics, statistics, verbal ability, and
even a few puzzles. Notably, there were no coding questions.

Out of 99 students who took the assessment, 42 were shortlisted for
the technical interview. There was a slight delay in announcing the
results, but it was communicated by the placement cell around 11
AM the following day.

Round 2: Technical Interview

The  technical  interview  was  conducted  via  Microsoft  Teams.  It
started  with  a  self-introduction,  after  which  the  interviewer  asked
me to discuss my web development projects, particularly one I had
listed on my resume. I used the STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
method to explain the project. The interviewer asked several follow-
up questions, especially about AWS, since I had deployed my project

using  cloud  services.  I  provided  a  detailed  explanation  of  EC2
instances and the steps involved in setting them up.

Technical Questions asked were:

•  What is a Data Engineer?
•  Can you explain the Star Schema?
•  How would you rate yourself in SQL on a scale of 1 to 10?
•  What is the difference between Drop and Truncate?
•  What is the difference between WHERE and HAVING clauses?
•  Which window function is used to access the value in the next

row?

•  I  responded  with  "LEAD  function"  and  elaborated  on  the
boundary conditions. The interviewer asked further questions
about  window  functions,  such  as  the  differences  between
RANK and DENSE_RANK.

•  How would you rate yourself in problem-solving?
•  Solve the Water Jug problem: Water Jug Puzzle Solution
•  Solve the 8 Balls Puzzle: Puzzle Link
•  Solve the Cake Cutting Puzzle: Cake Puzzle

I provided the correct solutions and explained my approach to the
puzzles.  The  interviewer  was  kind  and  professional,  and  he
concluded  by  asking  if  I  had  any  questions.  I  inquired  if  there  was
anything I could improve, and he responded positively, saying he was
fully satisfied with my performance.

A total of 13 candidates were shortlisted for the next round.

Round 3: Interview with Leadership team (Technical and HR)

This round was conducted by the Director of Data Engineering. He
began by asking how my previous interview went and then shifted to
questions about my project.

He was particularly interested in my cloud experience, so I shared my
screen  and  explained  him  through  both  the  code  and  the  user
interface of my project, explained sequelize ORM elaborately used in
my backend.

He asked why I had made specific technical decisions, and I explained
each feature in detail.

He  also  asked  about  my  experience  as  a  domain  director  for  my
college clubs, as this was mentioned on my resume. Finally, he posed
another puzzle: Man Fell in the Well Puzzle.

At the end of the process, five candidates received offers, and I was
so happy to be one of them.
