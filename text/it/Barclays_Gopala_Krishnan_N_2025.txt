About the Company:

Barclays is a prominent multinational financial services company with a history dating
back to 1690. Headquartered in London, Barclays operates globally, offering a broad range
of services such as retail, commercial, and investment banking. The Barclays Global
Service Centre in India provides world-class technology and operations support to
Barclays' global businesses.

Barclays has been at the forefront of innovation in the financial industry, being the first to
introduce credit cards in the UK and consistently evolving to meet the needs of a digital
economy. The company’s emphasis on values such as Respect, Integrity, Service,
Excellence, and Stewardship (RISES) reflects its dedication to building long-term
relationships with customers, employees, and communities alike.

About the Role:

Role: Technology Summer Intern

Description:
The role involves designing and developing scalable applications and microservices,
creating server components, and implementing functional solutions. It includes
collaborating with product owners and end users to gather requirements, conducting unit
tests, and coordinating software releases. The position requires participating in code
reviews , while also providing support for applications' business-as-usual activities.

Overview of the Selection Process:

The selection process for the  Intern role involved two rounds:

1.  Online Coding Round

2.  Interview Round (Technical + HR)

1. Online Coding Round:

The online assessment had three sections:

•  Section 1: 12 MCQs on topics like OOPS concepts, code output prediction, and

error detection.

•  Section 2: 1 medium-level Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA) question related

to strings.

•  Section 3: 1 SQL question requiring a query based on a given table structure.

29 students were shortlisted for the interview round after the online coding round.

2. Interview Round (Technical + HR):

The interview was a single session, combining both technical and HR questions, with two
panel members. They were both chill, making the experience stress-free. I first introduced
myself and then the interview continued with both technical and HR questions.

Technical Questions:

•  Basic DSA question: Swapping two variables without using a temporary variable.
•  SQL: Questions related to different types of joins.
•  Computer Networks: Protocol-related questions.
•  Discussion about my projects, including the tools and technologies I used.

HR Questions:

•  Who is your role model?
•  What do you expect from Barclays?
•  Scenario-based question: How would you meet a project deadline if you were an

employee?

•  Where do you see yourself in 5 years?

Finally, they asked me if I had any questions for them. I asked the panel members:

•  What are the responsibilities of the intern?

•  What should the intern learn before starting the internship?

Among 29 students, 14 students were given the Internship opportunity at Barclays.
