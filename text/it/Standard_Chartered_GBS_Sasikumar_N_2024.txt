Round 1:

For  me  the  round  was  the  technical  round  in  which  they  asked  me  to  design  the
problem statement they had given

PS: Need a design a system for the persons waiting in the lobby for an interview in
which the time is allotted for 30mins. If the interview has not completed within the
given  time  a  notification has  to  be  sent  to  the  interviewer  and  after  30  mins that
person name has to be deleted in the waiting list and to put in the completed list.

Solution: Database Design in which the concepts of triggers and procedures to be
used for notification.

and basic SQL queries and my current project working

Round 2:

No technical Questions were asked. Only general questions

Asked about any Experience in AI & ML and IOT related stuff

Round 3:

Full personal questions and nothing related to tech or general questions

Tips:

Constantly  do  coding  in  any  of  the  online  platforms  like  leetcode,

hackerrank, codecheef.

Start with easy and then gradually progress to medium and hard.

Top  companies  may  go  for  tough  questions  but  mostly  questions  will  be

easy to medium.

Don’t  get  too  stressed  for  missing  companies,  obviously  with  some  little

efforts you can get placed easily.

Include  what  you  know  confidently  in  the  resume.  Mostly  interviewers

won’t prepare questions themselves. Upon seeing your resume they ask questions.
