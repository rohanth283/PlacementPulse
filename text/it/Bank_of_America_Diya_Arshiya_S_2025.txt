**Overview of the interview process:**

1. Video Interview round
2. Technical Interview 1 (at CUIC)
3. Technical + HR Interview 2 (at CUIC)

**Video interview/coding round (90 mins):**

* This online round consisted of 5 questions, 3 Hr Questions and 2 coding questions.
* The coding questions were based on Arrays and strings , Hr questions had to be recorded with your camera and microphone switched on and the questions were “Explain the logic behind your code” , “Introduce yourself” and “Why do u think you are fit for the role at Bofa”.

**Technical Interview 1 (40 mins)**

The interview started with a simple introduction, where I talked about myself, my academic background, and my key projects. After that, they jumped straight into my projects. I was asked to explain each one in detail. They also asked if I had used Object-Oriented Programming (OOPs) in any of them, and when I said yes, they asked me to write a quick code snippet to show how I used it.

For my web development project, they were curious about how I connected to the database and wanted me to walk them through the entire workflow of the project. I explained the tools and technologies I used, how I handled the backend, and the overall functionality.

Next, they asked about my machine learning project. They tested me on some basic machine learning concepts and asked how I implemented the project , like what dataset and algorithms I used.

Then they asked 2 coding questions , the first one was to reverse the words in a sentence and the second question was to find the longest subarray with a sum equal to k .After coding, they asked me to solve the classic "bag of coins" puzzle from GeeksforGeeks. I shared my logical approach and solved it.

They followed this up with a quick SQL query to find the second largest salary in a table. Towards the end, they asked me a few basic questions on C++ and Python, covering things like memory management, data types in C++, Python libraries etc.

**Technical + HR Interview 2 (35 mins):**

This round was a mix of technical and HR questions.

On the technical side, the focus was mainly on SQL and Python.

* For SQL, they asked about all kinds of joins—inner join, left join, right join, self join, equi join, etc. I had to explain them and write examples.
* They also gave me a simple subquery question to solve and asked if I had worked with triggers and procedures. I explained the basics and wrote down the syntax for both.
* Write the code for detecting a loop or cycle in a linked list.

For Python, they asked:

* The difference between lists and tuples.
* Some common dictionary operations and how dictionaries are used.
* To write a simple palindrome-checking program.
* List 10 built-in Python functions and explain their use cases.

On the HR side, they asked questions like:

* How I would handle a conflict with a team member.
* What are my future aspirations and where I see myself in five years.
* To talk about my achievements and a few other personal questions.

One thing to note is that they repeatedly asked Python-related questions, even though I mentioned my core programming language is C++. So, my advice would be to brush up on Python if you’ve included it in your resume, as they might focus on it regardless of your primary language.

**Preparation Tips:**

* Be prepared to answer questions about anything you’ve mentioned in your resume. If you’ve listed a skill or a technology, make sure you know it in depth. Interviewers often test your understanding by asking detailed questions.
* Practice explaining your projects step by step, including their purpose, your role, the technologies used, and any challenges you faced. Be ready to dive into technical details if asked.
* Even if you’re unsure about something, stay calm and try to explain your thought process. If you don’t know the answer to a question, it’s okay to admit it rather than trying to guess or bluff.
* Revise core concepts related to your primary programming language , OOPs, DBMS, OS , CN etc.
