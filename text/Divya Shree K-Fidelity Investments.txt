About Fidelity Investments 
Fidelity Investments was founded in 1946 by Edward C. Johnson II and is 
headquartered in Boston, USA. It began as a small investment firm and has 
grown into one of the world’s largest financial services companies. Fidelity 
offers services like mutual funds, retirement planning, and wealth management. 
The company is known for its focus on technology, customer service, and 
helping investors achieve long-term financial goals. It serves millions of clients 
across the globe.

Round 1: Online Test 
The online assessment was conducted on the HirePro Platform and lasted for 
about 1 hour and 30 minutes. It consisted of 3 sections, each with a time limit. 
1. Section 1 – English Language and Verbal Ability 
Included questions on Sentence Arrangement, Fill in the Blanks, and 
other grammar-based exercises. 
2. Section 2 – Technical Aptitude 
Covered core CS topics such as Data Structures & Algorithms (Sorting, 
Graphs, Heaps), Database Management Systems (SQL queries), Object-
Oriented Programming, Networking, and output-based coding problems. 
It also included basic-level questions on Prompt Engineering and 
Generative AI. The difficulty ranged from easy to medium. 
3. Section 3 – Coding Questions (Easy Level) 
The section had two programming problems focused on arrays and 
strings: 
o Problem 1 – Transform a String by Custom Shifting 
Read a string of lowercase letters and shift even-indexed characters 
two steps forward and odd-indexed characters one step backward 
in the alphabet (with wraparound). 
Example: Input: abcdef → Output: caeceg 
o Problem 2 – Product of Digit Sums with Digital Root 
Read two integers, compute the sum of digits of each, reduce them 
to a single-digit number if necessary, and print the product of those 
sums. 
Example: Input: 58 97 → Output: 28 
4. Outcome 
A total of 75 students were shortlisted for the next round. 
 Round 2:   Technical Interview 
The Technical Interview was conducted at CUIC at 9:00 AM. The session 
began with a brief introduction to the company, highlighting its services, 
values, and areas of expertise. 
The interviewer first asked, “What question do you expect first?” I replied 
“Introduction,” and was then invited to proceed. I gave a brief introduction 
about myself, covering my background and interests. 
The interviewer then reviewed my resume and asked questions related to the 
technologies and skills I had listed. I answered the questions confidently and 
explained my experience. He further asked about real-world applications of 
data structures, including: 
• Where arrays are used in real life: I explained examples such as storing 
student records, managing inventory in e-commerce platforms, and 
handling images in computer graphics. 
• Which data structure is used in a railway ticket booking system: I 
explained that structures like queues and linked lists are commonly used 
to manage bookings, reservations, and waiting lists efficiently. 
• The interviewer asked which programming language I prefer, and I 
replied C++. He mentioned that he was interested in Java, and I 
responded that I am comfortable working with Java as well and can easily 
switch if required. He then asked a few output-based technical questions 
in java (try catch exception) which I was able to answer correctly. 
• The interviewer also asked about compile-time and run-time errors by 
writing a small piece of code and requesting me to identify whether it 
would produce a compile-time or run-time error, and to explain why. I 
provided the correct explanation. 
• Additionally, he asked questions on key programming concepts such as 
abstraction, inheritance, and other object-oriented principles, to which I 
answered confidently. 
• After that, he asked me to explain a project I liked the most. I briefly 
described the project, the technologies I used, how it was implemented, 
the challenges I faced during development, and the steps I took to 
overcome those issues. 
• After discussing the project, the interviewer asked, “Why Fidelity?” 
This question aimed to understand my motivation and interest in joining 
the organization. 
• At the end of the interview, the interviewer asked if I had any questions. I 
inquired about his experience at the company and the types of projects 
interns would work on. The interview session lasted for 45 minutes. 
 
Round 3: HR Interview 
After the technical round, I was called for the HR Interview within 5 
minutes. The session began with the interviewer introducing himself and 
asking about my personal background, including: 
• Family details 
• Hobbies and interests 
• Academic performance, specifically my percentage in 12th standard 
He asked why I chose to apply to Fidelity and inquired about my knowledge 
of mutual funds. I explained what I knew, and he provided further details 
about mutual funds and their workings. 
The interviewer also asked about software development methodologies and 
reviewed my project, asking why I chose that particular project and how I 
managed team collaboration during its development. 
Furthermore, he asked about my strengths and how I would handle a 
situation if a teammate was unable to contribute to the project. I responded 
positively, explaining how I would adapt, support the team, and ensure the 
project’s progress 
 
The HR interview lasted for 30 minutes. Afterward, around 25 to 30 students 
awaited the results. 
Finally, 12 students were selected for the internship, and I was delighted to be 
one of them. 
Key Takeaways: 
• Follow structured preparation resources like the Strivers Sheet. 
• Build a strong foundation in basics of programming and computer 
science. 
• Always have clear knowledge of data structures, how they are used in 
code, and their real-world applications. 
• If you do not know the answer, start with a brute-force approach and 
gradually optimize. 
• Do not hesitate to ask questions or clarify doubts during interviews. 
• Maintain confidence and a positive attitude throughout the process. 
 
Resources and Preparation Tips: 
Focus on one language and practice problem-solving regularly on platforms like 
LeetCode, HackerRank.  
Be ready to explain your projects end-to-end, as technical questions often 
revolve around them. 
Platforms like GeeksforGeeks and LeetCode are excellent for quick revisions 
and brushing up on key concepts. 
Understand the company’s background and prepare meaningful questions to ask 
the interviewer 
Always be truthful about the skills and experiences listed in your resume. Be 
prepared to explain every point in detail, providing examples wherever possible 
to demonstrate your understanding and experience. 
Be well-prepared in OOPs, DBMS, and also cover important topics from 
Operating Systems and Computer Networks.
