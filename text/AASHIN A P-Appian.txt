Technical Round - 1: 1 hr 30 mins
This round was primarily focused on Data Structures & Algorithms, Projects and Resume
based questions.
The interviewer started with a short intro about himself. Then I gave my intro and I had
mentioned my passion for "Astrophysics". The interviewer seemed interested in
Astrophysics too. So he asked questions from that. He asked about the recent trends in the
field of space for which I mentioned about the different countries of the world trying to prove
that America hasn't reached space yet. (Regarding the landing on the moon)
After this, the interviewer went to the DSA part of the interview. The DSA question asked
was:
Given an array of  intervals  where intervals[i] = any integer, merge all overlapping
intervals, and return _an array of the non-overlapping intervals in the form of {start,end} that
cover all the intervals in the input.
Example:
intervals = [3,6,2,5,8,1,9,11]
output = [{1,3},{5,6},{8,9},{11}]
My approach to the problem: I proposed an approach of sorting the array and then checking
whether there exists an overlap and in case of that add it to the existent output variable.
The approach had a time complexity of O(n log n). The interviewer helped me with an
approach of using a stack. However, I pointed out an edge case with a time complexity of
O(n^2) in that and so he proceeded with questions from my resume. It was very much
shocking that Appian had only one DSA question for the technical round.
After the DSA questions, the questions were based on my intern experience at Amazon. I
was asked about the task I did in Amazon and also how I performed in the team and about
my experience at Amazon. After this, the interviewer moved to the tech stack used in
Amazon and how I made use of Artificial Intelligence in my intern days.
Project based Questions:
I was asked which was the project in the resume I had worked on and is considered my
personal favorite. I chose my Railway DBMS project. I explained what inspired me to do it
and the things I tried to do in the project. After which I was asked about the ER structure of
the project and the tech stack used. Also the interviewer suggested few more things I could
add on to the existing project.
Finally he asked for any questions to him and I asked few questions about work-life balance
and career growth at Appian.
