### 3. PHASE-BY-PHASE STUDY PLAN

#### Phase 1: High Priority - Core CS Fundamentals & DSA Mastery (Weeks 1-4)

**Concepts to Master:**
*   **Data Structures:** Arrays (fundamental), Stacks, Queues, Linked Lists, Hash Maps/Sets (Hashing), Trees (Binary Trees, BSTs), Graphs (representation).
*   **Algorithms:**
    *   **Sorting:** Bubble, Selection, Insertion, Merge Sort, Quick Sort (implement from scratch, understand time/space complexity).
    *   **Searching:** Linear Search, Binary Search.
    *   **Basic Algorithmic Paradigms:** Two Pointers, Sliding Window.
*   **DBMS & SQL:**
    *   **Basics:** ACID properties, Normalization (1NF, 2NF, 3NF, BCNF), Keys (Primary, Foreign, Candidate).
    *   **ER Diagrams:** Design principles.
    *   **SQL Queries:** SELECT, FROM, WHERE, GROUP BY, HAVING, ORDER BY, LIMIT/OFFSET.
    *   **Advanced SQL:** Joins (INNER, LEFT, RIGHT, FULL OUTER), Subqueries, Aggregation functions (COUNT, SUM, AVG, MAX, MIN), Date Functions.

**Practice Targets:**
*   **Coding:**
    *   Implement various sorting algorithms.
    *   Solve array manipulation problems (e.g., finding pairs, rotating arrays).
    *   Practice problems involving intervals (e.g., merging, inserting).
    *   Solve problems requiring efficient logic and data structure choice.
*   **SQL:**
    *   Write queries to select highest/lowest values per group.
    *   Practice queries involving multiple joins and aggregations.
    *   Solve problems like "select the highest CGPA from each department" or "find the top-most manager of a given employee."
*   **Conceptual:**
    *   Explain DBMS concepts clearly.
    *   Draw ER diagrams for given scenarios.

**Standard LeetCode/GeeksforGeeks Equivalents:**
*   **Arrays/Sorting:** Two Sum (all variants), Merge Intervals, Insert Interval, Sort an Array, Kth Largest Element in an Array.
*   **SQL:** "Employees Earning More Than Their Managers", "Department Highest Salary", "Nth Highest Salary", "Find the top-most manager of a given employee" (Hierarchical Queries).
*   **Hashing/Two Pointers:** Two Sum, Longest Substring Without Repeating Characters.

#### Phase 2: Medium Priority - Advanced DSA, OS, OOP & Networks (Weeks 5-7)

**Concepts to Master:**
*   **Advanced Data Structures:** Heaps, Tries (if relevant to projects/interests).
*   **Graph Algorithms:** BFS (Breadth-First Search), DFS (Depth-First Search), Graph Traversal (on grids, general graphs).
*   **Operating Systems (OS):**
    *   **Core Concepts:** Process vs. Thread vs. Task, Memory Management (Paging, Segmentation), CPU Scheduling.
    *   **Concurrency & Synchronization:** Mutexes, Semaphores, Critical Section, Deadlock, Producer-Consumer Problem (explain with real-life examples).
*   **Object-Oriented Programming (OOP):**
    *   **Principles:** Encapsulation, Inheritance, Polymorphism (explain with real-world examples), Abstraction.
    *   **Design Patterns:** Basic understanding (e.g., Singleton, Factory) if time permits.
*   **Networks & APIs:**
    *   **Basics:** OSI Model (briefly), TCP/IP.
    *   **APIs:** What is an API, types of APIs (REST, SOAP), purposes, API fire/hit rate.
*   **Compilers & Interpreters:** Internal working, key differences.

**Practice Targets:**
*   **Coding:**
    *   Solve graph traversal problems (e.g., "Mall Treasure Hunt" - BFS/DFS on a grid).
    *   Problems involving permutations.
    *   Problems requiring optimization for large constraints (e.g., "Bank Transfers Simulation").
*   **Conceptual:**
    *   Explain OS concepts like mutexes and critical sections with practical scenarios.
    *   Provide real-world examples for OOP principles, especially polymorphism.
    *   Discuss different types of APIs and their use cases.
    *   Compare and contrast compilers and interpreters.

**Standard LeetCode/GeeksforGeeks Equivalents:**
*   **Graphs:** Number of Islands, Rotting Oranges, Word Ladder, Permutations.
*   **OS/OOP/Networks:** Focus on conceptual questions from GeeksforGeeks, InterviewBit, or specific OS/OOP textbooks.

#### Phase 3: High Priority - Project, Resume & Behavioral (Ongoing & Final Week)

**Concepts to Master:**
*   **Resume-Based:** Be prepared to discuss *every single line* on your resume. Quantify achievements.
*   **Project-Based:**

**Practice Targets:**

### 5. CANDIDATE TIPS & STRATEGIES

1.  **Master Your Resume & Projects:** This is your primary talking point. Be ready to discuss *every detail* of your projects, internships, and experiences. Explain *why* you made certain technical choices, *how* you implemented them, and *what impact* they had. Quantify results whenever possible.
2.  **Solidify Core CS Fundamentals:** DBMS, OS, OOP, and Networks are not just theoretical; be prepared to explain them with real-world examples and discuss their practical implications.
3.  **Practice Coding Consistently:** Focus on Data Structures (Arrays, Hash Maps, Trees, Graphs) and Algorithms (Sorting, Searching, BFS/DFS, Two Pointers). Don't just solve; understand the underlying logic and complexity.
4.  **SQL is Crucial:** Practice writing complex SQL queries involving joins, aggregation, subqueries, and hierarchical data.
5.  **Think Aloud:** For coding and problem-solving questions, articulate your thought process. Explain your approach, discuss trade-offs, and walk through your code line by line. This demonstrates problem-solving skills even if you don't reach the optimal solution immediately.
6.  **Optimize for Efficiency:** Pay attention to constraints. If a problem has large inputs (e.g., N up to 100,000), immediately think about time and space complexity and how to optimize.
7.  **Research the Company:** Understand their products, culture, and recent news. This helps you answer "Why us?" questions genuinely and shows your enthusiasm.
8.  **Prepare for Behavioral Questions:** Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to structure your answers for experience-based questions. Have a clear "elevator pitch" for your self-introduction.
9.  **Ask Clarifying Questions:** Don't hesitate to ask questions about problem constraints, edge cases, or specific requirements before diving into coding.
10. **Mock Interviews:** Practice with peers or mentors. This helps you refine your communication, identify weak areas, and manage interview pressure.
